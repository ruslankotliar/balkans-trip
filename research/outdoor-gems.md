### 1. Via Ferrata Fortica — Omiš, Croatia

**NOT in data** (existing `hr-starigrad-fortica` covers the fortress as a viewpoint, not the via ferrata route)

- **Coordinates:** Parking: `43.4472, 16.6947` | Via ferrata start: `43.4462, 16.6939`
- **Country:** HR
- **Category:** activity / via ferrata
- **Difficulty:** Grade B (UIAA scale) — beginner-accessible, no vertical or overhanging sections
- **Distance:** 1.2 km on the ferrata; 270 m vertical gain
- **Time needed:** 1h 25min total (10 min approach, 45 min ferrata, 30 min descent)
- **Self-guided:** YES. Route markings refreshed 2023; cables throughout. Gear rental available at the bottom (harness + helmet, meet staff before start, no guide required). Some sources note the operator "may not be happy" but many do it unguided. Gear rental is the preferred frugal approach.
- **What makes it special:** Limestone ridge directly above the Cetina gorge mouth and the old town of Omiš. Views of the river canyon below, the delta, and the open Adriatic. Combines well with a swim in the Cetina afterward. The fortress ruins at the top are worth 20 extra minutes.
- **June notes:** Gets very hot — start before 8am or after 5pm. Bring 1.5L+ water.
- **Cost:** Guided tour ~€50 pp; unguided gear rental cheaper (details at site). Entry to fortress ruins: small additional fee.
- **Sources:**
  - https://www.ferratago.com/fortica-omis/
  - https://www.theviaferrataguidesite.com/via-ferrata-omis-one-of-the-best-via-ferratas-in-croatia/

### 2. Neretva Canyon Kayak Safari — Jablanica, Bosnia

**NOT in data** (`ba-jablanica-neretva-museum` is there but only covers the war museum)

- **Coordinates:** Operator/launch point: `43.6609, 17.7655` (300 m from the Sarajevo–Mostar main road, across the bridge near Restaurant Kovacevic, Jablanica)
- **Country:** BA
- **Category:** activity / self-guided kayak
- **Difficulty:** Easy — calm emerald water, no rapids. Beginner-friendly, children welcome.
- **Time needed:** 3h round trip (1.5h down to the beach, time at the turquoise river beach, return tow option available)
- **Self-guided:** YES — this is the key point. No guide. Staff brief you, give you the kayak (tandem available), life jacket, and waterproof bag. You paddle at your own pace. Closing time ~19:00.
- **What makes it special:** You paddle through the Neretva canyon with sheer peaks (Prenj, Čvrsnica) towering above. The water is cold, crystal-clear, emerald. There is a rock face where Red Bull cliff divers have jumped — you can do a lower version. End point is a secluded river beach with no road access. ~40 min from Mostar by car; fits perfectly as a stop on the Mostar → Konjic → Sarajevo drive.
- **Cost:** ~$25 pp (one of the best-value adventure activities in the region). 5-star rated across 60+ reviews.
- **Sources:**
  - https://guidetoeurope.com/bosnia-and-herzegovina/best-tours-and-tickets/details/jablanica-kayak-rental-in-neretva-canyon
  - https://www.tripadvisor.com/Attraction_Review-g1632097-d16787373-Reviews-Neretva_Kayak_Canoe_Safari-Jablanica_Herzegovina_Neretva_Canton_Federation_of_B.html

### 3. Via Ferrata Piva — Piva Canyon, Montenegro

**NOT in data** (Piva canyon road/entry is in `me-scepan-polje-piva-canyon` but not this route)

- **Coordinates:** Launch point is ~20 min south of Plužine toward Šćepan Polje; parking lot (3 cars) near Piva River bridge. Exact GPS not published by operator — navigate to Nedajno village on the Žabljak-Plužine road, then follow signs.
- **Country:** ME
- **Category:** activity / via ferrata
- **Difficulty:** Grade B/C — moderate. Longest vertical section 80 m. One slight overhang. Begins from river level with a traverse 2 m above the turquoise Piva river.
- **Time needed:** 2–3h including approach and descent
- **Self-guided:** NO — guide required. Solo: €150; couples: €70 pp; groups 3–8: €65 pp. Includes equipment + professional photos.
- **What makes it special:** You traverse the sheer canyon walls at river level, literally hovering above turquoise water. Then 80 m vertical climb up the cliff face. Views down the canyon are exceptional. Built to 2024 European EN 16869 safety standard — modern installation. Combinable with Tara rafting (Žabljak–Piva is the same day anyway).
- **Access from Žabljak:** On the route Žabljak → Šćepan Polje (your confirmed route), so zero detour.
- **Note:** This is a legitimate new via ferrata that opened recently. Different from the Kotor via ferrata already in the data.
- **Sources:**
  - https://viaferratapiva.me/en/blog/via-ferrata-piva-guide/
  - https://explore-serbia.rs/en/via-ferrata-piva/

### 4. Bay of Kotor Self-Guided Kayak (Go Go Gaga Rentals) — Kotor

**NOT in data** (`hr-dubrovnik-sea-kayak` covers Dubrovnik; `me-virpazar-kayak` covers Skadar; but Bay of Kotor self-guided kayak is absent)

- **Coordinates:** Rental base near Old Town Kotor: approximately `42.4246, 18.7712` (near the old town waterfront)
- **Country:** ME
- **Category:** activity / self-guided sea kayak
- **Difficulty:** Easy — bay is sheltered, minimal current, calm water
- **Time needed:** Half-day to full day; or 2h for a quick Our Lady of the Rocks loop
- **Self-guided:** YES — rent by hour or day. Single kayak: €5/h, €20/day. Double kayak: €10 first hour, €5/h after, €25/day. You design your own route.
- **What makes it special:** Paddle from Kotor to Perast (8 km one-way), stopping at Our Lady of the Rocks island (€3 entry), poking into coves along the southern bank. The bay is a fjord lookalike — sheer limestone cliffs rising from calm water. Access to small beaches and coves impossible by road. At dawn the bay is mirror-flat and deserted. Can also reach the Blue Cave and submarine tunnels under your own steam if timing allows.
- **Best route suggestion:** Kotor → village of Ljuta → Perast (Our Lady of the Rocks) → return via northern shore. ~16 km round trip, 4h easy paddling with stops.
- **Sources:**
  - https://www.gogogagatours.com/en/rentals/
  - https://www.tripadvisor.com/Attraction_Review-g2558165-d19686857-Reviews-Kayak_Tours_Kotor-Kotor_Municipality.html

### 5. Hrčavka Canyon Canyoning — Sutjeska NP, Bosnia

**NOT in data** (Sutjeska general entry `ba-sutjeska` exists; Zelengora lakes exist; but Hrčavka canyoning is not there)

- **Coordinates:** Starting point: ~20 min by car from Tjentište visitor center; exact trailhead GPS not published (operator pick-up from Tjentište camp). Approximate area: `43.3800, 18.6900`
- **Country:** BA
- **Category:** activity / canyoning
- **Difficulty:** Beginner — no technical climbing experience needed. Water is 10°C even in summer (wetsuits provided).
- **Time needed:** 2–3h in the canyon
- **Self-guided:** NO — guided only (multiple operators: Outdoor Tara, Adventure Bosnia, Camp Sutjeska). Operators are all based in Tjentište so easy to book on arrival.
- **What makes it special:** The Hrčavka river cuts through a narrow slot canyon feeding into Sutjeska NP. The route involves wading, swimming through turquoise pools, natural water slides, jumps up to 7 m (all optional). The canyon walls are "physically too narrow for more than 8 people" — inherently intimate. Late May/June = highest waterfalls from snowmelt. This is the most adventurous thing you can do at Sutjeska beyond the Maglić hike.
- **June timing:** The waterfalls peak in June from snowmelt — best time of year.
- **Cost:** ~€40–50 pp typical
- **Sources:**
  - https://www.outdoortara.com/blog/canyoning-hrčavka-ultimate-guide
  - https://www.outdoortara.com/activities/canyoning-hrcavka
  - https://visitsutjeskanationalpark.com/hrcavka-canyoning-guide/

### 6. Viganj Windsurfing / Kite Beach — Pelješac

**NOT in data** (Pelješac has `hr-sveti-ilija-peljesac`, `hr-matusko-winery`, `hr-nakovana` etc., but Viganj as an activity hub is missing)

- **Coordinates:** `42.9598, 17.0820` (Viganj beach, between Orebić and Kučište)
- **Country:** HR
- **Category:** activity / beach
- **Difficulty:** N/A — spectating or taking a lesson
- **Time needed:** Half-day to full day
- **What makes it special:** Viganj is one of the top windsurfing/kitesurfing spots in the Adriatic, driven by the reliable Maestral (NW wind, 12–25 knots, 2pm–sunset, June–August). For non-surfers: the vibe is distinctly laid-back, board-sports beach-bar culture — very different from crowded Makarska. The strait between Pelješac and Korčula island is scenic from both directions. LIBERAN Surf & Camp has 30 years of tradition; several competing schools offer rentals and lessons. Camping options directly on the beach (Antony Boy campsite). Even if you don't kite: the beach is narrow and pebbly with a surfer atmosphere, small bars, clear water, and the view of Korčula is iconic.
- **For the group:** Rent boards for an afternoon, watch carnage and skill simultaneously. Lesson packages available. Those not surfing can SUP or swim.
- **Sources:**
  - https://www.welovetokite.com/destinations/peljesac/
  - https://liberansurf.eu/
  - https://antony-boy.com/activities/

### 7. Lovište — End-of-Peninsula Village, Pelješac

**NOT in data** (mentioned obliquely in notes but no map pin)

- **Coordinates:** `42.9986, 17.1785` (village center)
- **Country:** HR
- **Category:** beach / village
- **Difficulty:** Easy — car access
- **Time needed:** 2h stopover or overnight base
- **What makes it special:** Lovište sits at the very tip of Pelješac, facing the open sea and Hvar island. Population ~250, no tourist infrastructure. The surrounding micro-bays (Bila Punta: 20-min walk north from village, pebble beach, clear water for snorkeling; Trstenica: 2.5 km away, isolated) are essentially private in the morning. The northern coast road from Lovište to Duba Pelješka (13 km) is "carved into the mountains on the deserted northern coast" — a spectacular drive/walk. This is the "escape from Dubrovnik crowds" option on Pelješac that travel guides don't feature.
- **When to use it:** If staying on Pelješac for the island/winery block, making it to Lovište for sunset earns you the best non-island swim of the Dalmatia segment.
- **Sources:**
  - https://kathi-daniela.com/en/destinations/croatia/croatia-insider-tip-peljesac/
  - https://epepa.eu/zuljana-croatia-a-hidden-gem-with-beautiful-beaches/

## ACTIVITY NOTES — Supplementing Existing Data

### Biokovo / Sveti Jure — Sunrise Logistics (updates `hr-sveti-jure`)
- Park opens 06:00 in June — you can enter right at opening for sunrise (05:15 in June — the sun actually rises before the park opens)
- **Trick:** The road is free at NIGHT. Park your car on the coast, drive up around 04:30, watch sunrise from the summit, descend by 07:00 before fee booths are fully staffed. Multiple blogs confirm this works.
- Crowds arrive from 09:00 onward. Before 07:00 you will likely have the summit to yourself.
- Parking at summit: ~30–40 cars. Not an issue pre-07:00.

### Mrtvica Canyon — Status Update
- Important: The historic wooden bridges were destroyed in a 2020 flood. As of June 2025 they were still NOT rebuilt. This means the "Gate of Wishes" section (8.5 km, 3h) is still intact and doable, but the deeper "Danilov Put" section (15 km, 6h) may have accessibility issues at the old bridge crossings. Check with local sources before committing to the full route.
- The canyon itself is not affected — walls, turquoise water, rock passageways all intact.

### Ćurevac Viewpoint — Quick-Win Add (updates `me-durmitor-curevac`)
- Parking confirmed: 43.193896, 19.089645
- Park entry fee: €3–4 (paid at entry gate before parking area)
- Only 1–1.5 km walk one way; 30–45 min. Suitable for a 2h detour.
- Not crowded on weekdays but popular on weekends — go early (before 09:00) or late (after 18:00) for solitude.

### Trnovačko Jezero — Camping Confirmed (updates `me-trnovacko-jezero`)
- Wild camping at the lake shore is confirmed and practiced by many travelers
- Small park fee ~€1.50–2.50 for tenting (+ €2.50 park entry)
- Trailhead GPS: 43.287754, 18.716250 (Prijevor saddle, end of rough road)
- Access road drivable with standard rental car (gravel/rough, take it slow)
- June: solitary hiker reported "didn't see a single person on the whole hike" on a weekday

### Piva Canyon Road Wild Camping
- Kruška Lookout (main viewpoint): 5 km from Plužine center on the road to Žabljak
- Pull-offs along the canyon road are tolerated for overnight camping (van/tent)
- Plužine camping coordinates: 43.15687, 18.84106 (confirmed park4night listing)
- Best light: morning (canyon faces roughly east in its upper section)

### Komovi — Practical Access Notes (updates `me-komovi-vasojevicki-kom`)
- Trailhead: Štavna plateau, 1,700 m elevation, reached from Andrijevica
- Andrijevica is ~2h from Kotor, ~1.5h from Podgorica, ~2.5h from Žabljak
- NOT a practical day trip from Žabljak (4h round drive) — better as a detour en route Podgorica → Kotor
- Hike: 4.7 mi / 7.5 km out-and-back, ~4h. Moderate-challenging, some scrambling.
- Season opens fully in late June — good for Jun 24–28 window.

## COMPLETELY UNEXPECTED FINDS — What Overlanders Rave About

### The Neretva Canyon at Jablanica: The Anti-Rafting Option
Standard travel advice pushes rafting at Konjic or Tara. What gets overlooked: the **self-guided kayak at Jablanica** (see entry #2 above) is cheaper, calmer, more beautiful per hour, and takes only half a day. The emerald canyon water here is among the best river swimming in the Balkans. Multiple overlander blogs describe discovering it accidentally while stopping for fuel.

### Viganj vs. Dubrovnik: The Pelješac Attitude Swap
Active travelers consistently report that the energy at Viganj is the antidote to Dubrovnik's cruise-ship congestion. A 3-hour stop on the way from Dubrovnik to Ston — watching kiters, grabbing a drink at a beach bar, swimming in the strait — resets the trip rhythm better than another walled city.

### Piva Lake Kayak at Dawn
Multiple trip reports describe the Piva Lake (above the Piva dam near Plužine) as one of the most surreal kayaking experiences in the Balkans — the flooded valley means the lake has "ghost arms" reaching into former valley tributaries, with submerged roads and occasional drowned village foundations visible in the clear water. Kayak rental: available from Beach Bar Hook near Plužine. Self-guided. Best at first light when the lake is mirror-flat and the canyon walls turn pink.

### The Road from Lovište North: Montenegro Feeling in Croatia
The 13-km track along the deserted northern coast of Pelješac from Lovište to Duba Pelješka is not a tourist route. It runs along sheer cliffs above the sea, with no guardrails and no traffic. Several overlander accounts describe wild camping on the cliffs overlooking the channel to Hvar. No facilities, no crowds.

## DECISION NOTES FOR ROUTE PLANNING

**Highest-impact new adds for your specific route:**

1. **Jablanica Kayak (BA)** — Zero difficulty, highest community rating (5 stars), self-guided, €25, fits perfectly between Mostar and Sarajevo/Konjic. The emerald canyon kayak that overlanders consistently call a highlight. **Strong recommend add.**

2. **Via Ferrata Fortica Omiš (HR)** — Already passing through Omiš on Day 2. Add 2h to the Omiš stop. Self-guided. Combines directly with the cetina cliff jump and canyoning already in data. Morning start beats the heat.

3. **Hrčavka Canyon Canyoning (BA)** — If spending a night at Sutjeska, this is the most memorable thing to do there beyond the hike. Book same day from Tjentište camp. June = prime season for waterfalls.

4. **Via Ferrata Piva (ME)** — On your confirmed Žabljak → Šćepan Polje → Dubrovnik route. Not a detour. If the group is energized after Durmitor and wants one more adrenaline hit before the coast, this is it.

5. **Kotor Bay Kayak (ME)** — Morning of the Kotor day (you'll be there for the fortress anyway). Rent kayaks at 07:00, paddle to Perast, back by 10:30 before the crowds build. Transforms Kotor from "walk around the walls" into something the group will actually talk about.

**Lower priority:**
- Viganj is a drive-by bonus on the Pelješac day — worth a 2h stop.
- Lovište is only worth it if staying on Pelješac overnight (the driving time to the peninsula tip makes it a commitment otherwise).
