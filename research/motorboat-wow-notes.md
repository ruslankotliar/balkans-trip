## 1. Motorized Boat Rental — Skadar Lake (Virpazar)

### Verdict: Guided is the default; true self-drive is hard but exists

**The honest picture:** Every major operator at Virpazar (Kingfisher, Falcon, Milica, Skadar Lake Trips, Boat Viktor) runs *guided* tours with a captained vessel. The captain is mandatory and included. You are not driving — you're being taken around. Prices:
- 1h guided private: ~€45
- 2h guided private: ~€90
- 3h (Kom Monastery): ~€150
- Full day (8h): ~€270

**Self-drive motorboat — YES, it exists, but you must go through booking platforms:**
- **Click&Boat** lists a "Wooden Boat" self-drive motorboat in Virpazar (listing ID `wooden-boat-boat-qp817gk`). Site was 403-blocked but the listing exists. Price implied: ~€107-260/day range cited by Nautal/Click&Boat aggregate.
- **SamBoat** lists "Bat 996 Open EFB" in Virpazar (3 listings). HOWEVER — on inspection, these all come with a mandatory captain ("Dragan," speaks English/Italian). Not true bareboat.
- **Nautal.com** says "with or without skipper" for Skadar — the no-skipper listings are the Click&Boat wooden boats.

**Practical recommendation for the group:** Don't try to self-drive a motorboat on Skadar — the logistics (finding the listing, vetting the boat, navigating a national park in an unfamiliar vessel) aren't worth it when a guided private 2h tour is €90 for the whole boat (4 people = €22.50pp). For true "we go where we want" freedom, use **kayaks or SUP** (fully self-guided, €5/h single, €20/day).

**If you really want a motorized independent experience:** Contact [Click&Boat's Virpazar listing](https://www.clickandboat.com/us/boat-rental/virpazar/motorboat/wooden-boat-boat-qp817gk) directly — this is the only confirmed self-drive motorboat found.

### What can you access by motorboat that you can't on foot?

1. **Karuč village** (boat-only or hairy single-track road) — stone fishing hamlet, Karučko Oko blue spring, ~20min from Rijeka Crnojevića. Private boat tour ~€250 for 4h/6 people from Balkan Adventure.
2. **Kom Monastery** — medieval monastery 1415-1427, only reachable by boat (no road access). All guided tours include this.
3. **Grmožur Island** ("Montenegrin Alcatraz") — 19th-century island prison, ruined fortifications. Accessible only by boat.
4. **Pančeva Oka** (Pelican's Pool) — the Dalmatian Pelican breeding colony, in the reedbeds of the north shore. Only a narrow water-trail through lily fields. *Undiscovered Montenegro* runs specific guided kayak trips to this spot (see below).
5. **Dodosi village + Karatuna river channel** — beautiful flooded river channel with transparent water, old fishing village, summer kayak + swimming spot. Accessible from Rijeka Crnojevića by water.

## 2. Motorized Boat — Kotor Bay (Day 12, Jun 27)

### Self-drive without license: YES — kayak is the practical option

**Motorboat self-drive:** Requires a valid ICC or equivalent skipper's license. No license = no self-drive motorboat in Kotor Bay. The one source quoting "no license needed" on TripAdvisor likely referred to a small electric/pedal boat.

**BBC Rent a Boat (Tivat):** Fleet of 4.8m–18m vessels. "Sail independently with our fleets, with mandatory training and support" — suggests self-drive IS available with their smallest boats after a briefing. Contact: +382 67 18 10 08 / office@bbcrentaboat.com. No public pricing — quote only. Likely €80-120/h for the 4.8m model.

**Practical option for Day 12 without a license:**
- **Kayak from Go Go Gaga** — already documented as `me-kotor-bay-kayak`. €25/day double, self-guided. Paddle to Perast and Our Lady of the Rocks. 1.5-2h to Perast. Operator: +382 69 472 288.
- **Kayak from Kotor Bay Tours** — €10/h single, €30/day. +382 69 152 015.
- **For Our Lady of the Rocks specifically:** the fastest option is still the local boatman from Perast (€5pp, already documented in `kotor-day12.md`).

**Motorboat tours (with captain) in Kotor Bay:**
- Go Go Gaga speedboat: €80/h (up to 7 pax) or €60/h for 3h+, includes drinks and skipper.
- Speedboat Montenegro: €80-200/h depending on boat size, all with skipper.
- These are the right choice if the group wants to see Blue Cave (Luštica), Mamula island, or other spots beyond Our Lady of the Rocks.

### Summary for Day 12

The group doesn't need to rent anything extra for Kotor Bay — the boatman to Our Lady of the Rocks is €5pp and is the right product. If they want a broader Kotor Bay speedboat adventure on Day 12, Go Go Gaga (€80/h) can take them to the Blue Cave + Our Lady of the Rocks as a half-day. But Day 12 is already packed (fortress + old town + Perast + border drive) — don't add more.

## 3. Boat Tours/Rentals — Hutovo Blato & Neretva (Bosnia, Day 6 area)

### Hutovo Blato Nature Park

**Self-drive: NOT available.** All boat experiences are guided.
- Park boats (at Karaotok visitor centre): 4 large boats (25 pax), 3 small boats (4 pax), canoes. All guided.
- **Standard price:** €10/person for 45-min guided boat ride; €15/person for 90-min.
- **Guided canoe/birdwatching safari (Adventure.ba):** Starts from €30pp, 3-4h, includes guide + NP entry. No self-drive canoe.
- **June wildlife reality:** Not peak bird season (spring/autumn migrations are the show). Expect herons, kingfishers, maybe pygmy cormorants. No guaranteed pelicans. Reviewers note "saw hardly any animals apart from a couple of small water snakes" on some visits.
- **Address:** Karaotok visitor centre, Čapljina municipality. ~30km from Mostar.
- This is already documented as `ba-hutovo-blato`. The boat price (~€10pp) is confirmed.

Already documented as `ba-jablanica-kayak-neretva`. Price confirmed: ~€25pp (20 BAM). Operator: "Haris" — contact via the Jablanica kayak operators' WhatsApp listed on Viator. Duration: 90 min one-way, self-guided, no experience needed. This is already **shortlisted** — no change needed.

## 4. Perućica Primeval Forest — Booking Reality (Sutjeska NP)

### The key fact: ADVANCE BOOKING REQUIRED, guide mandatory

- **Self-guided entry is NOT permitted.** Management requires a licensed NP guide; rangers enforce this at the entry gate.
- **Daily capacity: 16 people maximum.** Book early in June (high season).
- **How to book:** Via the Sutjeska National Park website: [sutjeskanp.com/primeval-forest-perucica-tour/](https://sutjeskanp.com/primeval-forest-perucica-tour/) — OR through local guide operators: Camp Nomad (visitsutjeskanationalpark.com), Tara Sport (tarasportrafting.com), Outdoor Tara (outdoortara.com).
- **Price:** Park entry ~5 KM (~€2.50) + guide fee (flat rate per group, ~100-150 KM / ~€50-75 for a group). Practical cost for 4: ~€60-80 total for the group.
- **What you get:** 3.5-4h hike into one of the last primeval forests in Europe. Ancient beeches and spruces, no cleared trails, some climbing and route-finding. Skakavac Waterfall (98m high, one of the Balkans' tallest) at the end. Genuinely extraordinary — not a curated park walk.
- **Walk-in policy:** Walk-ins are NOT accepted as a formal policy. However, Camp Nomad and local guides sometimes have spots on the day. Best approach: email/WhatsApp Camp Nomad (visitsutjeskanationalpark.com) 3-5 days before arrival to check.

### Booking strategy for the group (Day 8-9 area, ~Jun 23)

1. Book through Camp Nomad (~visitsutjeskanationalpark.com) — they organize this regularly and can be flexible.
2. As backup, email the NP directly via sutjeskanp.com at least 5 days ahead.
3. If Perućica is full: the **Maglić peak hike** (2386m, BiH's highest) and the **Skakavac waterfall trail** approach from outside Perućica are both possible without a guide.

This enhances the existing `ba-skakavac-waterfall` entry in `experiences.json`. The current entry doesn't mention the mandatory-guide/advance-booking requirement.

## 6. Skadar Lake — Pelican Colony Kayak (Undiscovered Montenegro)

### The most memorable thing to do on Skadar Lake

**Undiscovered Montenegro** (undiscoveredmontenegro.com) runs a specific guided kayak tour to the Dalmatian Pelican breeding colony — "Pančeva Oka" — in the north shore reedbeds. This is described as their "favourite adventure of all."

- The route is a narrow water-trail through floating fields of water lily, threading into the rushes where the pelicans nest.
- Coordinate of the pelican colony (Pančeva Oka): north end of Lake Skadar, in extensive reedbeds along the north shore. Not precisely pinnable without local knowledge — this is why a guide is important.
- **Can you get there by kayak independently?** Possibly, but the water-trail is narrow and requires local knowledge to find. The guided kayak tour is the reliable option.
- **Undiscovered Montenegro contact:** [undiscoveredmontenegro.com](https://undiscoveredmontenegro.com) — they also do multi-day kayak + wild camp tours (7 days, longer format).
- **Alternative operator for pelican kayak:** [skadarlakewildlife.com](https://skadarlakewildlife.com) — Flora & Fauna Boats (site was down but listed as a specialist wildlife tour operator).

**What the standard Kingfisher/Falcon tours show:** These usually stay near Virpazar — Grmožur island, Lesendro fortress, lily channels. They may pass bird habitat but the dedicated pelican colony kayak requires a specialist tour that goes further north.

**Recommendation:** If the group wants the pelican colony experience (it is genuinely extraordinary — the endangered Dalmatian Pelican, nesting, you kayak through the reedbeds), book Undiscovered Montenegro's tour separately. The standard Virpazar kayak rental from Kingfisher gives you independence to paddle the lily channels but won't reliably get you to the colony.

### Kayak from Rijeka Crnojevića (separate access point)

A second base for Skadar Lake kayaking:
- **Enjoy Skadar Lake** (enjoyskadarlake.com) — kayak rental from Rijeka Crnojevića. Single: €7/h or €25/day. Double: €10/h or €35/day. Includes life jacket + waterproof bag + route suggestions. Self-guided.
- Important safety note: "Not recommended to kayak out into the open lake after 12:00 (noon) because of the possibility of strong winds."
- The water channel near Ploče leads directly to Karuč village — reachable by kayak from this base.
- Rijeka Crnojevića is also the base for the **Stari Most restaurant** (Lonely Planet's pick for best restaurant in Montenegro; see below).

## 7. Stari Most Restaurant, Rijeka Crnojevića — Hidden Gem Dinner

This is an unexpected find and genuinely worth the minor detour from Virpazar.

- **Name:** Stari Most (not the Mostar bridge — different restaurant, same name)
- **Location:** Rijeka Crnojevića village, on the old stone bridge over the Crnojevića river, ~30-35 min from Virpazar (40km via Podgorica or via the mountain road)
- **What:** Riverside konoba with outstanding fish. Menu: eel, trout, carp from the lake. Fish soup is the standout. Extremely affordable: full 3-course meal with a drink ~€13.50/person.
- **The vibe:** "Magical place hidden in the heart of mountains," outdoor tables on the bridge/riverbank, mountain views, total quiet. Lonely Planet lists it among best restaurants in Montenegro.
- **Honest note:** One reviewer found the trout overcooked; others rate it highly. Cash only. Not easy to find (GPS the village).
- **Worth the detour?** Only if you're already routing through Rijeka Crnojevića (e.g., visiting Karuč, or coming from Cetinje/Lovćen direction). The mountain road approach to Rijeka Crnojevića from the Podgorica-Cetinje highway is beautiful but narrow and slow.
- **Contact/booking:** No reservation needed (small, informal). Show up.

## 9. Vjetrenica Cave — Updated Ticket Prices

Existing entry `ba-vjetrenica-cave` lists "~10 KM / a few euro pp." The actual 2026 price from the official website:
- **Adults (foreign visitors): 25 KM (~€12.75)**
- Students/pensioners: 20 KM
- Children: 15 KM
- Museum visit (separate, optional): +2 KM

**This is higher than the existing entry.** For 4 adults: 100 KM (~€51). Cash KM required.

**Opening times (summer):** Multiple tour slots starting at 09:00, 10:00, 11:00, 12:00, 13:00, 14:00. Guided tour: 800m of cave. ~1h per tour.

**Worth it?** Honest verdict: Yes, for the group. It's on the Dubrovnik-Mostar corridor (near Trebinje/Ravno, 25 KM = 12.5 EUR per person is reasonable). The blind olm salamander (Proteus anguinus) lives in the underground lakes here — a genuinely rare animal. Not a mainstream cave with Instagram stalactites, but it's weirder and more interesting than most.

### NEW finds from this session:

#### A. Durmitor Katun Cabin Stays (Žabljak area, Day 8-9)

The Bold Passport confirms katun (traditional shepherd cabin) stays are the right product for Durmitor. Three candidates:

1. **Domaćinstvo Krstajić** — 5 min outside Žabljak. ~$110/night. Two-floor cabin, authentic chalet feel, homemade rakija from hosts. ★9.5/250+ reviews on Booking.com. The right Tier-2/Tier-3 option for Durmitor. Not a villa-tier WOW but highly rated and very local.

2. **Cottage Bumblebee** — 15 min outside Žabljak. ~$56/night. All-wood rustic shepherd's cabin feel, fully equipped kitchen. ★9.8/190+ reviews. Budget-friendly. Sleeps unclear — check capacity.

3. **Homeland Nest** — inside Durmitor NP. ~$199/night (summer). Private pool, floor-to-ceiling curtains, loft bed, mountain views. ★10.0/40+ reviews. This is the villa-tier WOW option for Durmitor if budget allows.

4. **Shiny Flake (Bosaca)** — highest permanent settlement in the region (1600m), inside Durmitor NP, near hiking trails. Rating noted as 4.9. Cheapest option; authentic altitude experience. Worth researching further.

**Recommendation:** For the group's Durmitor night(s), **Domaćinstvo Krstajić** (mid-range, authentically Montenegrin, highly reviewed) or **Homeland Nest** (if they want the WOW pool/view option). Book early — "katun cabins sometimes sell out weeks in advance for summer."

#### B. San Duyevo Estate & Winery (Rijeka Crnojevića, Skadar Lake area)

- Location: 3 km from Rijeka Crnojevića, inside Skadar Lake NP. Near Karuč and the Stari Most restaurant.
- Type: Winery guesthouse apartment with vineyard walk-through access.
- Price: ~€35/night (apartment with terrace). Very cheap.
- Hosts: Slavica (cook). Home-cooked meals, wine tasting included.
- Rating: 8.1 Booking.com — positive but mixed. "Old and unkept house in the middle of the hills" in some reviews vs "hospitable host, delicious meals."
- **Honest verdict:** NOT a villa-tier WOW despite the vineyard framing. The Jablan Winery (`me-villa-jablan-winery-rvasi` — already ★4.86/219 reviews) is the better Skadar winery stay. San Duyevo is a Tier-2 budget option for those specifically wanting to be near Rijeka Crnojevića. At €35/night, it's a steal, but don't expect WOW aesthetics.
- **Airbnb:** [airbnb.com/rooms/14401858](https://www.airbnb.com/rooms/14401858)

#### C. Pelješac Vineyard Stay — Dingač (HR)

- **"Holiday Home in Wine Yard Paradise Dingač"** — Potomje village, Pelješac Peninsula.
- In the heart of Dingač wine region (Croatia's finest Plavac Mali grapes, steep south-facing slopes above the Adriatic).
- **What:** House with terrace and sea views, surrounded by vineyards. Kitchen, dishwasher, oven. Garden with beachfront access noted in the description.
- **Where in the trip:** Day 4-5 area (Dubrovnik → island-block leg, before heading into Bosnia). The Pelješac route is on the confirmed route.
- **Price:** Not confirmed (Booking.com wouldn't load). Search: [booking.com: "holiday home in wine yard paradise Dingač Pelješac"](https://www.booking.com/hotel/hr/holiday-home-in-wine-yard-paradise-dingac-peljesac.html)
- **Verdict:** If the group passes through Pelješac for the Korčula/Mljet ferry, a one-night stay in a Dingač vineyard house would be a genuine WOW-tier experience — isolated hillside, wine country, Adriatic views. Better than a typical Dubrovnik apartment. Worth the research investment: search Airbnb for "Dingač Pelješac" and filter for houses with sea views.

## 11. Night Paddle / Moonlight Kayak on Skadar Lake

No dedicated "moonlight kayak" tour was found at any Virpazar operator. Falcon Boat does offer **sunset tours** (departing 16:00-18:00 depending on month; June = 17:00-18:00 departure) — the closest equivalent.

The **Undiscovered Montenegro 7-day kayak + wild camp tour** includes a segment where participants "kayak back to camp by starlight after a fish dinner at a remote hamlet" — but this is a multi-day structured tour, not a standalone evening rental.

**Practical option:** Rent a double kayak from Kingfisher or Falcon in the late afternoon (they close at ~19:00), paddle the lily channels at sunset. Not a full moonlight night but magical. In June, sunset is ~20:30 — ask if you can return kayaks at 20:00. Alternatively, kayak from Rijeka Crnojevića (Enjoy Skadar Lake), which explicitly says "not recommended after 12:00" for open lake but the sheltered river channel near the village is calm.
