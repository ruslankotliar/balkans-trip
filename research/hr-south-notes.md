# Central/South Dalmatia corridor + Dubrovnik-area islands — research notes

Session scope: the Zadar→Dubrovnik drive corridor (Split/Trogir/Omiš/Makarska) + the Dubrovnik-area island play (Mljet vs Korčula vs Pelješac swing, plus zero-driving Lokrum/Elaphiti) + Dubrovnik city. Data pins live in `src/data/hr-south.json` (28 new `candidate` places). Existing user-saved pins (Punta Rata, Sveti Jure, Camping Krvavica, Šćirovac, Montokuc, Split, Omiš, Biokovo Skywalk, Hvar, Dubrovnik) were **verified here but not re-pinned** — findings below.

Treated those as the traveler voice; flagged the few stale (2014–2022) club/nightlife quotes. All coordinates verified via OSM (Photon/Nominatim) — corrections noted where the first guess was wrong.

## 1. Big verdict — does the car go to Hvar/Brač/Vis? **No.** (keep Hvar as a backup only)

Both the schedule and the island itself argue against it:

| Route (with car) | Operator | Crossing | June freq | Car+4 (1-way) | Reality |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Split → Stari Grad (Hvar) | Jadrolinija L635 | ~1h50 | ~7/day | **€81** (€47.60 car + 4×€8.40) | Reservation line but **car spaces sell out in summer**; arrive 60–90min early or get bumped. Lands at Stari Grad, ~20min from Hvar Town. |
| Drvenik → Sućuraj (Hvar east tip) | Jadrolinija L632 | ~30min | ~10/day | €36 (€19.70 + 4×€4.10) | Cheap/short BUT Sućuraj is the **wrong end** — ~76km / ~1h40 slow drive to Hvar Town. |
| *(ref) Split → Hvar Town catamaran* | Krilo/Jadrolinija/TP | ~50–60min | up to ~17/day | **no cars** | The realistic way in: leave the car, foot-passenger, lands directly in Hvar Town. |

**Why skip it entirely on this trip:** our route is Zadar→coast→Dubrovnik then a Dubrovnik-anchored loop — it never actually passes through Split as a base, so Hvar is a deliberate detour, not a freebie. The car buys nothing on a small island (everything clusters around Hvar Town + the Pakleni islands, reached by taxi-boat). And a missed/queued car ferry is an unacceptable risk against the **Jun 18 evening airport pickup**.

**Party-scene update (important, kills the 2015 reputation):** Hvar Town voted in an **85-decibel summer noise limit** (fines ~€1,400) to shed its party image. **Carpe Diem Beach stopped its night parties in Aug 2024** — it's now day + "after-beach" only, closing midnight. Town bars (Kiva, harbour) still close ~2am; "after 2am Hvar town is dead, except the kebab shop" (r/travel). Hula Hula is still a great **sunset** beach bar (but ~€40/sunbed, "overpriced drinks"). Net: Hvar is now upscale see-and-be-seen, **not** a rage-till-sunrise island.

**If the group's priority is actually partying:** the real options are **Split** (broader everyday nightlife, on no detour) and, near Zadar on days 1–2, **Zrće/Pag (Novalja)** — Croatia's unrestricted Ibiza (4 open-air clubs on one beach). Worth flagging to Session A (Zadar). Hvar only makes sense as an optional foot-passenger sunset overnight from Split — kept as a backup, not pinned.

## 2. Dubrovnik approach (late afternoon, Jun 18) — **take the Pelješac Bridge**

Verdict: **D8 → Pelješac Bridge → Ston → D8 to Dubrovnik.** Not Neum, not the ferry.

- **Bridge** = 100% inside Croatia/Schengen → zero border stop, no toll, fixed predictable time. From Ploče you're ~1h15–1h45 to Dubrovnik. "Smooth, well signposted, surprisingly quick... a highlight of the trip." Renovation starts **Oct 2026** (after our trip) — it's open in June.
- **Neum** is only ~12min shorter *and only if there's no queue* — it's an external Schengen↔Bosnia crossing with **summer waits up to ~2h each way**. Unacceptable gamble before a flight pickup.
- **Ploče–Trpanj ferry** adds risk, not removes it (no reservation, arrive 90min early). It's a scenic option for a relaxed day, not a deadline. Table-ready facts:

| Ploče → Trpanj ferry | Value |
|---|---|
| Operator / line | Jadrolinija L633 |
| June frequency | 3/day (Jul–Aug 4/day) |
| June departures (Ploče) | 10:30, 14:15, 19:30 |
| Crossing | ~60min |
| Car ≤5m | €25.20 |
| Adult | €6.70 |
| **Car + 4 (1-way)** | **~€52** |
| Book ahead? | **No reservation** — first-come; arrive 60–90min early in peak |

Bonus: the bridge route runs straight down the Pelješac approach, so **Ston walls + Mali Ston oysters** are right on the path with no ferry anxiety.

## 3. The island play — **Mljet vs Korčula vs both (Pelješac swing)**

All three share the same Pelješac drive out of Dubrovnik, so the decision is really *how many island days* you want. Ferry facts (all Jadrolinija, all **first-come / no car reservation** — queue 60–90min early in June):

| Ferry | Line | Crossing | June freq | Car ≤5m | Adult | **Car+4** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Prapratno ↔ Sobra (**Mljet**) | L832 | ~45min | 5/day | €25.50 | €6.10 | **~€50** |
| Orebić ↔ Dominče (**Korčula**) | L634 | ~20min | ~20–22/day | €14.71–16.20* | €3.99 | **~€31** |

*Korčula car price: sources split €14.71 vs €16.20 — confirm on jadrolinija.hr at booking. The Korčula ferry is so frequent (every ~30–60min) that a missed sailing just means a short wait; Mljet's 5/day is the one to actually plan around.

Drive context (from `drive-times.md`): Dubrovnik→Prapratno 1:39; Dubrovnik→Orebić ~3:00; **Orebić→Prapratno only 1:24** — which is what makes a 2-day combo plausible.

### Verdicts
- **Mljet** — the nature/adventure pick. Lakes (swim/kayak/bike), Montokuc, St Mary islet (free boat), **Odysseus Cave** cliff-jumping near Sobra. BUT ferry lands at Sobra ~30min from the NP, and NP entry is **steep: €25/adult in June (~€100 for 4)** on top of ~€50 ferry. → **Overnight, don't day-trip** (camp at Autokamp Marina/Ropa or Camping Mungos; or apartment in Polače). Buy NP tickets after 16:00 for −25%.
- **Korčula** — the town+beach+wine pick, and the *cheapest/easiest* ferry. Korčula old town (half-day "mini-Dubrovnik") + **Pupnatska Luka** (the wow-bay) + **Lumbarda** (sandy Vela Pržina + Grk wine). **Skip Vela Luka** (far west tip): ~1h each way, Vela Spila is a modest cave, and reviewers say Proizd's water is "no better than the rest of Korčula." Only go west if you want a dedicated Proizd swim-island day.
- **Both, as a 2-day Pelješac swing** — genuinely feasible because Orebić↔Prapratno is only 1:24. Shape: Dubrovnik → **Ston walls + Mali Ston oysters + Matuško winery (Dingač)** → Orebić → Korčula day → back to Orebić → drive to Prapratno → Mljet overnight → ferry Sobra→Prapratno → Dubrovnik/Bosnia. Pelješac itself (Ston, oysters, Dingač wine, Žuljana/Divna/Trstenica beaches) is a worthy thread regardless of which island.

**Recommendation:** if one island → **Mljet overnight** (best fit for an adventurous group: cliff-jumping + kayak + camp). If two days available → the **Pelješac swing (Korčula day + Mljet overnight)** with Ston/oysters/wine on the drive. Pure day-trippers who skip the car go Pelješac-free via the Pomena/Korčula catamarans — not our case.

### Zero-driving options (do these on a Dubrovnik city day, midday when cruise crowds peak)
- **Lokrum** — 10min boat from the old-town harbour, €30 round-trip incl. entry. Peacocks, "Dead Sea" saltwater lake, monastery, GoT throne, and the **best cliff jumping near Dubrovnik (~2–10m)**. No overnight (protected reserve).
- **Elaphiti** — Jadrolinija L807 from **Gruž** (not old-town harbour). **Lopud/Šunj** is the only proper *sand* beach in the area (go early, off by 1pm). **Koločep** has the swim-in **Blue Cave**. **Šipan** is the quiet lunch-and-wine island. Best combo for our crew: a kayak/boat tour pairing Koločep Blue Cave + Šunj.

## 4. Omiš / Cetina — adventure operators & prices (2024–26)

The canyon (~25–30min south of Split, on-route) is the coast's adventure hub. All operators cluster in/around Omiš; the canyoning happens upstream near Zadvarje/Gubavica.

| Activity | What | Difficulty | Price pp | Operator real people name | Book ahead? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Zipline** | 8 lines, 2,100m, longest 700m @150m | crowd-pleaser, no skill | **€80** (private €120) | Zip-line Croatia (everyone resells this course) | Yes, ≥1 day in June |
| **Canyoning — Extreme** ⭐ | ~12m jumps + rappels nr 60m Gubavica falls | fit adults 18+ | **€70** | **Malduk / Canyoning.Cetina** (Mtn-Rescue guides); **Vladimir @ Dal Mare** (named repeatedly) | Yes |
| Canyoning — Basic | up to ~5m jumps | beginner/family | €50 | same | Yes |
| **Rafting** | ~9–12km, Penšići → Radmanove Mlinice | tame, Class I–II + a few III | **€45** (incl. transfer) | Rafting Cetina (clean local) | recommended |
| Fortica/Starigrad fortress | 262m cliff fort, ~45min hike | moderate | €3.50 cash | self | no |

**Pick for this group:** Extreme canyoning (~€70) is the activity that earns "best thing we did" reviews and matches the cliff-jumps+rappels brief. Zipline is the easy crowd-pleaser. Rafting is the gentle scenic option (good if mixed energy). Camp at **Auto Camp Lisičina** right by the old town (~€30–40 for 4, cash, walkable, hot showers).

## 5. Makarska Riviera — user-saved verifications + additions

**Verified user-saved pins (kept as-is in hr-user-saved.json):**
- **Punta Rata (Brela)** ✓ 43.37, 16.9228. Famous pine-backed pebble beach; the Brela Stone is the postcard. **Morning swim only** — packed after ~10am, parking €4/h or €20/day and scarce.
- **Camping Krvavica** ✓ 43.3233, 16.9860. **Confirmed operating, tents OK.** ~€6–12/person + €6–12 pitch + €2.70–5.50 electricity → **~€35–55/night for 4** (June mid-tier). Hot showers, pine shade, beach 5min, WiFi, laundry. Open Apr–Oct. Strong fit; rating 4.
- **Sveti Jure** ✓ summit pin fine (1762m, drive-up via toll road). See the new **Biokovo toll-road logistics pin** — €15/adult June (~€60 for 4), **must book a timed online slot (20 cars/hour cap)**, road is single-lane-two-way but doable in a rental; open 06:00–20:00.
- **Šćirovac** ⚠ **the saved coord likely points at the easy "French Road" panoramic walk above Brela, NOT the summit.** The actual Šćirovac is a **1,619m peak = a hard 4–4.5h alpine-start hike** (via Bukovac/Pozjata cave), not a quick viewpoint. Clarify with the group which they want; for most, the Biokovo drive gives 90% of the view for 10% of the effort.

**Additions pinned:** Nugal hidden beach (unsigned 30min walk S of Makarska, ex-nudist, snorkelling); Makarska nightlife (Deep cave club + Osejava cliff-jumping + Riva bars); Gornja Vala/Gradac and Tučepi as quieter southern-end swims (Gradac corrected to **43.106, 17.342** — earlier estimates were ~50km off).

⚠️ **Camp Baško Polje no longer takes tents** — rebranded **"Pinadria Camping Resort"** (mobile-home/glamping only for 2026). Don't route a tent there. **"Camping Sirena" on the Riviera couldn't be verified** — likely doesn't exist here (other Sirena sites are in Istria/Krk). **Krvavica is the tent winner** on this strip.

## 6. Split / Trogir — corridor stops

- **Split:** Diocletian's Palace + Riva + **Vidilica viewpoint** (15min up Marjan stairs) = the high-value ~2.5–3h core. Swim at **Kašjuni** (clearest water, locals' pick) over crowded **Bačvice** (go to Bačvice only to watch *picigin*). Nightlife is fun-not-legendary — old-town bar crawl (Charlie's → Vanilla) beats the Bačvice mega-clubs (pricey cover + bouncer complaints). A notch below Hvar/Zrće for clubbing — don't reshape the schedule for it. *Verify specific club names closer to 2026; couldn't cross-check on Reddit and the scene churns.*
- **Trogir:** worth a **1.5–2h** stop (UNESCO old town + St Lawrence cathedral; Kamerlengo fortress optional — pay €5 only for the rooftop view, interior is "dilapidated... no info inside"). Not a base; evenings are quiet. Don't double up beaches here if you swam at Kašjuni.

## 7. Dubrovnik city — crowd-dodging + swim/nightlife

- **City Walls** €40 (2025): go at **08:00 opening** or **after ~15:00**; avoid 10:30–15:00 (cruise window). Enter via **Ploče Gate or St John's Fortress**, not jammed Pile. **June is a relative cruise lull** (2 ships/day capped); check cruisetimetables.com / cruisemapper for your day and pick low-ship gaps. Spend midday **off the rock** (Lokrum/Elaphiti), return for sunset.
- **Srđ** at sunset (hike free/late-afternoon, or cable car €27 return; museum ~€5).
- **Swim:** **Sveti Jakov** (quiet local favourite, 150 stairs) > Banje (crowded beach club). **Buža II** for cliff-jumping + sunset drinks (cash only).
- **Nightlife:** chill/upscale, not a party town. Buža for sunset; **Culture Club Revelin** (in the fortress) is the one proper big club — pre-drink, it's expensive. D'Vino wine bar for the calm option. *(Troubadour jazz bar unverified for 2024/25 — confirm on arrival.)*
