## 1. Zrmanja River Kayaking — Operators, Prices, DIY Options

### Key Finding: No independent paddling allowed
The Zrmanja flows through Velebit Nature Park. Solo/DIY paddling is **not permitted** — the only legal access is through a licensed operator. The designated section runs from Kaštel Žegarski to Muškovci (approx. 12 km). This is a hard constraint, not a preference.

### Best Operator: F-Zrmanja (f-zrmanja.com)
The main specialist operator, based at the put-in point. Most reviews point here first.

- **Price:** €55 adults / €35 children under 13
- **Duration:** 6 hours total — 4h on the water + 2h for breaks, meal, refreshments
- **Departure:** 09:30 arrival, on water by 10:15, finish at base by ~14:30, depart 16:00
- **What's included:** coffee, drinks, fruit, park entrance fees, guide, equipment, insurance
- **Contact:** +385 92 360 8387 (also WhatsApp) | f-zrmanja.com
- **Seasonal note:** Kayak season June–November; rafting only April–May (high water). June = kayak mode confirmed.

### Also bookable via:
- **Raftrek Adventure Travel** (raftrek.com/tour/kayaking-zrmanja-river/) — 4.9/5, 548 reviews, books the same stretch
- **GetYourGuide** (t414275) — "River Zrmanja Guided Kayak Safari & Waterfalls" — starts from ~€35 (possibly a partial/shorter route or promotional price; verify)
- **Checkyeti** — Classic kayaking from €35 (same caveat)
- **Tripadvisor direct booking** available on the attraction page

### What recent travelers say
- "Crystal-clear river, fig-tree-lined gorge, feels untouched" — consistently the top descriptor
- "Incredibly good value" repeated across multiple 2024–2025 reviews
- "My favourite day of a wonderful week in Zadar" (Sep 2024)
- Guides rated excellent: professional, friendly, safety-conscious, let you go at your own pace
- Multiple swimming stops with rope swings, optional waterfall jumps up to ~5m
- Works for complete beginners; mild rapids, nothing technical
- One older complaint about equipment odor (2016) — not repeated in recent reviews
- Meal quality criticism appears in a 2019 review — more recent visitors seem satisfied with the included fruit and drinks

### Timing advice
- **Book at least a day ahead** — fills quickly in summer (June = high season)
- **Morning start is the right call** — "mornings are quieter, light is perfect for photos," fewer other groups on the river
- Departure is fixed at 09:30 so you get the morning slot by default
- ~1h from Zadar, ~1.5h from Zadar via Paklenica approach — factor transfer time

## 2. Cetina Canyon — Zipline & Rafting Details

### Zipline Operator: Red Adventures (confirmed primary provider)
**Website:** red-adventures.com/offer/zipline-omis-river-cetina-canyon/
**Email:** info@red-adventures.com | **Phone:** +385 95 255 22 55

#### The setup
- 8 cables, 2,100m total, longest wire = 700m at 150m above canyon floor
- Groups: 2–12 people, 2 guides
- Duration: ~3 hours on the cables
- Min age: 7 years. Max weight: 140 kg.
- TripAdvisor: 4.9/5 from 1,279 reviews (as of mid-2025)

#### Price
- **€80/person** (independent booking, no transfer)
- **€100/person** with transfer from Split

#### Booking
- **Book by the day before at the latest** — slots every 30 min from 08:00 to 17:00 in summer
- GetYourGuide listing for comparison: https://www.getyourguide.com/omis-l2096/omis-3-hour-zipline-over-cetina-canyon-t95919/
- Free cancellation up to 24h before

#### Morning timing trick — CONFIRMED
One 2022 reviewer specifically recommended **booking the 07:30 slot** (the earliest), noting it "can get really hot up in the hills — 30°C+ by midday." The hike up to the first platform is described as "a challenge" on unstable terrain — doing it in morning cool is the obvious call. May 2026 reviewer: "nothing compares to the adrenaline rush... most amazing and professional experience." September 2025: "great experience, recommended."

#### Meeting point
Old part of Omiš, Poljička Square on the river Cetina, 100m from the bridge toward Radman's Mills.

## 3. Pelješac Wine — Better Than Dingač Alone

The wine action on Pelješac is concentrated in two zones: the **Potomje/Dingač corridor** (central, inland) and the **Orebić–Lovište western tip**. Here's the actionable breakdown for a self-driving group passing through.

### The key stop: **Potomje village** (on D414, signposted off the main spine)
Potomje is a tiny village inside the Dingač wine appellation zone. Multiple family wineries within walking distance of each other — this is the easiest drop-in cluster.

**Must-visit in Potomje:**

#### Vinarija Miloš (Ponikve / Potomje area)
- Family winery, patriarch Frano Miloš or sons Josip/Ivan will likely host you
- Old wine cellar carved into rocky mountainside — genuinely atmospheric
- **Hours: daily 10:00–18:00** (April 2026 reviewer confirmed this)
- Walk-ins accommodated — one reviewer arrived 30 minutes before closing and still got a tour+tasting
- Only Plavac Mali varieties: quality, premium Stagnum, rosé, semi-sweet, dessert
- Award-winning wines; ships internationally
- **Location:** Boljenovici 15, Ston 20230 | +385 20 / milos.hr

#### Matuško Winery (Potomje 5a)
- Largest underground wine cellar on the peninsula — very walkable from Miloš
- Handles high volume (tour buses stop here) so drop-ins are reliable
- Broad portfolio including Plavac Mali, white wines, liqueurs, olive oil
- Phone: +385 20 742 399

#### Vicelić (Dingač zone)
- Certified organic; views of the sea from the vineyard terrace
- Smaller, quieter than Matuško — more personal

### Better than Dingač alone: Bartulović (Prizdrina)
- Family winery, 480-year history, revitalized by young Mario Bartulović
- **Only producer on Pelješac offering rosé**
- Sustainable/organic production, pioneering in the area
- More of a detour (central spine toward Orebić) but worth it for wine geeks

### The WOW winery for an experience: Saints Hills (Zagruda, near Trpanj)
- Brand-new cellar + tasting centre, "edge of an ancient hill village"
- Guided cellar tour + "Treasury room"
- More polished/premium than family cellars
- Tripadvisor: strong reviews
- **Requires advance call/email** — not pure walk-in

### Best roadside stop specifically between Ston and ferry port (Orebić)
The D414 runs through the interior. **Potomje** is the cleanest stop — it's literally on the road, signed, small enough to walk between two wineries in 20 minutes. Driving from Ston → Potomje → Orebić ferry adds maybe 10–15 minutes vs the coast road. Absolutely worth it.

### Konoba pairing for the wine road
**Konoba Feral Brijesta** (see §6 below) is on the water in Brijesta, roughly between Ston and Potomje — combine wine in Potomje with lunch/dinner at Feral.

## 4. Makarska Riviera — Hidden Beaches

The main tourist beaches (Makarska town beach, Brela/Punta Rata) get crowded by mid-morning in June. Here are the actual local alternatives, from north to south:

### Vruja Beach (near Brela) — STRONG FIND ★★★★
- Between Brela and Pisak, accessible only by steep trail or by sea
- **FKK/naturist** designated but families with children frequent it too
- Dramatic cliffs, red-rock canyon feel, turquoise water
- "Croatia's most dramatic and untouched seaside escape" — multiple sources
- Steep zigzag trail down — 20–30 min hike, genuine commitment
- Best reached early morning or boat; afternoon = crowds from Brela
- **Status: verified genuine local gem** — consistently described as "requires effort to reach = fewer people"
- Sources: https://croatiahiddengems.com/beaches-and-islands/vruja-beach-in-makarska | https://makarskabeaches.com/index.php/plaze/62/en/brela_vruja

### Virgin Beach / Djevičanska (between Drašnice and Igrane) ★★★
- ~1 km of unspoiled pebble beach, potable freshwater springs on site
- Accessible via old unpaved road between Drašnice and Igrane — park along the road, short walk down
- **Free parking.** Zero facilities.
- Very quiet — accessible by car but not many tourists know this track
- The Drašnice–Igrane stretch in general has "many small bays only reached by boat" and wild naturist locals
- Sources: https://www.frankaboutcroatia.com/makarska-riviera-beaches-hidden-gems-part-2/

### Garma Coves (Podgora) ★★★
- "Picturesque bay with five or six beautiful beaches" at Podgora's far end
- Easily accessible on foot from Podgora village
- Pine shade, pebble, calm — locals' spot

### Velika Duba (Živogošće) ★★★
- Sheltered hidden bay with pine cover, crystal clear water
- ~30 min south of Makarska by car
- Accessible from road or forest paths — option to swim into a small sea cave
- "Very pristine, abundant with freshwater sources"
- Sources: various Croatian local blogs; confirmed in English sources

### Ramova-Čurilo cluster (Krvavica) ★★
- Multiple small beaches accessible from Krvavica village (where Camping Krvavica is)
- Parking ~€3/day
- Good if you're already camping there

### Nugal Beach (Makarska) — Already in database but worth the sub-note
- 20-min hike through pine forest, no facilities
- FKK (naturist) but widely used by non-naturists
- 30m waterfall in spring, dry cliff in June
- Already shortlisted; confirmed active as a genuine hidden gem

### NOTE on timing
June = start of peak season. Most of these beaches are **still uncrowded in early June**, but Vruja and Nugal get busy by July. Weekday mornings are the move.

## 5. New Moon / Dark Sky Window (Jun 15–18)

**Context:** New moon was Jun 15. Jun 16–18 is the darkest sky window of the entire trip. On-route on those nights: Zadar area → coast → Makarska → Dubrovnik. This is the exact corridor where dark sky opportunities exist.

### Best confirmed dark sky spots on the Jun 16–18 route:

#### Cape Planka (Rt Planka) — near Rogoznica ★★★★★
- The most prominent cape of the Croatian mainland — **no islands block the seaward view** toward Italy
- Light pollution from the mainland falls behind you when facing the sea
- Professional astrophotographers specifically use this for Milky Way over open water
- Drive: ~30–40 min from Split toward Rogoznica, then ~15–20 min walk along coast
- **No facilities, dark, accessible.** Interesting foreground: small chapel + lighthouse
- This is the best documented dark sky coastal spot between Zadar and Dubrovnik
- **Night Jun 16 (drive day):** If staying somewhere between Zadar and Split, this is within reach for a late-night detour. But it's 30–40 min from Split, so only practical if you're sleeping near Split.
- Sources: https://lidija-photo.com/blog-en/astrophotography/milky-way-above-cape-planka/ (professional astrophotography report from this exact spot)

#### Biokovo ridge (Sveti Jure access road) ★★★★
- Park closes at 20:00 (last entry 19:00) in summer — **no night access via the official road**
- BUT: the Biokovo ridge can be hiked to from the bottom; the Sveti Jure road isn't the only route
- At 1,762m altitude: zero light pollution from the sea, exceptional sky
- **Practical limitation:** park closes at 20:00, gates lock. Not accessible for a spontaneous stargazing drive-up in June.
- **Option:** The park opening in June is 06:00–20:00; drive up at 18:00, hike/sit until darkness at ~21:30 (June sunset ~20:40), then you're locked in. Actually viable if you time it — but you'd be inside the park after closing. Risk factor.
- The **Biokovo Skywalk** is a day attraction only (officially).

#### Inland from Makarska — road to Biokovo ★★★
- Even without reaching Sveti Jure, the D327 road climbing from Makarska toward Biokovo gets you above coastal light pollution very quickly
- The first few km of mountain road above Makarska have minimal development and good sky-view
- Not officially a dark sky park but practically dark after 22:00

#### Pelješac peninsula (Jun 17–18 night) ★★★
- The road across Pelješac is almost completely unlit
- The ridge viewpoints between Potomje and Orebić face the open sea south — no islands blocking
- Very low light pollution for a main road; good for a spontaneous stop
- **Brijesta bay shore** (where Konoba Feral is) faces south across the channel — quiet, dark at night

### Verdict
**Cape Planka is the single best accessible dark sky spot on the Jun 16–18 segment** — confirmed by astrophotographers, accessible by car + short walk, no permit required, no curfew. It sits between Split and Zadar (slightly north of the main route). 

If sleeping near Split on Jun 16 night, a 22:00 drive to Cape Planka is feasible. If sleeping south of Split, Pelješac or roadside Biokovo ridge is the opportunistic alternative on Jun 17 night.

**Approximate coordinates for Cape Planka:** 43.495°N, 15.965°E (Rt Planka lighthouse) — verify on OSM before navigating.

## 6. Konoba Recommendations — Omiš/Cetina + Pelješac

### Near Omiš / Cetina Canyon

#### Konoba Joskan ★★★★
- **Best overall for food quality in Omiš**
- Hidden entrance via narrow alleyway off the back street — locals know it
- Open fire at the entrance for meat cooking; traditional Dalmatian
- "Sea bass incredibly fresh, simply cooked" — July 2025 visitor
- Notable for: authentic feel, genuinely local (not tourist-facing entrance on main square)
- TripAdvisor: well-rated, consistently praised for fish and atmosphere

#### Konoba Ćaća ★★★★
- Riverside location, strong seafood, "great views during the day"
- Known for generous portions
- More accessible/findable than Joskan — on the river road
- "Superb restaurant near the river, great service" — multiple reviews

#### Konoba Gusti ★★★
- Family-run, rustic wooden decor, open fireplace
- "Difficult to find in a small street in the old town, but worth every step"
- "Best restaurant in the area around Omiš" during one summer stay (2024)

#### Gusarska Konoba ★★★
- Cozy outdoor seating, "stunning views of the surrounding landscape"
- Described as a highlight for visitors to Omiš
- Good for a longer meal with views

**Recommendation for the group:** Konoba Joskan for the food if you're willing to hunt for the alley. Konoba Ćaća for easy riverside lunch before or after the zipline/rafting.

### Pelješac — Seafood with a View

#### Konoba Feral Brijesta ★★★★★ — TOP PICK
- **Location:** Brijesta village, facing the bay — on the D415 running along the south coast of Pelješac, roughly between Ston and Potomje
- **The owner/family catch the fish daily** — father-in-law is the fisherman
- Specialties: black tagliatelle with seafood (homemade pasta), fresh oysters, black risotto
- "Probably the best seafood pasta I've ever had" (October 2024)
- Rating: 4.7/5 on Restaurant Guru across 600 reviews; perfect 5.0/5 on TripAdvisor (6 reviews, consistently enthusiastic)
- Prices: ~€25–30/person, described as "refreshingly fair for the quality"
- **On-route:** if you drive the south coast road of Pelješac (D415) toward Potomje, Brijesta is right on the way — not a detour
- **Booking:** small place — **call ahead in peak season** — may not have tables without reservation
- Facebook: facebook.com/p/Konoba-Feral-Brijesta-100094404580459

#### Konoba Barsa (Lovište) ★★★★
- At the far western tip of Pelješac, on the harbor in Lovište
- 40 years of hospitality; owner personally advises on fish selection
- House specials: lobster buzzara with homemade pasta, grilled endemic mullet
- Sunset views from the terrace, right on the water
- Rating: 3.9/5 (460 reviews); TripAdvisor also strong
- **Only relevant if taking the Orebić ferry and returning to explore western Pelješac** — Lovište is ~15km past Orebić, not on the through-route
- Reservations strongly recommended in peak season

#### Gastro Mare (Kobaš, near Ston) ★★★
- Fresh local ingredients, waterfront, own beach bar and shower facilities
- Popular with yachters — fresh seafood, great sunsets
- Practical if you're coming through Ston area and want a longer stop

## 7. Campsite Verification

### Camping Krvavica — VERIFIED OPEN, TENTS ALLOWED ✓
- **Status:** Open May–October, confirmed operational for 2026
- Tents explicitly allowed alongside caravans, campervans, bungalows
- Recent reviews (2025–2026): "beautiful little camp, wonderful and helpful staff, peaceful" (Jun 2025), "quiet campsite with sufficient shade, friendly German-speaking manager, 200m from pebble beach" (May 2025)
- TripAdvisor ranking: #1 of 4 specialty lodging in Krvavica
- **Location:** Heart of Makarska Riviera, 6km from Makarska town
- Multiple small beaches (Ramova-Čurilo cluster) within easy reach from the camp
- ACSI, Campercontact, and park4night all list it as active
- Sources:
  - https://www.tripadvisor.com/Hotel_Review-g1943764-d21002761-Reviews-Camping_Krvavica-Krvavica_Split_Dalmatia_County_Dalmatia.html
  - https://park4night.com/en/place/10004
  - https://www.eurocampings.co.uk/croatia/split-dalmatia/krvavica/camping-krvavica-122288/

### Auto Camp Lisičina (Omiš) — VERIFIED OPEN, TENTS ALLOWED ✓
- **Status:** Open all year round — confirmed as year-round operation
- Tents allowed; arranged pitches for tents, caravans, campers
- **Price:** €13–20/night
- Rating: 3.8/5 — mixed reviews; positives dominate on quiet location and clean facilities
- Genuine positives: very nice location between rocks, 5-min walk to old town, 10-min walk to beach; modern and clean ablutions; free wifi; kitchen and BBQ available
- Genuine negatives: very tight/narrow access road (difficult for longer/wider vehicles), tent pitches cramped with little privacy, some noise from neighbors
- **Suitability for the group:** ideal as a base for the Cetina activities — you're in walking distance of Omiš old town and the canyon action. The cramped pitches are a mild issue for 4 people; not a dealbreaker.
- Own website: ac-lisicina.hr
