## 2. Gear Packing List

**Legend:** E = Essential / N = Nice-to-have / S = Skip

### Sleeping

| Item | Rating | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| Sleeping bag rated to +5°C (comfort) / 0°C (limit) | **E** | The Žabljak nights (7–12°C) make a cold-rated bag essential. A coastal-only summer bag (+15°C limit) will leave you miserable in Durmitor. One bag per person that works everywhere — a 3-season (~0°C) or 4-season bag is overkill but comfortable |
| Sleeping pad / mat | **E** | Don't assume campsites have soft grass. Paklenica, Mljet, and Durmitor are stony ground |
| Tent with decent waterproofing | **E** | June is Durmitor's rainiest season. Check fly seams before packing |
| Small pillow or compressible pillow | N | Some Airbnbs provide, campsites never do |

**Sleeping bag temperature decision:** A bag rated to 0°C comfort (or +5°C lower-limit, which has a comfort of ~+10°C) covers the full trip. Coastal nights (Zadar, Dubrovnik, Budva) are 15–17°C so a 3-season bag is borderline overkill on those nights — fine, not oppressive in summer. Do NOT bring a summer bag (+15°C rated) and expect it to be fine in Žabljak. It won't be.

## 3. Day-Specific Clothing Logic

### Days 1–3: Zadar → Makarska → Dubrovnik (Jun 16–18)

**Weather:** 27–30°C days, 17°C nights. Sea 21–22°C.

Pack for the beach. The fleece and rain jacket ride in the bag — you won't need them. The one exception is Day 1 if doing Paklenica gorge in the afternoon: the canyon is shaded and always 5–8°C cooler than the coast.

For Biokovo/Sveti Jure (Day 3 morning): **no ski gear needed** — summit is 1762m and in June it's typically 15–20°C at the top, but windy. Layering: t-shirt + light long-sleeve + windproof shell in your daypack. Swap shorts for trousers on the summit ridge. Back in Makarska by midday: swimwear.

### Days 4–5: Dubrovnik / Mljet (Jun 18–20)

**Weather:** 25–27°C. Sea 22–23°C. Same coastal vibe.

Walls, island, beach. No mountain kit needed. The one dress-code consideration: Lokrum Monastery ruins are casual, no requirement. Light linen shirt for Dubrovnik Old Town evenings — it's a nice-restaurant/bar scene.

### Day 6: Kravica + Mostar (Jun 21)

**Weather:** 28–32°C. **No sea.**

This is the hottest day of the trip. Kravica: swimwear + light clothes over the top. Bring 2L+ water per person — the canyon is sunny and humid. Mostar old town at midday: punishing. Stari Most evening: tolerable. Pack lightest clothes. The fleece stays in the car. Linen shirt for the evening bar crawl.

### Day 7: Sarajevo (Jun 22)

**Weather:** 23–24°C day, 11°C evening.

Transitional day. Morning can be a t-shirt; by evening you'll want a light layer for the cable car (Trebević summit is breezy). The fleece or long-sleeve earns its first outing. The Latin night at Underground Club — whatever you feel comfortable dancing in.

### Days 8–9: Tara Rafting + Žabljak / Durmitor (Jun 23–24)

**Weather:** 18–22°C day, 7–12°C night. High rain risk.

**The cold zone.** Night temps regularly 7–10°C at the campsite (1,450m). You will be cold without the right sleeping bag. Layer up for the campsite evenings: base layer + fleece + jacket. Rain shell on standby every afternoon.

**Tara rafting (Day 8 morning):** Wetsuit, life jacket, and helmet are all provided by operators. Bring: swimwear underneath, towel, dry clothes for after, sandals/shoes you don't mind getting wet, sunscreen. Leave electronics in the car or in provided waterproof bags.

**Durmitor hike — Prutaš (Day 9):** Start 07:00 = cold start (8–10°C at that hour). Full hiking boots, long hiking trousers, base layer, fleece, shell in pack. By midday on the trail: t-shirt weather. Afternoon thunderstorms are common — be off exposed ridges by 14:00 ideally.

### Day 10: Ostrog Monastery + Skadar Lake (Jun 25)

**Weather:** 28–30°C dropping back to warm.

Back to hot. Ostrog dress code is the main gear decision here: covered shoulders and covered knees for both genders entering the upper monastery. A sarong or light cotton trousers + long-sleeve shirt thrown over swimwear works. The monastery reportedly provides wraps at the entrance if you're caught out, but don't rely on it. Wear the modest layer, it takes 5 minutes and then you're back in shorts.

Skadar Lake in the evening: mosquito territory at dusk. Long sleeves + repellent after 18:00.

### Days 11–13: Budva → Kotor → Trebinje → Dubrovnik (Jun 26–28)

**Weather:** 24–26°C. Sea 21–24°C (Bay of Kotor warmest).

Back to coast mode. Kotor fortress is a sweat-fest if started after 09:00 — start 07:30, bring 1.5L water per person. The fortress climb is ~1,500 steps and fully exposed after the lower sections.

Final days: lightest kit. The fleece, sleeping bags, and tent are now just dead weight — they've done their job.

## 4. Key Warnings

### Žabljak sleeping bag — non-negotiable

The campsite is at 1,450m. Average June lows are 7–10°C, with records down to −2°C in freak cold snaps. A summer bag rated for +15°C comfort is completely inadequate. You need a bag rated to **0°C comfort** (or lower-limit +5°C) for each person sleeping in a tent. If you rent/borrow, confirm the rating before you go. This is the most expensive packing mistake to make on the trip.

### Piva Canyon tunnels — headlamps are useful, not just for drama

The road from Šćepan Polje to Plužine passes through **56 unlit tunnels** carved into the rock. The tunnels are narrow, dark, and have no lighting. Car headlights handle the driving, but a headlamp is useful if you stop to take photos through tunnel windows, or if you pull over at one of the canyon viewpoints. Not a safety emergency, but add it to the pre-canyon check: headlamps accessible, not buried in the boot.

### Ostrog dress code — both genders, no exceptions

Shorts and bare shoulders get you turned away from the Upper Monastery. Orthodox Christian active monastery, enforced. Pack a sarong per person or at least 2 for the group — it weighs nothing and takes 3 seconds to tie. The wraps at the entrance are a fallback, not a guarantee.

### Biokovo / Sveti Jure — pre-book the car slot

This is a logistical warning rather than gear, but it has gear consequences: the road up is access-controlled (limited car slots, must pre-book via Biokovo Nature Park website). If you don't have the slot, you walk from the bottom, which is a full-day commitment and a much larger gear undertaking (3–4L water, full hiking kit).

### Water: know where to fill, where to buy

- **Croatia:** Tap water is safe everywhere on the route. Zadar, Split, Makarska, Dubrovnik — all EU-regulated, spring-sourced.
- **Bosnia (Mostar, Sarajevo, Kravica):** Mixed. Urban tap water in Sarajevo and Mostar is generally treated and locals drink it, but the infrastructure in rural areas is aging. For towns: fine to use for brushing teeth, buy bottled for drinking. Boračko jezero and Kravica: fill from bought bottles.
- **Montenegro:** Generally safe. Locals drink tap water in Podgorica and the interior. Coastal areas (Budva, Kotor) are more chlorinated due to summer demand — many travelers prefer bottled there. Mountain streams in Durmitor look clean but carry giardia risk — don't drink without filtering.
- **Durmitor/Žabljak:** Buy or filter. The campsite should have tap water — confirm on check-in.

### Ticks in June — Durmitor is the risk zone

June is peak tick season in the Balkans. Durmitor's forested and grassy trails are the main exposure point. Long trousers tucked into socks, insect repellent with DEET on trousers and socks, and a thorough body check after each hike. The Prutaš trail goes through forest and meadows. Carry a tick removal card or tweezers.

### June sea temperatures — no wetsuit needed

The Adriatic in mid-to-late June is 21–23°C. This is genuinely warm and all four of you should swim at every opportunity. No wetsuit required for any sea swimming on this trip. The Tara river (cold, glacier-fed, ~10°C) is a different matter — that's what the provided wetsuit at the rafting operator covers.

### Mostar midday — avoid outdoor activity 11:00–16:00

Mostar in June reaches 30–32°C and the limestone of the old city radiates heat. It's not dangerous but it's genuinely unpleasant. Do your Kravica swim early (07:00 arrival), arrive Mostar mid-afternoon, explore Baščaršija equivalent in early evening, cross Stari Most at sunset. The heat is the schedule-setter here.
