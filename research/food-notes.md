Companion to `src/data/food.json` (37 entries, all `status:"candidate"`, coordinates verified against OpenStreetMap/Nominatim). This file holds the stuff that doesn't fit the schema: the per-overnight-zone "must-eat" shortlist, the camping-supplies/water strategy, typical prices per country, and the wineries/peka decisions the group still has to make.

The cheap institutions (ćevapi windows, burek under the sač, Tanjga, peka you split, self-catering from markets) carry the trip; the 3 marquee splurges (Mali Ston oysters, Pelješac plavac mali, Njeguši pršut) are documented but flagged as treats, not defaults. Almost everything survives being dropped — that's the point.

## The decisions the group still owns

### Skadar Lake winery — recommended: **Vinarija Mašanović (Virpazar)** (`me-vinarija-masanovic`)
The skeleton's "Skadar kayak or winery" stop should be **Mašanović**, not a backtrack to the already-listed Kopitović (`me-skadar-winery-kopitovic`):
- **Value:** flat **€10/person for 4-5 wines + a rakija**, repeatedly called "super affordable" — perfect for four value-hunters.
- **Logistics:** it's **right in Virpazar** on the M-2, so it bolts straight onto a Virpazar fish lunch (`me-konoba-badanj`), kayak rental, and the Pavlova Strana viewpoint with **zero detour**. Kopitović sits a few km out.
- **Substance:** genuine 10-generation Crmnica Vranac producer, strongest/most consistent host reviews.
- **Caveats:** **book ≥1 day ahead** (no walk-in tastings) and **cash only**. Backups if full: Konoba Virpazar (restaurant-winery) or the vineyard village of **Godinje** (scenic cluster, e.g. Winery Garnet).
- **Data correction:** the "Vino & Ribe" lead from the brief is a mix-up — that restaurant is in **Ljubljana, Slovenia**, not Virpazar. The real Virpazar fish spots are **Badanj** and **Konoba Virpazar**.

### Pelješac plavac mali winery — two distinct options now in data
- Existing: **Matuško** (`hr-matusko-winery`, Potomje/Dingač) — the big, accessible cellar.
- New: **Grgić Vina** (`hr-grgic-vina-trstenik`) — Mike Grgich's headland cellar, €10 tasting, two top-tier wines (Plavac Mali, Pošip), surreal setting but bottles pricey (~€34). Pick Grgić for the name + the view, Matuško for breadth. **Konoba Antunović** (`hr-konoba-antunovic-kuna`) is the cheap farm-peka lunch on the same wine road.

### Peka (pre-order, split the cost) — three coastal options
`hr-konoba-gusti-omis` (Omiš), `hr-konoba-feral-brela` (Brela), `hr-konoba-antunovic-kuna` (Pelješac). All need a phone call 2-24h ahead. One whole octopus/veal peka feeds the table — frame it as the group's "nice night," not a daily expense.

### Mali Ston oysters — real prices
~**€1 each at roadside farm stands** (Mali Ston/Bistrina, open ~9-16), ~**€1.3-2 each at restaurant tables** (served in 3s). Cheapest authentic experience = buy off a stand and eat them on the seawall. `hr-bota-sare-mali-ston` is the sit-down version (and generally better value than its neighbour Kapetanova Kuća).

## Camping-supplies strategy (cheapest chains per country)

**Croatia 🇭🇷** — **Lidl** is the consistent budget winner (install Lidl Plus for coupons); **Eurospin** similar where present. **Konzum** is the most ubiquitous but a touch pricier. **Plodine** sits mid-pack, conveniently on town-exit roads (also stocks beach gear) — good for a big drive-in stock-up. **Tommy** dominates Dalmatia and is the go-to for local **cheese + pršut**. **Studenac** = small corner shops, most expensive per item, emergency-only. → *Big load at Lidl/Plodine on the way into a region; Tommy for cured meat/cheese; Studenac only for last-minute.* Real food experiences double as supply runs: `hr-trznica-zadar`, `hr-split-pazar-ribarnica`.

**Bosnia 🇧🇦** — **Bingo** is the home-grown giant and the cheapest/widest-stocked (frequent promos, big out-of-town hypermarkets); pin: `ba-bingo-mostar`. **Konzum** (Croatian-owned) and **Amko Komerc** are the next tier. → *Hit a Bingo on the way into/out of Mostar before the inland mountain stretch (Konjic/Sutjeska), where shops thin out.* Cash in KM is handy.

**Montenegro 🇲🇪** — **Voli** dominates and is the reliable cheapest mainstream chain (branches in every town); pin: `me-voli-budva`. **IDEA** sometimes marginally cheaper but rarer; **Franca** has the best butcher counters; **Aroma** = small tourist-town shops (convenient, pricier). → ***Stock up in Žabljak before driving the Durmitor ring*** — limited shops up high.

## Free water refills (don't waste budget on bottled)

- **Croatia:** tap water is fully potable EU-standard; **Dalmatia has some of the cleanest** (Split/Solin/Trogir run on the Jadro spring). Fill straight from the apartment/campsite tap; public fountains in Zadar old town; Dubrovnik = **Onofrio's Fountain**. *Island caveat:* in peak summer some small-island supply is tanker-delivered and can taste off — refill on the mainland before a ferry day.
- **Bosnia:** **Sarajevo** tap is safe and good (mountain spring, heavily chlorinated) — the **Sebilj fountain** + public česme are drinkable; no active 2026 advisory found. **Mostar** is generally safe/treated but locals avoid the tap **right after heavy rain** (farm runoff). Blagaj/Buna and Boračko are karst-spring areas with clean public česme.
- **Montenegro:** tap water safe across the country; coast tastes more mineralised in summer (some prefer bottled, still potable). **Durmitor/Žabljak springs are excellent free refill points** — there's a drinkable cold spring near **Zminje (Snake) Lake** and around Black Lake; fill bottles for free on the hikes. Žabljak was literally named "Varezina voda" (water) for its springs.
- **Camping kit:** carry a 5-10 L jerry can; every legit campsite has potable taps — top up there rather than buying bottled.
