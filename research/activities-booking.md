### 1. Krka National Park
**Days 1–2 (Jun 16–17)**

**Booking:** MANDATORY online. The park requires online pre-booking at [shop.np-krka.hr](https://shop.np-krka.hr). Tickets are NOT available on-site — only online. Pick a timed entry slot. Morning slots (before noon) sell out fastest.

**Price:** €40/adult for June–September. €15/child (under 18). After 15:00: €30/adult.
- 4 adults = **€160**. If you arrive after 15:00: **€120**.

**Time needed:** 3–5 hours for the main Skradinski Buk loop. Allow a full half-day.

**Best timing:** Arrive at opening (early morning) OR go after 15:00 for reduced price and smaller crowds. Midday in June is packed and blazing hot.

**Watch out for:**
- You're on the way from Zadar to Split. Skradin entrance is the main one (good parking). Lozovac entrance is an alternative.
- Swimming used to be allowed — it was banned in 2021 due to algae; do NOT plan on it. You walk the boardwalks only.
- Plitvice is a separate decision (it's 4h21m out of the way north — see route-skeleton.md). If doing Plitvice, same advance booking rules apply but **3–4 weeks in advance** because it's more popular. Same price: €40/adult in June.

**Booking URL:** https://shop.np-krka.hr

### 2. Biokovo Skywalk & Nature Park
**Day 2 (Jun 17) — Makarska**

**Booking:** REQUIRED online at [shop.pp-biokovo.hr](https://shop.pp-biokovo.hr). Only 20 cars allowed per hour on the mountain road. You choose a specific 1-hour entry window and must arrive 5–10 minutes before it. Shows sell out even in shoulder season — book at minimum 1–2 days ahead; 3+ days safer in June.

**Price:** €15/adult for June–September. Children under 7 free. 
- 4 adults = **€60**. (Hiking-only / cyclist rate: €7–10, if you go on foot — not relevant here.)

**Time needed:** 2–3 hours including the Skywalk platform and viewpoints. The road up is scenic but narrow — allow 30 min each way driving.

**Best timing:** First morning slot to beat heat and have the clearest sea views. The Skywalk perch is at ~1,200m so carry a layer.

**Watch out for:**
- You absolutely cannot show up without a reservation in June. Full stop.
- The road up is single-track in places — take it slow.
- Skywalk is a glass-floored platform hanging over the cliff face above Makarska — genuinely spectacular on a clear day.

**Booking URL:** https://shop.pp-biokovo.hr

### 3. Cetina Canyon — Rafting (Omiš)
**Day 2 (Jun 17) or Day 3 (Jun 18 morning)**

**Booking:** Recommended 1–2 days ahead, not strictly required but June fills up fast. Several operators run near-identical trips.

**Operators:**
- **Rafting Cetina / CanyoningCetina** — direct booking, €38/pp: https://canyoning-cetina.com
- **Malduk Adventures** — well-reviewed, ~€40/pp: https://maldukadventures.com
- **Split Rafting** (also Omiš-based): https://split-rafting.eu

**Price:** €38–60/pp depending on platform. Direct booking ~€38–40. Via GetYourGuide/Viator ~€55–60.
- 4 adults direct = **~€150–160**.

**Time needed:** 2.5–3 hours on the water (10 km canyon route). Add 1 hour for logistics/travel. Half-day activity.

**Includes:** Raft, paddle, helmet, lifejacket, guide, parking, activity insurance. Some operators include cliff jumping and cave exploring en route.

**Best timing:** Morning start to avoid afternoon heat. The canyon has natural shade from the limestone walls.

**Watch out for:**
- Multiple operators on the same river — quality is similar, price varies mainly by booking channel. Book direct to save €15–20/pp.
- Jun 18 morning is tight: the Dubrovnik airport pickup is that **evening**. If doing rafting on day 3, start early and keep the drive Omiš→Dubrovnik (2h without stops) in mind.

### 4. Cetina Canyon — Zipline (Omiš)
**Day 2 (Jun 17) or Day 3 (Jun 18 morning)**

**Booking:** "At least 1 day ahead" per the operator; for groups 4+ book several days ahead to get preferred time slot. Can sometimes book same-day if slots open, but don't count on it.

**Operator:** **Red Adventures Croatia** is the primary zipline operator in Omiš.
- Direct booking: https://red-adventures.com/offer/zipline-omis-river-cetina-canyon/

**Price:** €80/group of 2–12 people (not per person — group price). That's **€80 total for 4 pax** if booked together. Add €20 if you want transfer from Split (not needed if you're driving through Omiš).

**Time needed:** 3 hours including gear-up and 8-line polygon. 2 guides per group.

**Includes:** All equipment (helmet, harness, gloves), 2 guides, return transport from their agency to the start platform.

**The course:** 8 ziplines, total 2,100m, longest single line 700m, highest point 150m above the canyon floor. Serious adrenaline.

**Best timing:** Morning or late afternoon to avoid peak heat on the platform.

**Watch out for:**
- You can combine zipline + rafting in one day if you start with zipline (morning) and raft in afternoon, or the reverse. Many visitors do both.
- Weight limits apply (check their site — typically 35–110 kg range).

### 5. Mljet National Park
**After Jun 18 — island block**

**Booking:** Not required, can buy on arrival. Online purchase also available at [parkovihrvatske.hr](https://www.parkovihrvatske.hr/en/web-shop-tickets).

**Price:** €25/adult (2026 tariff confirmed). **After 16:00: 25% discount = ~€19/adult.**
- 4 adults before 16:00 = **€100**. After 16:00 = **~€75**.

**Time needed:** Full day for the park (Great Lake / Small Lake loop, Montokuc hike, St. Mary's Island). Montokuc is ~2h hike up and back with panoramic Adriatic views.

**Best timing:** Arrive by ferry from Prapratno (Pelješac) in the afternoon specifically to hit the after-16:00 discount — saves €25 for the group. Prapratno→Polače ferry is the main route.

**Watch out for:**
- The ferry from Prapratno to Polače (the park's main port) runs several times daily — verify timetable at jadrolinija.hr. Ferry takes ~35 min.
- Entry ticket covers the lakes loop and boat to St. Mary's Island monastery. Montokuc hike is within the NP boundary (ticket covers it).
- June is high season — the lakes paths can be crowded midday. Early morning or evening (with late ticket discount) is better.

### 6. Kravica Waterfalls
**Bosnia leg (Mostar area)**

**Booking:** No advance booking. Walk up and pay.

**Price:** 20 KM/adult = **~€10/pp**. 4 adults = **~€40 cash**. Parking: 3 KM/hour or 6 KM/day = **~€3 for half-day**.
- Note: Local residents pay half price. Tickets collected 08:00–20:00; before/after is technically free access.

**Time needed:** 1.5–2 hours. Swimming in the pool below the falls is allowed and popular.

**Best timing:** Early morning (before 10:00) if possible — it gets very crowded by mid-morning in June. Locals report it's jammed by 11:00 with tour buses from Mostar.

**Watch out for:**
- Cash only (Bosnian Convertible Marks). Keep BAM on hand. €/BAM rate is fixed: 1€ = ~1.955 KM.
- Parking is the same cash situation.
- The falls are beautiful but the site becomes touristy and loud by midday. Come first thing.
- Located 40 km from Mostar — easily combined with Mostar as a morning stop en route to Sarajevo or Sutjeska.

### 7. Tara Canyon Rafting — BOOK URGENTLY
**Montenegro — entering at Šćepan Polje**

This is the most popular adventure activity in Montenegro and fills up weeks ahead in June. **Book now, before you leave on Jun 16.**

**The route:** Standard 1-day trip covers 18–22 km of the Tara canyon from Brstnovica to Šćepan Polje — 2–4 hours on the water, 23 rapids, the world's second deepest canyon (1,300m). June water levels are still high = more powerful rapids vs. September.

**Recommended operators:**

| Operator | Price (1 day) | Includes | Contact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Rafting Centar Drina-Tara** | €60/pp | Breakfast + lunch, guide, equipment, transfers, NP fee | raftingtara.com / office@raftingtara.com / +381 64 420 1956 |
| **TaraSport Rafting** | €50/pp | Rafting; meals not specified | tarasportrafting.com / info@tarasportrafting.com / +387 66 606 306 |
| **Blue River Tara** | €65–75/pp | Full package including meals | bluerivertara.me |
| **Rafting Montenegro (Tara)** | €75/pp | All meals + guide | raftingmontenegro.com |

**Best deal: Rafting Centar Drina-Tara at €60/pp** — well-established, includes full meals and NP entrance, 4 adults = **€240**.

**What's included in a full package:** Guide, all safety equipment (wetsuit, helmet, lifejacket, paddle), breakfast and lunch at riverside restaurant, transfers to/from put-in, National Park entrance fee.

**How far ahead to book:** The Drina-Tara center notes that reservations need advance payment to confirm. Blue River Tara requires booking "one day in advance" minimum but June peak-season days fill weeks ahead. **Book at minimum 1–2 weeks out for June; many operators already have peak summer dates filling up.** Email/WhatsApp directly — faster than web forms.

**Same-day availability:** Unlikely for 1-day trips in June. Some operators run shorter 3-hour packages (€59/pp) which may have more last-minute slots, but don't rely on it.

**The route geography:** The rafting base camps (Šćepan Polje side) are right at the BiH/Montenegro border — you cross into Montenegro here, raft, then continue south toward Plužine and Žabljak. Most operators are based at or near the bridge. You can raft on arrival day before checking into Žabljak.

### 8. Durmitor National Park — Black Lake + Veliki Međed Hike
**Žabljak, Montenegro**

**Booking:** No advance booking needed.

**Price:**
- NP entry: **€5/pp/day**. 4 adults = **€20/day cash**. Annual Parks Pass €13.50 covers all 5 Montenegrin NPs (good value if you're also entering Sutjeska or Biogradska Gora).
- Black Lake parking: **€2–3 cash** at the barrier before the lot.
- Bring cash (euros) — card acceptance is patchy.

**Time needed:**
- Black Lake circuit (easy): 3.5 km loop, ~1 hour, flat boardwalk, very scenic.
- Veliki Međed via Black Lake: 14–16 km, +1,200m elevation, **6–8 hours** full day. This is the group's target peak hike. Start early — summit thunderstorms are common in afternoon.

**Best timing:** Leave Žabljak by 07:00 for the Veliki Međed hike. Pack lunch, water (2L+), layers. The hike is unmarked in places — download trail GPX from Wikiloc or AllTrails before you go (offline map recommended).

**Watch out for:**
- NP entry ticket is checked on the road approaching Black Lake — pay at the booth, keep the stub.
- Parking fills by 09:00–10:00 in high season. Arrive before or park in town and walk 20 min to the lake.
- The hike to Veliki Međed (2,437m) involves some route-finding. No technical climbing but steep terrain. Proper shoes essential.

### 9. Ostrog Monastery
**Montenegro (between Šćepan Polje and Skadar Lake)**

**Booking:** No booking, no ticket, completely free.

**Price:** FREE.

**Time needed:** 1.5–2 hours. The Upper Monastery (carved into the cliff face) is the main draw. Parking is at the Lower Monastery (free), then either walk the path up (~30 min) or take a shared mini-bus shuttle.

**Dress code — strictly enforced:**
- Cover knees and shoulders (both men and women).
- No shorts or sleeveless tops. If you arrive underprepared, wraps are usually available at the entrance.
- No photography inside the prayer rooms and reliquary.

**Hours:** May–September 06:00–17:00.

**Best timing:** Early morning (before 10:00) to avoid pilgrimage crowds — this is one of the most visited religious sites in the Balkans. Gets very busy by midday. The road up is winding and narrow; give way to downhill traffic.

**Watch out for:**
- The road to the monastery is one-lane and steep — fine for an SUV but requires patience.
- Genuinely sacred active monastery. Behave accordingly regardless of your own beliefs.
- There's a donation box — a small donation is appropriate.

### 10. Skadar Lake — Kayak / Boat
**Montenegro (Virpazar or Rijeka Crnojevića)**

**Booking:** Recommended 1–2 days ahead, especially in peak season. Most operators in Virpazar (the main base town) are small local outfits reachable by phone/WhatsApp.

**Operators:**
- **Falcon Boat** (Virpazar): €10/hour or €40/full day per kayak. [boatfalcon.com](http://boatfalcon.com)
- **Flora & Fauna Boats** (Virpazar): €30/day per kayak. [skadarlakewildlife.com](https://skadarlakewildlife.com)
- **Eagle Boats** (Virpazar): boat tours from €15/pp. [skadarlakeboat.com](https://skadarlakeboat.com)
- **Veronika's Adventure**: kayak + SUP. [veronikasadventure.com](https://veronikasadventure.com)

**Price:** Renting 2 double kayaks for a full day = ~€60–80. Guided boat tour = €15–20/pp.

**Time needed:** Half to full day. The lake is 48 km long — paddle the northern bays around Virpazar for pelicans, cormorants, and water lilies. Wine and fish lunch at a lakeshore konoba is the standard pairing.

**Best timing:** Early morning for wildlife (pelicans feed at dawn). Afternoon can be windy. June water levels are still good.

**Watch out for:**
- Skadar Lake is a national park (Montenegro side) — fishing requires a permit but kayaking does not.
- Virpazar is a small village: book ahead or arrive early to secure equipment.
- The optional "deep south" extension (Bar/Ulcinj/Ada Bojana) adds ~2h but is separate from Skadar — don't conflate the two.

### 11. Kotor Fortress Walls (San Giovanni)
**Kotor, Montenegro**

**Booking:** No advance booking needed. Buy at the gate or online.

**Price:** €15/pp. Children under 12 free.
- 4 adults = **€60**.
- Payment: cash (euros) OR card. Cash strongly preferred at the kiosk; card reliability is inconsistent.
- Early morning before 07:00 is technically free (gates often unmanned), but the official ticket booth opens at 07:00.

**Time needed:** 45–60 min to the top. 2–2.5 hours round trip with photography. 1,350 limestone steps.

**Best timing:** Arrive at or before 07:00 for sunrise and cooler temperatures. The climb in midday June heat is brutal and the white limestone reflects heat intensely. Sunset is also excellent (second-best option).

**Watch out for:**
- Steps are worn smooth and **very slippery** — proper shoes, not sandals.
- The upper fortress has no shade. Carry water.
- Open 07:00–20:00 (closes at 22:00 in some sources — check on arrival). Do not get caught above the lock-up time.
- Part of the lower walls is accessible free of charge; the full climb to the fortress requires the ticket.

### 13. Sutjeska National Park + Perućica
**Bosnia (en route to Šćepan Polje)**

**Booking:** Walk-up OK for the main park. Perućica primeval forest **requires a guide — book in advance**.

**Price:** ~10 KM (~€5)/pp for NP entry. Guide fee for Perućica is a flat group rate — call to confirm.

**Perućica rule:** Access to the primeval forest is ONLY allowed with an NP-licensed guide. You cannot self-hike it. Book via the park's information center or by calling:
- **+387 58 233-130** (Reservations, Sutjeska NP)
- Or email through [sutjeskanp.com](https://sutjeskanp.com)

**Time needed:** Sutjeska without Perućica (Skakavac waterfall hike or Maglić base approach): 3–4 hours. With Perućica guided walk: add 3–4 hours (full day).

**Best timing:** If including Perućica, call at least a week ahead to arrange a guide for your arrival day. The forest has timed group entries to protect the ecosystem.

**Watch out for:**
- The road through Sutjeska (Tjentište canyon) is spectacularly scenic even as a drive-through — minimum 2 hours even if you don't hike.
- Sutjeska is on the route between Mostar/Foča and Šćepan Polje — a natural stop.

## Hidden costs and fees you might miss

### Road tolls (Croatia)
- **Croatian motorway (A1):** Pay-as-you-go, ~€6–8/100 km. No vignette. 
  - Zadar → Split section: ~€5–7.
  - Split → Dubrovnik (via toll road, Pelješac bypass): ~€10–15 for the full southern stretch.
- **Pelješac Bridge:** FREE. No toll.
- Summer surcharge (Jun–Sep): tolls rise ~20% vs. off-season.
- **Učka tunnel** (north HR, irrelevant to this route): mention only to say we're not going there.

### Montenegro motorway
- **A1 Montenegro (Smokovac–Mateševo):** Toll applies only on this 42 km stretch. ~€3–5 for a passenger car.
- No vignette required elsewhere in Montenegro.

### National park day passes — Montenegro bundle
- Montenegro Parks Pass: **€13.50** gives access to all 5 national parks. If you'll hit Durmitor + Sutjeska (Bosnian but nearby) + potentially Biogradska Gora: individual day tickets = €5/pp × 2+ parks × 4 people can add up. The Parks Pass is worth it if you're entering 3+ Montenegro parks. **Buy online at cozymontenegro.com/parks or tourist info in Žabljak.**

### Rental car — cross-border
- **Sicily By Car cross-border fee:** Verify before pickup (see rental-car.md). Some suppliers charge **€50/country/trip** for BiH + Montenegro. Get WRITTEN confirmation. If not resolved before Jun 16 pickup, confirm at the Zadar desk and pay any fee on the spot.
- **Green Card:** Should be included automatically — confirm at pickup. It's the international insurance certificate, required at both BiH and Montenegro borders.
- **Borders with no fees:** The Šćepan Polje (BiH→ME) crossing is free. No border tax. Just passport stamps.

### Ston town walls (Pelješac)
- You'll pass Ston on the Pelješac peninsula (Dubrovnik → Mljet route). The medieval salt-pan town walls charge a small entry: ~€5–8/pp if you want to walk them. Optional stop — beautiful but easily skipped.

### Croatian tourist/city tax
- Standard overnight tax: ~€1.50–3.00/pp/night in Croatia. Usually collected by accommodation hosts. Budget ~€15–20/pp for the Croatian nights (5–6 nights).
- Bosnia and Montenegro have similar small taxes at accommodation (~1–2 KM or €0.50–1/pp/night). Mostly cash-only at small guesthouses.

### Ferry costs (Croatia islands)
- **Zadar → Ugljan/Pašman area:** Small car ferries, ~€15–25 for car + 4 passengers one-way.
- **Prapratno → Mljet (Polače):** Car ferry, ~€30–40 for car + 4 passengers one-way. Timetable at jadrolinija.hr. **This ferry is the key one if doing Mljet — check schedule in advance and consider arriving at Prapratno with time buffer.**
- Jadrolinija ferries to major islands (Hvar, Brač) are a separate question per route scenarios.

### Bosnia entry: no fees
- No visa, no border fee for EU/Schengen passport holders. Just a passport stamp (ID cards are NOT accepted at BiH borders — passports only).

### Parking
- Dubrovnik Old Town parking: **€3–5/hour** near the walls. Brutal in summer. Use the Park & Ride at Ilijina Glavica (€1/day, free shuttle) if staying in DBV.
- Sarajevo: street parking 1–2 KM/hour in city center.
- Kotor: The old town has parking outside the walls — arrive early, it fills by 09:00. Off-season fee ~€1–2/hour, peak season can be higher.
