## 2. Car Reservation — Queue and Risk

**No car reservation is possible** on this route. Jadrolinija explicitly states this is a first-come, first-served service. Buying a ticket online in advance does not hold a vehicle spot — the ticket is valid for any departure on the date printed, subject to capacity. You must still queue at the port.

### How early to arrive:
- Official guidance: "at least 60 minutes before departure in the summer season" (Jadrolinija FAQ and travel guides).
- Forum consensus: **arrive 60–90 minutes before your target sailing** on a summer Saturday. One source states: "if you come 90 minutes before departure, you should be able to secure a place."
- The ferry is described as "large" and multiple sources note it's "very unlikely" to be turned away entirely — a worst case is waiting for the next sailing (3 hours later on this schedule).

### Risk assessment for Jun 20 (Saturday, early-June shoulder):
- Moderate risk of missing target sailing if you arrive less than 45 min early.
- Low risk of being turned away entirely (next sailing available same ticket).
- **Recommended strategy:** Target the **10:15 sailing**, arrive at Prapratno by **08:45–09:00** (90 min early). If bumped, the 13:00 sailing still gives a functional afternoon. Avoid the 07:00 unless you're willing to overnight at the port area or leave Dubrovnik at 05:00.

Sources: [croatiaferries.com forum](https://www.croatiaferries.com/topic/advance-booking-for-prapratno-sobra-ferry/), [croatiatraveller.com](https://www.croatiatraveller.com/Local-Ferries/Prapratno-Sobra.html), [Jadrolinija FAQ](https://www.jadrolinija.hr/en/faq)

## 3. Mljet NP Entry — 2026 Prices

### Price discrepancy: €25 vs €30

Two official Croatian sources give different figures for high-season adult admission:

- **np-mljet.hr official price list (PDF published Nov 2025):** €25/adult in high season (Jun–Sep), confirmed by absolute-croatia.com aggregator.
- **parkovihrvatske.hr online ticketing shop:** Lists €30/adult for Jun 1–Sep 30, 2026.

**Working assumption: €25 at the gate / €30 online.** The discrepancy likely reflects an online booking convenience surcharge (common on parkovihrvatske.hr) or a price increase phased in for 2026. Verify on arrival — but budget for €30pp to be safe.

### What's included in the ticket:
- One **return boat trip to St. Mary's Island** (Benedictine monastery on a small islet in Veliko Jezero)
- Access to all marked walking and cycling trails around both lakes (Malo and Veliko Jezero)
- Parking at Vrbovica
- Visitor insurance
- Electric train transport (seasonal schedule)
- Additional return boat trips to St. Mary cost €5 extra

### Late-entry discount:
**25% off** for adults, students, and children (ages 7–18) when tickets are purchased **after 16:00** at the park. Good option if arriving late — costs ~€18.75/adult instead of €25.

### Group cost estimate (4 adults, gate price):
€25 × 4 = **€100** (or €75 with 16:00 discount). Online: €30 × 4 = **€120**.

### Can you buy online?
Yes — parkovihrvatske.hr sells advance tickets. Worth buying online the day before to skip the gate queue, even if slightly pricier.

Sources: [np-mljet.hr price list](https://np-mljet.hr/price-list/?lang=en), [np-mljet.hr PDF 2026](https://np-mljet.hr/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/ENG-Cjenik-ulaznica-za-posjetitelje-za-2026.g.pdf), [parkovihrvatske.hr](https://www.parkovihrvatske.hr/en/web-shop-tickets?parkId=486), [absolute-croatia.com](https://www.absolute-croatia.com/mljet/parks-beaches/mljet-national-park)

## 4. Camping — Autokamp Lovor (= Autocamp Mljet)

**Yes, same place.** The campsite is officially called **Auto-camp "Lovor"** and also markets itself as **Autocamp Mljet** (website: autocamp-mljet.com). Located in **Kozarica village**, 15 km from Sobra ferry port on the northern side of the island, on the edge of the NP.

### 2026 Pricing (from official website, high season 15 Jun – 15 Sep):
- Adult: €15/person/night
- Tent pitch: €12/night
- **4 adults + 1 tent: €60 + €12 = €72/night** (plus ~€1.30/person tourism tax = ~€77 total)
- Extra: electricity €7, washing machine €7, fridge €2

The €72 figure in the research digest is **confirmed correct** (matches site pricing exactly).

### Facilities:
- Hot showers, toilets, kitchen, electricity hookup
- 30 pitches, primarily tent-focused (not suitable for motorhomes over 6 m)
- Friendly owner (Ana), very clean per park4night reviews
- Beach/swimming nearby
- Access road: 3 km narrow, unpaved — fine for a standard rental car

### Advance booking:
Reservation form exists on autocamp-mljet.com. Call or email to confirm. Park4night gives it **4.31/5** (26 reviews). Contact: +385 98 702 200.

### Other camping on Mljet:
- **Camp Marina Mljet** (also in Babino Polje area) — listed on hrcamping.net but fewer reviews and facilities details. Less popular than Lovor.
- Wild/informal camping on the NP boundary is theoretically possible but the NP is protected — stick to the official campsite.

Sources: [autocamp-mljet.com](https://www.autocamp-mljet.com/?lang=eng), [park4night](https://park4night.com/en/place/137639), [campingcroatia.net](https://www.campingcroatia.net/campsite-lovor-kozarica-mljet-island/), [hrcamping.net](https://www.hrcamping.net/croatia-dubrovnik-neretva-county/dalmatia/174-camp-marina-mljet-babino-polje)

## 5. Odysseus Cave — Access and Cliff Jumping

### Location:
The cave is on the western tip of the island, outside (but near) the NP boundary, accessible from the road near **Babino Polje village**. Look for Tommy Supermarket on the main highway — the trailhead is a few minutes' walk from there.

### Land access (confirmed):
- **Yes, accessible by land.** Hike from the road: ~1.2–2.6 km round trip depending on source; approximately **25–30 minutes one way** (downhill going, uphill return).
- Trail is described as rocky, steep in places, with loose gravel — moderately challenging, not technical.
- The trail ends at the clifftop/cave entrance at sea level. You must jump or swim in; there is no dry walkway inside.
- A metal rod with rope allows exit from the water.

### Cliff jumping:
- **Multiple jumping spots**, described as "beginner to intermediate" difficulty.
- Not a high cliff — described as significant enough to require a forceful entry but not extreme.
- **Unregulated, no lifeguards.** No official prohibition found, but no official permission either. Jump at own risk.
- Swimming ability essential — strong current possible.

### Boat access:
- Commercial tour operators (from Korčula, Dubrovnik) also bring you here by private yacht — not necessary if you're on the island with a car.

**Verdict:** Fully doable on foot from a car parked near Tommy's. Allow 1–1.5 hours round trip including swim/jump time. Best combined with the NP visit as an add-on (it's outside the paid NP zone — no extra entry fee).

Sources: [tayloronatrip.com](https://tayloronatrip.com/odysseus-cave-mljet-island-croatia), [awellreadwanderer.com](https://www.awellreadwanderer.com/odysseus-cave-mljet/), [mljet.hr tourist board](https://www.mljet.hr/?u=odysseus-cave%2Fen%2Fst%2F97%2F121)

## 7. Is Mljet Worth It for This Group?

### The case for YES:
- **Kayaking the saltwater lakes is genuinely excellent for active travelers** — turquoise, calm, warm water with cliffs and forest. Multiple independent sources agree it's one of Croatia's most beautiful NPs.
- **Less crowded than Hvar/Dubrovnik** — it's an island requiring a car ferry, which filters out day-trippers who don't commit.
- **Odysseus Cave adds adventure** — cliff jumping, swimming into a sea cave, no fee, no crowds compared to the NP lakes.
- **Camping at Lovor is ideal** — €77/night for 4 is cheap, great facilities, forested/coastal setting, owner is praised.
- The NP is very swimmable — the lakes are warmer than the open sea and crystal clear.

### The case for caution:
- The NP experience is primarily **walking, cycling, kayaking, swimming** — it's beautiful but not adrenaline-heavy. Less so than rafting at Konjic or hiking Durmitor.
- "Hordes of tourists" cited in summer TripAdvisor reviews on peak weekends. Jun 20 is before the Jul–Aug peak — should be manageable.
- The €25–30 pp NP fee feels steep for what is essentially a walking/kayaking park. One reviewer: "not exactly cheap."
- **From Dubrovnik, total cost per person for a day trip:** €12.50 ferry × 2 + €25–30 NP = **€50–55 per person / €200–220 for 4**, excluding kayak rental and food.

### Community verdict:
- Best framing for this group: "Not a party island — go for the water, the cave, the quiet." If the group wants beaches+adventure, Mljet delivers on both. If they want nightlife or pure adrenaline, skip.
- Best use: **overnight** (not day trip). The overnight option unlocks a relaxed pace, a genuine campsite, a sunset over the lakes, and a morning swim in uncrowded conditions before the next day's ferries.
- One 2024 traveler: kayaking Mljet was "the best idea possible" for combining water, swimming, and snorkeling in a single morning.
- TripAdvisor consensus 2024–2025: "Worth visiting, not only for one day" — but acknowledge crowds and high restaurant prices.

### Verdict for this trip:
**Worthwhile, especially as an overnight.** The day is beautiful, the cost is real (€200+ for group), but this is the most accessible Croatian NP island experience available on this route — Mljet is right on the way between Dubrovnik and Bosnia. Skipping it to save €200 would be a miss given the group's interest in nature and active water sports. The cave is a genuine adventure bonus.
