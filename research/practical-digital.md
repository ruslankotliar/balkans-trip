## Part 1: Mobile Data and Phone Plans

### 1.1 What roaming actually costs on Austrian plans

Bosnia & Herzegovina and Montenegro are **not EU**, so "Roam Like at Home" does **not** apply. The Western Balkans do have a separate roaming agreement (WB6, signed 2021, phasing down costs toward 2028) but it is a different, more expensive ceiling than EU rules.

**A1 Austria**
- Bosnia and Montenegro fall under **Zone 1** of A1's Roaming Pass system.
- Rate: **€4.90 per 1 GB** of data used. Data is billed in 1 GB blocks.
- Also available: "A1 Roaming Datenwoche" — **€4.90 for 2 GB, valid 7 days** — a better deal if you activate it before crossing.
- Default safety limit: €60 (cuts data once reached).
- Source: [a1.net roaming tariffs](https://www.a1.net/handys-tarife/zusatzoptionen/roaming-tarife)

**Magenta Austria (T-Mobile Austria)**
- Under the EU–Western Balkans agreement, the **price ceiling** per GB is:
  - **€14/GB** from 2026 onward (down from €18/GB since Oct 2023, will drop to €9/GB in 2028)
- Magenta offers "Travel & Surf Balkan" passes covering Albania, Bosnia, Kosovo, Montenegro, North Macedonia, Serbia, Ukraine, Moldova.
- Also: "Internet Bosnien/Serbien/Türkei" monthly add-on — 5 GB for 1 month.
- Source: [Magenta newsroom](https://newsroom.magenta.at/2023/05/05/eu-und-westbalkan-senken-roamingkosten/), [magenta.at reisepakete](https://www.magenta.at/handytarife/zusatzpakete/reisepakete)

**Drei Austria**
- Without any package: **€15.00 per MB** (yes, per megabyte — this is the punishing default Zone 3 rate). Using data without a Drei roaming pack in Bosnia or Montenegro would cost ~€15,000/GB at the default rate.
- With a "Go" pack added: **€5 for 1 GB, valid 15 days** in Bosnia or Montenegro. This is the option to use.
- Source: [drei.at roaming with contract](https://www.drei.at/de/info/roaming/roaming-mit-vertrag/), [drei.at roaming tickets](https://www.drei.at/de/info/roaming/roamingtickets/)

**Bottom line for the group:**
- A1: activate the **Datenwoche add-on** (~€4.90/2 GB/7 days) before entering Bosnia. Cheap and enough for a few days of navigation.
- Magenta: activate the **Travel & Surf Balkan** package before crossing — check current price on magenta.at, ceiling is €14/GB in 2026 but their package price may be lower.
- Drei: **CRITICAL** — do NOT use data without activating the "Go" pack first (€5/1 GB). The default rate at €15/MB would be catastrophic.
- All three: local SIM cards are still cheaper and give vastly more data (see below). For 4 people, 1–2 local SIMs are probably the best strategy.

### 1.2 Local SIM cards in Bosnia & Herzegovina

**Three carriers** operate in BiH: BH Telecom (best coverage), HT Eronet (cheaper, weaker coverage), m:tel (decent coverage).

**BH Telecom — recommended for coverage:**
- Ultra Tourist 1: **20 BAM (~€10)** → 15 GB data + 5 BAM credit, valid **10 days**
- Ultra Tourist 2: **40 BAM (~€20)** → 30 GB data, valid **30 days**
- Ultra Tourist Group (4 people): **100 BAM (~€51)** → 100 GB, valid **15 days** — designed exactly for groups

**HT Eronet:**
- !hej Tourist: **20 BAM (~€10)** → 20 GB + 30 local minutes, valid **30 days** — good value

**m:tel Bosnia:**
- Standard packs: 4–10 BAM for 4–10 GB, 7–15 day validity (no specific tourist pack, just top up a standard SIM)

**Registration:** No SIM registration required in Bosnia. Anyone can buy freely — no passport needed.

**Where to buy in Sarajevo:**
- BH Telecom has a store **at Sarajevo Airport** (ground floor, car building, Terminal B) — buy on arrival
- City center: BH Telecom and HT Eronet shops near Baščaršija, plus convenience stores, newsstands, petrol stations, and post offices throughout the city.
- Avoid airport-adjacent newsstands — prices are inflated there; the carrier stores give proper tourist packs.

**Practical tip:** The BH Telecom Ultra Tourist Group pack (100 GB for ~€51 split 4 ways = ~€13/person) is a strong option if you want one shared hotspot phone. Alternatively, one person buys a 20 BAM Tourist SIM and everyone hotspots off it.

Sources: [phonetravelwiz.com BiH guide](https://www.phonetravelwiz.com/buying-a-sim-card-in-bosnia-and-herzegovina-guide/), [monito.com BiH SIM cards](https://www.monito.com/en/best-prepaid-sim-card/bosnia-and-herzegovina)

### 1.3 Local SIM cards in Montenegro

**Three carriers:** m:tel Montenegro (best rural coverage), ONE (formerly Telenor), Crnogorski Telekom (T-Mobile ME).

**Tourist packages — all carriers are nearly identical:**
- 15-day plan: **€10–15** → 200–500 GB data
- 30-day plan: **€15–20** → 500 GB – 1 TB data

**m:tel Montenegro** is specifically recommended for rural areas (Durmitor, Piva canyon, inland roads) — other carriers have weaker signal outside coastal towns.

**Crnogorski Telekom (T-Mobile ME):** €15 / 500 GB for 15 days, also includes 7 GB for use in other Balkan countries.

**Registration:** Passport or ID required — mandatory by Montenegrin law.

**Where to buy:**
- **Airport:** All three carriers have desks at both **Tivat Airport (TIV)** and Podgorica (TGD), open during flight arrivals. Tivat is the closest airport to Budva/Kotor — grab one there.
- **Budva and Kotor:** Carrier stores in city centers. Budva also has **beach kiosks in summer** that sell tourist SIMs.
- Also available: gas stations, tobacco shops, kiosks throughout coastal towns.

**Strategy for this trip:** Buy one m:tel tourist SIM on arrival in Montenegro (either at Tivat airport or Budva/Kotor city center). The €15/500 GB pack lasts well beyond any trip duration. Use as hotspot for the group for inland areas.

Sources: [montenegrodigitalnomad.com SIM guide](https://montenegrodigitalnomad.com/montenegro-blog/how-to-buy-simcard-montenegro/), [phonetravelwiz.com Montenegro](https://www.phonetravelwiz.com/buying-a-sim-card-in-montenegro-guide/)

### 1.4 Wifi-only + offline maps strategy

**Is wifi-only viable?** Partially viable but not reliable enough to be the sole strategy:
- Guesthouses in Žabljak (Durmitor Magic, Durmitor Paradise, Ukus Durmitora) all advertise free wifi — adequate for planning the next day but not for real-time turn-by-turn on mountain roads.
- Skadar Lake: Guesthouse Skadar Lake (Virpazar) has wifi; small auto-camp and some guesthouses offer wifi but not all.
- Campsites in rural Bosnia: wifi is spotty or absent at smaller sites. Sutjeska NP: no reliable wifi outside the main camp.
- **Rural roads** (Piva canyon, Šćepan Polje, Durmitor plateau): zero cellular signal, let alone wifi.

**Verdict:** Download everything offline before entering rural areas. Wifi is fine for evening logistics, not for navigation.

### 1.5 Offline maps: Google Maps vs OsmAnd vs Maps.me

**Google Maps offline:**
- Supports downloadable offline regions for both Bosnia and Montenegro — works well for major roads and towns.
- Gap: some rural mountain roads and unmarked tracks are missing or outdated; limited hiking trail data.
- Turn-by-turn works offline — good for standard road navigation.
- Can download the **entire Montenegro map** and **BiH map** as single regions. Do this before leaving Croatia.

**OsmAnd (recommended for this trip):**
- Built on OpenStreetMap — generally has more detail on minor roads, mountain tracks, and trails than Google Maps in this region.
- Offline map files for Bosnia and Montenegro are downloadable (free tier: 7 maps, paid removes limit).
- Includes: elevation data, hiking trails, national park boundaries, campsites, springs, mountain huts — all useful for Durmitor.
- Preferred by overlanders and hikers for Balkans specifically.
- Slightly steeper learning curve than Google Maps.

**Maps.me:**
- Also OSM-based. Simpler than OsmAnd. Reasonable offline navigation. Less actively maintained than OsmAnd in recent years; OsmAnd is the stronger pick.

**Recommendation:** Use **Google Maps offline** as primary for road nav (familiar interface, good coverage on major routes); have **OsmAnd** installed with Bosnia + Montenegro maps downloaded as backup for rural roads, unmarked tracks, and hiking. Both are free to install.

Sources: [montenegropulse.com maps guide](https://www.montenegropulse.com/montenegro-map.html), [pindropapps.com OsmAnd review](https://pindropapps.com/osmand-maps-review/)

### 1.6 Google Maps reliability on rural roads

- Major roads (M-roads in BiH, magistrale in Montenegro): Google Maps is accurate and reliable.
- Rural mountain roads (Piva canyon D-road, Durmitor plateau roads, Šćepan Polje approach): Google Maps has some gaps — OSM-based apps handle these better.
- Missing roads in BiH: there are known issues (Google Maps Community reports confirm some roads are missing or outdated, particularly in rural RS/FBiH areas).
- **Street View is not available** in Bosnia & Herzegovina — no photographic confirmation of road conditions.
- Montenegro: better Google Maps coverage than BiH, but still not comprehensive off main roads.

**Action:** Download OsmAnd with full BiH and Montenegro maps before departure. Use for Piva Canyon, Durmitor, and any rough-road routing. Google Maps offline for main route navigation.

## Part 2: Cash and ATMs

### 2.1 Bosnia & Herzegovina — BAM/KM

**Currency:** Bosnian Convertible Mark (BAM/KM). Fixed exchange rate: **€1 = 1.955 KM** (permanent peg to Euro via currency board). This rate never changes — you always know exactly what you're paying.

**ATM basics:**
- ATMs dispense KM only.
- Typical transaction limits: **400–1,000 KM per withdrawal** (some up to 4,000 KM).
- Always select **"Decline Conversion"** / pay in local currency at ATMs — never let the ATM do the conversion.
- Fee-free ATMs for foreign cards: **MF Bank, BPS Banka, Raiffeisen Bank**. Avoid ProCredit and UniCredit (fees up to 18 KM per transaction ~€9).

**Mostar ATMs:**
- Multiple ATMs in Mostar Old Town / Stari Grad area, near Stari Most (Old Bridge).
- Raiffeisen and UniCredit branches in the city center.
- **Withdraw here before heading to Kravica or inland** — reliable and multiple options.

**Kravica Waterfall area:**
- **No ATM on-site**.
- Nearest reliable ATMs: **Čapljina** (~12 km) or **Ljubuški** (~10 km) — both have multiple bank ATMs.
- Studenci village (5 km): one small ATM, often offline — don't rely on it.
- Entry fee at Kravica gate: cards officially accepted but reader is unreliable → **bring cash (KM or EUR)**.
- Parking and kayak rental: **cash only**.
- Recommended: withdraw KM in Mostar Old Town before visiting Kravica.

Sources: [exploremostar.com Kravica payment guide](https://exploremostar.com/kravica-waterfall-payment-guide/), [atmfeesaver.com Bosnia](https://atmfeesaver.com/cash-atms-in-bosnia-and-herzegovina/)

### 2.2 Montenegro — Euros

**Currency:** Euro (EUR). No conversion needed.

**ATM fees:** Most Montenegrin ATMs charge **€4.99–€7 per transaction** for foreign cards. Fee-free options:
- **Ziraat Bank** (Turkish bank, bright red branding) — €0 fee. Branches in Podgorica, Budva, Bar.
- **Universal Capital Bank (UCB)** — €0 fee, gold/black branding.
- Avoid **Euronet** ATMs (found in tourist squares) — worst rates and highest fees.

**Typical withdrawal limit:** €500–€800 per transaction. No daily limit preventing multiple transactions.

**Žabljak specifically:**
- Žabljak town has ATMs (confirmed locations: Vuka Karadžića bb, Narodnih heroja 1, Trg durmitorskih ratnika 1).
- These are basic town bank ATMs — **not Ziraat/UCB**, so likely charge the €5 fee.
- Mountain areas within Durmitor NP: no ATMs.
- **Strongly recommended:** withdraw sufficient cash (€150–200 per person) in Podgorica, Budva, or Kotor before heading to Durmitor. ATMs exist in Žabljak but are limited; getting stranded cashless on a mountain is a real risk.

Sources: [atmfeesaver.com Montenegro](https://atmfeesaver.com/cash-atms-in-montenegro/), [adriaticways.com Montenegro currency](https://adriaticways.com/montenegro-currency-guide/)

### 2.4 Border crossings — fees and requirements

**No entry fees** at Croatia→Bosnia or Croatia→Montenegro borders for EU/Austrian passport holders.

**Bosnia border formalities:**
- Austrian passports: visa-free entry for up to 90 days.
- Border police may ask for **proof of funds**: legally 150 KM per intended day of stay. In practice rarely enforced for Western Europeans, but have a card ready to show.
- **Green Card (insurance card)** required for rental cars. Confirm with the rental company that the Green Card covers BiH and Montenegro — most Croatian rental companies include it for the Western Balkans, but verify.

**Montenegro border:**
- Austrian passports: visa-free up to 90 days.
- No fee.

**EES (EU Entry/Exit System), active since April 10, 2026:**
- Applies at **Croatian** borders (Schengen external border) for re-entry into Croatia from BiH or Montenegro.
- Non-EU/EEA nationals: biometric registration on first Schengen entry — add 10–15 min at Croatian re-entry crossing.
- Austrian citizens: not affected (EU passport = no EES registration).
- Other group members: check nationality. If any non-EU in the group, expect longer re-entry queues.

**Best crossings for this route:**
- Croatia → Bosnia (coastal): **Kamensko/Bijača** crossing near Neum — typically under 15 min wait.
- Bosnia → Montenegro (inland): **Šćepan Polje** — remote, rarely crowded.
- Montenegro → Croatia (final return to Dubrovnik): **Karasovići** — can be 2–3 hours in peak summer afternoon. Cross before 8am or after 9pm.

Sources: [easybalkantransfers.com border crossings 2026](https://easybalkantransfers.com/blog/border-crossings-balkans/), [schengentraveler.com BiH entry](https://schengentraveler.com/travel-bosnia-and-herzegovina/)

### 2.5 Specific cash withdrawal recommendations

These are per-stop recommendations based on what's confirmed cash-only or ATM-scarce ahead:

**Before leaving Croatia / in Dubrovnik:**
- Ensure everyone has their Austrian/European debit cards working internationally. Test at a Croatian ATM.

**Mostar (Bosnia, first major stop):**
- Withdraw **200–300 KM** (~€100–150) per couple from a **Raiffeisen or MF Bank** ATM in the city center.
- This covers: Kravica entry + kayaks (~50–70 KM), local restaurants and konobas for 2–3 days, small campsites, Sutjeska entry.
- KM is also accepted at major Mostar tourist spots in EUR, but smaller vendors and rural areas strongly prefer KM.

**Before Sutjeska / heading into Montenegro:**
- Top up to ~150 KM per couple if you've been spending. Konjic and Foča have ATMs.

**Podgorica or Budva (Montenegro):**
- Withdraw **€200–300 per couple** at a **Ziraat Bank ATM** (fee-free) if possible, or UCB.
- This covers: Durmitor NP entry (€5/person/day × 2–3 days), mountain hut meals, campsite fees, Ostrog donation/gift shop, inland konobas, Skadar Lake boat trips.

**Kotor:**
- If you've spent down, top up here — good ATM selection including Ziraat.
- Carry €50–100 in smaller bills for the final Dubrovnik re-entry stretch.

## Part 3: Other Practical Digital Prep

### 3.1 Google Maps reliability — summary verdict

- **Reliable on:** major roads, city navigation, Mostar, Sarajevo, Budva, Kotor, Podgorica.
- **Unreliable or incomplete on:** rural mountain roads in BiH (some missing), Piva Canyon approach roads, unmarked forest/national park tracks, Durmitor plateau roads.
- **No Street View in Bosnia** — can't verify road condition visually before driving.
- **OsmAnd is better for mountain/off-road routing** — use it for Šćepan Polje → Piva → Žabljak and any dirt-track approaches.

### 3.2 Offline map downloads — pre-trip checklist

Download all of these **before leaving Austria/Vienna**, on home wifi (files are large):

| App | What to download | Approx size |
|-----|-----------------|-------------|
| Google Maps | Croatia (if not already), Bosnia & Herzegovina, Montenegro | ~200–400 MB each |
| OsmAnd | Bosnia & Herzegovina full map, Montenegro full map | ~150–300 MB each |
| OsmAnd | (Optional) hiking layers for Durmitor and Sutjeska | Included in country map |

**Additional useful tool:** Download **park4night** app with offline data for BiH and Montenegro — it covers wild camp spots and campsites that neither Google nor OsmAnd index well.

### 3.3 Border crossing live cameras and wait times

**For Croatia border crossings:**
- **HAK (Croatian Automobile Club):** [hak.hr](https://www.hak.hr) — live webcam feeds at Croatian border crossings. Browser-based, no app needed.
- **BorderAlarm.com** — crowdsourced real-time wait times, covers Croatia–Bosnia crossings (e.g., Stara Gradiška). Free, browser-based.

**For Montenegro border crossings:**
- **kamere.mup.gov.me** — Montenegro Ministry of Interior webcams covering all 9 border crossings, refreshing every 40 seconds. Browser-based, works on mobile. Occasional glitches; refresh if feed appears frozen.

**"Granični prijelazi" app (Croatia):** This is the HAK app/tool, functionally covered by hak.hr. For this trip's crossings (Croatia→Bosnia at Kamensko, BiH→Montenegro at Šćepan Polje, Montenegro→Croatia at Karasovići), the **Montenegro MUP webcam portal** is the most useful live tool.

**Practical tip:** Peak summer crossing at Karasovići (Montenegro→Croatia) can be **2–3 hours** on a busy afternoon in July. Check the webcam ~30 min before you intend to leave and adjust departure time accordingly. Cross before 8am or after 9pm to avoid waits.

Sources: [montenegrodigitalnomad.com border webcams](https://montenegrodigitalnomad.com/montenegro-blog/montenegro-border-webcams/), [borderalarm.com](https://borderalarm.com/), [easybalkantransfers.com 2026 border guide](https://easybalkantransfers.com/blog/border-crossings-balkans/)
