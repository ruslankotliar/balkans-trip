## FINDING 3: The Tara Rafting / Durmitor routing logic is backwards

⚠️ REAL PROBLEM

The route skeleton has:
- Day 8 (Jun 23): Sutjeska → cross **Šćepan Polje** → Piva canyon → **Žabljak**
- Day 10 (Jun 25): Tara rafting → Đurđevića bridge → south via Ostrog

But `me-deep2.md` §2 confirms: **"The Brštanovica → Šćepan Polje section is the standard 1-day rafting route."** Šćepan Polje is the END point of the rafting run, and it is also the Bosnia/Montenegro border crossing the group uses on Day 8.

This means:
- On Day 8, the group drives past the Tara rafting put-in point at Brštanovica and the take-out point at Šćepan Polje.
- On Day 10, the plan has them driving back ~2h from Žabljak to raft *the same canyon they literally just drove through on Day 8* — then continuing south past Ostrog to Skadar Lake.

**The logical alternative: Do Tara rafting on Day 8 (crossing day)** — arrive at the Brštanovica rafting camp in the morning from Foča, raft Brštanovica → Šćepan Polje (the standard 18 km route, 3–4 hours on water in June), cross the border at Šćepan Polje as the natural exit, then drive the Piva canyon to Žabljak. The rafting operators are based right at the border area. `me-deep2.md` §2 confirms: "The rafting base camps (Šćepan Polje side) are right at the BiH/Montenegro border — you cross into Montenegro here, raft, then continue south toward Plužine and Žabljak. Most operators are based at or near the bridge. You can raft on arrival day before checking into Žabljak."

**What this buys:** Day 10 (currently "Tara rafting") is freed up entirely. This could become: a second Durmitor hike day, a rest day, or used to do Sutjeska properly (if Perućica is booked for an earlier date). Currently the plan gives Durmitor only 1 hiking day (Jun 24) but the group expressed a wish for the serious Veliki Međed route which `me-deep2.md` calls "9 hours, T5 I+, experts only." You want maximum Durmitor time.

**Practical considerations for Day 8 rafting:**
- Operators like Rafting Centar Drina-Tara and TaraSport are based in Foča (BiH side), making a morning start from Foča logical.
- Drive Sarajevo → Foča ≈ 1h30. Depart Sarajevo 07:30, at Foča 09:00, start rafting 10:00–10:30, finish at Šćepan Polje ~14:00–15:00. Cross border, drive Piva canyon (~2h), at Žabljak by 18:00–19:00. Tight but entirely doable in June daylight (sunset ~20:40).
- **Book Tara rafting for Jun 23, not Jun 25.** Email operators today — same 1-week lead time applies.

**The risk if you keep the current plan:** On Day 10 you spend 2+ hours driving from Žabljak back to the starting area you came from on Day 8, raft, then drive 3+ hours south to Skadar Lake. That is a ~7 hour drive day wrapped around a 4-hour raft trip. It is also a misuse of Durmitor time when you're only there 2 nights.

## FINDING 16: The "bad for THIS group specifically" audit

💡 WORTH KNOWING

The question asks which shortlisted items would be great on average but bad for 4 young adventurous adults specifically.

**Places that underserve this group:**

**`ba-daorson`** (Daorson megalithic walls, Ošanići): A Bronze Age Illyrian ruin above Stolac — genuinely impressive for archaeology enthusiasts, essentially "rocks on a hill" for most visitors. **`ba-tunnel-of-hope`** (Sarajevo War Tunnel Museum): Historically significant, genuinely moving for anyone interested in the siege. The tunnel itself is only 25m of the original 800m. For a young group that wants the Trebević cable car + bobsled track + Baščaršija + nightlife — this competes for a limited Sarajevo day and skews the tone toward museum tourism. Honest read: powerful if the group has any interest in 20th century history, skip-able if not. The bobsled track is the unique highlight; the tunnel is the educational one. Pick based on the group's actual interests.

**`hr-peljesac-bridge`** (Pelješac Bridge): It's a bridge they'll drive over. The shortlist has it as a "sight." They will see it. No additional stop needed.

**`me-mratinje-dam`** (Piva Dam): Listed as a sight. The dam and reservoir are genuinely dramatic — an impressive feat of Yugoslav engineering. But it's a viewpoint on the road to Plužine, worth 10 minutes of slowing down and looking, not a dedicated stop. The Piva canyon tunnels themselves are the experience; the dam at the start is context.

## FINDING 17: The Mljet vs. mainland alternative — the honest cost comparison

💡 WORTH KNOWING

The question asks: is Mljet worth it vs. a different island or a mainland beach night?

**Mljet case FOR:** The salt lakes are genuinely unique in the Adriatic — enclosed sea-water lakes inside an NP, kayakable, swimmable, with an island monastery in the middle. Montokuc (2h hike) gives a Adriatic panorama. Odysseus Cave cliff jump is a legitimate highlight. You can't get this experience anywhere else easily.

**The honest case AGAINST for this group:**
- It's a **Saturday** arrival (Jun 20) — the busiest ferry day. Peak queue risk.
- One night is borderline — the NP needs a full morning to itself, which means either arriving by the 13:00 ferry (€100 NP entry) or the 17:00 ferry (€75 NP entry but only 3h of daylight left). Neither is ideal.
- Total cost: ~€250 for 4 including ferry both ways + NP + camp. For comparison, Hvar costs less (car ferry from Split, cheaper NP, more nightlife). But Hvar doesn't fit the route.
- Pelješac itself (free, driving, wineries, Konoba Feral, swimming off the south coast) offers a very good evening and morning at near-zero cost by comparison.

**Verdict:** Mljet is worth it IF the group commits to: (1) arriving Prapratno 90+ min early for the 13:00 Saturday ferry, (2) accepting the €100 NP entry (no discount possible at that arrival time), (3) using the afternoon entirely for the NP. If the group is ambivalent, Pelješac overnight + Ston walls on the drive is a legit alternative that's cheaper and less logistically fraught. The research unanimously recommends Mljet overnight — that verdict stands — but the logistics are more brittle than the digest implies.
