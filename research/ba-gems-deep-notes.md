## The 3 best finds, argued

1. **Crni Vir waterfall, Džajići (`ba-crni-vir-dzajici`)** — The single best "missed gem" of
   the session. It's an 8m double waterfall into a 3m-deep emerald pool you can swim in and
   jump into, sitting *right at the Džajići Buk rafting take-out* — i.e. it's literally where
   the group's already-planned Neretva rafting trip ENDS, a ~10-15 min forest walk from the
   car park. Zero added driving, perfect taste for 4 adventurous adults, and it's genuinely
   off the radar: every Bosnian source frames it as "the hidden pearl of Konjic that few know
   about," the path isn't even marked. Highest value-per-effort find on the corridor.

2. **Mostarska Bijela cave-canyon waterfall (`ba-mostarska-bijela-canyon`)** — A genuine
   skriveni-dragulj most Mostarians haven't done: a ~3h riverbed hike up a Prenj-side slot
   canyon (1m wide, 20m tall) to a 10m waterfall dropping through a hole in the roof into a
   sun-lit emerald pool, 20 km north of Mostar off the M17. It's exactly the brief's
   "emerald swimming + waterfalls off the tourist track + adventurous," peaks in May-June
   (our window), and is doable unguided by fit adults ("well marked, follow the river"). A
   proper adventure half-day, not a roadside selfie.

3. **Glavatičevo + the upper-Neretva swim corridor (`ba-glavaticevo-neretva`)** — The clearest
   *negative-space* miss: the whole upper Neretva between Konjic and Boračko had no pin, yet
   it's the purest emerald stretch of the river, with pebble beaches, an old Ottoman bridge,
   shepherd-country scenery, and an eco-rural base (Villa Neretva: trout, kajmak, fly-fishing,
   homemade everything). It reframes the Konjic stop from "rafting + bunker and move on" into
   a swimmable mountain-valley basecamp, and it's the launch point that ties together Crni Vir,
   the rafting, and Boračko.

## Angles that came up empty / deliberately NOT pinned (so nobody re-digs)

- **Mostar Partisan Necropolis (spomenik).** Striking brutalist site, BUT FLAGGED OUT on
  safety grounds: vandals destroyed all ~700 markers in June 2022, it's on Europa Nostra's
  "7 most endangered" list (2023), and the Spomenik DB itself warns "not advisable to visit
  alone, especially as a tourist" after documented group attacks on tourists/students
  (2017 incident, ongoing far-right vandalism). Fails the bar for "young adventurous adults,
  safe." Noted here, not pinned. (Source: spomenikdatabase.org/mostar, Al Jazeera 2022,
  Europa Nostra 2023.)
- **Sarajevo→Konjic scenic backroad via Bjelašnica/Visočica/Sinanovići/Luka.** A real
  beautiful alternative drive (visitbih), but it's a ROUTE not a point, partly macadam before
  Luka, and its highlights (Umoljani, Studeni Potok, Glavatičevo, Boračko) are now each
  pinned individually — so the route is captured by its stops. Logged here as a driving
  option for `research/route-scenarios.md` to consider, not a pin.
- **Veliki Movran waterfall (Drežnica).** Bosnia's tallest (500m+) but ephemeral — "appears
  only once or twice a year after prolonged rain." Not reliable in June; folded into the
  Drežnica pin's notes rather than pinned as a standalone (a wrong promise is worse than none).
- **Vlašić/Vranica waterfalls (Buk on Ilomska, Ždrimački, Žukovica, Blihin skok).** All real
  klix "skriveni dragulji" — but CENTRAL/NW Bosnia, the wrong direction entirely (same
  off-corridor problem as the already-rejected Jajce/Una). Left out.
- **June festivals in the corridor:** re-confirmed thin (prior gap-hunt already established
  Mostar Summer Fest is Jul 2-4, misses the trip). Nothing new pin-worthy in Jun 16-28.
- **Foča-area katuns (Prijevor/Zelengora/Maglić katuns serving cheese & kajmak):** real and
  charming (visitsrpska), but they sit ON the Prijevor/Trnovačko/Perućica routes that the
  hikes file already owns — captured as a flavour note, not a separate pin.

## Coverage verdict (honest)

Bosnia held up as the leg with the most undiscovered upside, but the surface is now genuinely
thinning. After 15 sessions the Mostar/Blagaj/Sarajevo/Sutjeska/Trebinje cores are saturated;
the real remaining gold was in **two specific negative-space pockets** the fleet had under-
explored: (1) the **upper Neretva / Konjic micro-region** (Glavatičevo, Crni Vir, Konjic
bridge — the river-swim corridor), and (2) the **Mostar↔Jablanica M17 axis** (Mostarska
Bijela, Drežnica, Jablanica museum, Žitomislići). The Bjelašnica (Studeni Potok, Umoljani
mills), Sutjeska (Donje Bare), Foča (Gradac), Stolac and Trebinje-water finds round it out.

These 13 are real — each rests on multiple independent sources (local Bosnian media +
official tourist-org/operator pages + an English ground-truth report where possible) and
OSM-verified coordinates. I'd rate the top tier (Crni Vir, Mostarska Bijela, Glavatičevo,
Drežnica, Konjic bridge, Stolac, Jablanica) as confidently worth pinning; the second tier
(Donje Bare, Gradac, Studeni Potok, Umoljani mills, Žitomislići, Lastva/Jazina) as solid
backups whose appeal scales with how much time the group spends in each sub-area.

**I would NOT push hard for a 16th Bosnia session** — what's left after this is mostly
off-corridor (NW/central) or fails a safety/reliability bar (Partisan Necropolis, ephemeral
waterfalls). The corridor is now well-covered.
