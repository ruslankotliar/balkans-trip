# Trip Ops — weather, daylight, crowds, packing, date-specifics

Research session: TRIP OPS. Covers the whole loop (Zadar → Dubrovnik coast → Bosnia inland → Durmitor → Montenegro coast → Dubrovnik), Jun 16–28, 2026. Cross-references the overnight zones in [route-skeleton.md](route-skeleton.md) and drive legs in [drive-times.md](drive-times.md). No map pins were written — existing pins are referenced by name.

All clock times are **CEST (UTC+2)**, which applies in HR, BA and ME alike. Long-range caveats are flagged where the data genuinely doesn't exist yet (see §6).

---

## 0. Timing rules of thumb (the cheat sheet)

1. **Sightsee at the edges, swim/siesta in the middle.** Coast + Herzegovina midday is 30–35 °C. The honeypots (Dubrovnik walls, Kotor, Mostar bridge, Plitvice/Krka) are *before 9:00* or *after 17:00*; midday is for water, shade and driving.
2. **Mountain hikes start at dawn.** Durmitor/Biokovo afternoon thunderstorms build **12:00–15:00**. On the ridge there's no shelter. Bobotov Kuk / Veliki Međed: roll out of camp by **06:00–07:00**, be off the high ground before noon. Long days (light by ~05:10) make this easy.
3. **Use Vitaljina/Karasovići crossing (ME→HR, Jun 27) — NOT Debeli Brijeg.** Vitaljina is 15 km south of Kotor toward Herceg Novi: 15–45 min vs 1–4 h with EES biometric queues at Debeli Brijeg. Debeli Brijeg peak-day queues of 2–5 h are now the norm (EES since Apr 2026).
4. **The whole trip is already school-holiday peak** (Croatian holidays start Jun 15). Treat every day as high season — but late June is the *front edge* of the crush, not the July/Aug maximum.
5. **Lucky break: Kotor on Jun 27 has almost no cruise traffic** (1 small ship, 170 pax) vs 4 ships / 7,500+ pax on Jun 21 & 25. Climb the fortress early anyway for heat, but crowds will be a non-issue that day.
6. **Pack one genuinely warm sleeping bag setup for Durmitor.** Žabljak nights are 6–10 °C at 1,450 m even in late June. Beach-rated bags = a cold, bad night.
7. **Download offline maps before you leave.** Bosnia & Montenegro are *not* in EU free-roaming yet (still being negotiated as of Jun 2026). Your phone is the nav and the data clock is ticking the moment you leave Croatia.
8. **Bura/jugo are a non-issue in June.** Big car ferries (Mljet, Korčula, Ploče–Trpanj) run through normal summer weather; only fast catamarans get touchy.

## 1. Weather per zone (late June)

Numbers are climate normals for late June; treat ±3 °C as normal year-to-year swing. "Swimmable" = sea/lake comfortable for healthy young adults.

| Zone (nights) | Day high | Night low | Water | Rain / storms | Ops notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Adriatic coast** — Zadar, Split, Omiš, Makarska, Dubrovnik *(Jun 16–19, 27)* | 27–30 °C | 18–20 °C | Sea **22–24 °C — swimmable everywhere** | Low; brief showers possible | Strong sun; midday is for swimming, not walking walls. |
| **Herzegovina lowland** — Mostar, Kravica, Počitelj, Blagaj, Trebinje *(Jun 21)* | 30–33 °C, **spikes to 35 °C** | 14–16 °C | Kravica falls cold-refreshing; Buna @ Blagaj **icy** (spring) | Low rain, but brutal radiant heat off the limestone | The valley bakes. Mostar bridge at **08:00 or after 18:00**; midday = Kravica swim / shade. |
| **Bosnia mid-altitude** — Konjic, Boračko (~400 m), Sarajevo (~500 m) *(Jun 22)* | 25–28 °C | 12–14 °C | Boračko Lake cool but swimmable; Neretva cold (rafting = wetsuit) | Afternoon convective storms in the surrounding mountains | Noticeably milder than Mostar. Pleasant evenings. |
| **Durmitor / Žabljak** (~1,450 m) *(Jun 23–24)* | 14–22 °C | **6–10 °C (can dip to ~5 °C)** | Black Lake **~17–18 °C — cold, "chill in your bones" but doable** | **Daily afternoon thunderstorm risk, builds 12:00–15:00** | Warm bag + layers essential. Hike at dawn. Intense UV at altitude. |
| **Skadar / Podgorica basin** — Virpazar, Pavlova Strana *(Jun 25)* | 28–35 °C (Podgorica is the regional hot-spot) | ~17 °C | Lake warm & swimmable (reedy in places) | Low rain | **Mosquitoes at dusk — the worst on the trip.** Hot, hazy middays. |
| **Montenegro coast** — Budva, Sveti Stefan, Kotor *(Jun 26)* | 27–30 °C | 19–21 °C | Sea **23–24 °C — swimmable** | Low | Kotor bay is sheltered → feels humid/sticky. Sun drops behind Lovćen early (see §2). |

**Swimmable everywhere?** Yes for the sea (22–24 °C, comfortable). The *cold* swims are the mountain/spring waters — Black Lake (~17–18 °C), Buna at Blagaj, the Neretva, Tara. Refreshing on a hot day, but nobody's lingering.

**Mostar heat → what it does to the plan:** highs of 30–35 °C in a stone canyon. In-sun "feels-like" runs ~8 °C above air temp. The day-6/7 skeleton (Mostar *evening*, then Mostar *morning* + Blagaj) already dodges this correctly — keep it. Don't schedule the bridge or old-bazaar wandering for 13:00–16:00; that window is for the Kravica or Buna swim.

**Durmitor night temps in a tent → yes, bring warm bags.** 6–10 °C at the campsites (Mlinski Potok, Razvršje), and exposed pitches near Black Lake run colder with wind. A 3-season bag (comfort ~0 to +5 °C) + insulated pad + beanie is right. Summer/beach bags (comfort +10–15 °C) will leave people cold — add a fleece liner or sleep in layers if that's all you've got.

**Afternoon thunderstorms → start hikes when?** Durmitor storms reliably build **noon–15:00**; above the Lokvice saddle on Bobotov Kuk there is *no* shelter and lightning is a genuine hazard. Dawn start (camp by 06:00–07:00) puts you summit-by-noon, down-before-storms. Same logic for Biokovo/Sveti Jure and Paklenica on hot days — early also beats the heat.

**Rain odds:** coast is dry in June (a handful of brief-shower days all month). Mountains are the opposite — convective storms are a near-daily *possibility* on hot afternoons, usually short. Pack a packable rain shell for the Bosnia/Durmitor stretch; you likely won't need it on the coast.

**Bura / jugo winds & ferry risk:** these are autumn/winter/early-spring problems. June is the calm season. The big Jadrolinija car ferries you'd actually use — **Prapratno↔Sobra (Mljet)**, **Orebić↔Korčula**, **Ploče↔Trpanj** — are heavy and stable and run through normal summer weather; fast *catamarans* are the ones that get cancelled in wind, and they're not load-bearing for this itinerary. Net ferry-cancellation risk in our window: **low.** (Separate, unrelated note: the international **Split–Ancona** line is suspended 13 May–2 Jul 2026 for cost reasons — irrelevant to us, we take no Italy ferries.)

## 2. Daylight, golden hour & the best sun spots

Late June = the longest days of the year (solstice **Jun 21, 12:24 CEST**). ~**15.5 hours** of daylight everywhere on the route. Eastern locations (Durmitor, Kotor) run ~15–20 min *earlier* on the clock than Zadar for both sunrise and sunset, because they're further east.

**Approximate sun times for the trip window (all CEST, ± a couple of min across Jun 16–28):**

| Zone | Sunrise | Sunset | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| North coast (Zadar) | ~05:13 | ~20:47 | Latest sunsets of the whole route. |
| Split / Makarska | ~05:16 | ~20:43 | |
| Dubrovnik / south HR | ~05:08 | ~20:30 | |
| Mostar / Bosnia | ~05:05 | ~20:27 | |
| Durmitor / Žabljak | ~05:02 | ~20:23 | **Peaks block the sun** — effective sunrise later, sunset earlier in valleys. |
| Kotor / Budva | ~05:04 | ~20:22 | **Kotor town**: Lovćen swallows the sun early — real "dark" in the bay ~19:00–19:30, well before clock sunset. Budva (open west coast) gets the full sunset. |

- **Golden hour:** roughly the first hour after sunrise (~05:10–06:15) and the last hour before sunset (~19:30–20:45). **Blue hour** runs ~30 min past sunset (north coast stays usefully bright to ~21:10).
- **Astro note:** at this latitude near the solstice, true astronomical darkness is short and late (~midnight–03:00). See §5 for the moon, which matters more.

## 3. Crowd timing

**The headline:** Croatian school summer holidays begin **Jun 15, 2026** — so *the entire trip is peak season from day one*. The mitigation is time-of-day, not date. Late June is still the front edge (July/Aug are worse), but the honeypots already need the early/late treatment.

**Per-hotspot "go before / go after" rules:**

- **Plitvice (if chosen) & Krka:** in peak season gates open **07:00**; be *inside* before **08:00–09:00**. Crowds build steadily and **peak ~13:00** when tour buses dump. Plitvice now sells **timed-entry slots — book online in advance** (Entrance 2 is the less-mobbed start). For our route, Krka (1 h from the coast, half-day) is the realistic one; Plitvice costs +4:21 driving (see drive-times §1) — if you do it, sleep nearby and hit the 07:00 gate.
- **Dubrovnik city walls:** open **08:00** (summer). Walk them **at opening** — cooler, quieter, and ahead of the cruise wave. Cruise-day rhythm: 07:00–09:00 quiet (passengers still aboard) → **10:00–16:00 peak crush** → 17:00+ calm as ships leave. Old town itself is best **07:00–11:00** and again after **17:00**. Enter via **Ploče** (east) or **Buža** (north) gates, not Pile (where cruise buses unload).
- **Kotor old town & fortress:** climb the **Ladder of Kotor / San Giovanni at opening** for heat and crowds both. **But see the cruise calendar — our Kotor day (Jun 27) is a near-empty cruise day** (1 ship, 170 pax), so this is the one marquee site where timing is forgiving on our actual date.
- **Mostar / Stari Most:** no gate, no fee, open 24/7. Crowds + heat both peak midday. Best **before 09:00** or **after 17:00**; the bridge **softly lit at 21:00–22:00** (only possible if you overnight, which we do) is the version most day-trippers never see. The skeleton's Mostar-evening + Mostar-morning split nails this.

**Cruise-ship schedules (there *is* a public calendar):** cruisetimetables.com / cruisedig.com publish per-port daily 2026 calendars. **Kotor, Jun 16–28, 2026** (ships / total pax):

| Date | Ships | Pax | | Date | Ships | Pax |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Jun 16 | 3 | 5,482 | | Jun 22 | 2 | 2,640 |
| Jun 17 | 2 | 3,726 | | Jun 23 | 1 | 2,394 |
| Jun 18 | 3 | 7,172 | | Jun 24 | 1 | 1,814 |
| Jun 19 | 1 | 3,660 | | Jun 25 | 4 | **7,472** |
| Jun 20 | 2 | 4,590 | | **Jun 27** | **1** | **170** ← our Kotor day |
| Jun 21 | 4 | **7,968** | | Jun 28 | 1 | 1,984 |

So Jun 27 is the quietest cruise day of the fortnight at Kotor by a wide margin — keep Kotor on Jun 27. (Heavy days to *avoid* if the schedule shifts: Jun 21 & 25.) **Dubrovnik** has a similar published calendar and sees at least one ship almost every June day — there, lean on the 08:00-walls / Ploče-gate rules regardless. **Caveat:** cruise schedules this far out still drift by a ship or two; re-check ~2 weeks before.

**School holidays flipping the level:** already flipped — **Jun 15** start means no "before the holidays" window exists for us. Regional (BA/ME/RS) school holidays also fall across late June, reinforcing domestic-tourist volume on the Montenegrin coast and at Skadar/Durmitor on weekends. Plan the Montenegro-coast beach days and the Durmitor honeypots for **weekdays** where the route allows; weekends draw regional day-trippers.

## 4. Packing checklist (built for *this* trip: altitude camping + beach + serious hikes + 3 borders)

Tags: **[BRING]** from home · **[BUY]** cheaper/easier locally or must-buy-there · **[CONFIRM]** check it's included (rental/insurer) · **[DO]** an action before departure.

### Documents, car & borders — the critical pile
- **Passports, all 4** **[BRING]** — *required* for Bosnia **and** Montenegro; EU national ID cards are **not** accepted there. (Also needed even if you transit the Neum corridor.)
- **Green Card** (international motor-insurance cert) explicitly listing **BIH + MNE** **[CONFIRM at pickup]** — without it you are not authorised to take the rental across, regardless of whether the guard asks. The §3/skeleton loop crosses into BA and ME, so this is non-optional.
- **Rental agreement permitting cross-border BA + ME** **[CONFIRM]** — already arranged per CLAUDE.md; carry the paperwork, expect a possible per-country surcharge (~€5–15/day) baked in.
- **Driving licences** (all who'll drive) **[BRING]** — EU licences fine in HR/BA/ME, no IDP needed.
- **Travel insurance covering BA + ME** **[DO]** — your EHIC covers Croatia/EU only; Bosnia & Montenegro are outside it. Make sure the policy covers *the activities* (rafting, canyoning, cliff-jumping, high hiking).
- **In-car legal kit:** hi-vis vest **per person**, warning triangle, first-aid kit, spare bulbs — mandatory in HR/BA/ME. Usually in the rental **[CONFIRM at pickup]**.
- **Cash:** EUR is the currency in **both HR and ME**. **Bosnia uses the convertible mark (BAM/KM)** — cards work in cities, but carry **~€50–100 worth of KM** for tolls, parking, rural konobas. Keep small EUR/KM for border-area tolls. **[DO / BUY at an ATM in BA]**

### Power & navigation — 13 days of phones-as-satnav
- **Multi-port USB-C **car** charger** (PD + QC), ideally **two** so all 4 phones rotate while driving **[BRING/BUY ~€15–25]** — this is the *primary* charging strategy; most days have 1–3 h of driving (see drive-times), which tops everyone up.
- **Long charging cables** that reach the back seats **[BRING]** + a **phone mount** for the driver **[BRING/BUY ~€10]**.
- **Power bank 20,000 mAh+**, one per pair **[BRING]** — for camp nights with no hookup (Mljet, Durmitor wild-ish pitches).
- **12 V inverter (150 W+)** **[BUY only if needed]** — *optional*; only worth it if someone has **AC-only** chargers (camera batteries, laptop). If everything charges over USB, skip it — the USB-C car chargers cover you.
- **Offline maps downloaded** **[DO before departure]** — **essential.** Google Maps offline regions for HR + BA + ME, plus **Organic Maps / maps.me** as a no-data backup. Because:
- **Roaming reality** **[DO]**: Croatia = EU, your EU SIM roams free. **Bosnia & Montenegro are NOT in EU free-roaming** — as of Jun 4, 2026 the "Roam Like at Home" extension to the Western Balkans is only *entering negotiations*, not in force. So an EU/Croatian SIM will rack up roaming charges across most of the trip. Options: rely on offline maps + Wi-Fi, **or buy a regional Balkans eSIM** **[BUY]**, or check whether your carrier has a Western-Balkans add-on. Decide before you leave.

### Camping at altitude
- **Warm sleeping bags** (3-season, comfort ~0 to +5 °C) **[BRING]** — the single most important Durmitor item; see §1. A liner adds ~5 °C if your bags are lighter.
- **Insulated sleeping pads** (decent R-value — the ground is cold at 1,450 m) **[BRING]**.
- **Warm layers:** fleece/light down, beanie, warm socks, long trousers for Durmitor evenings **[BRING]**.
- **Camping stove gas canisters** **[BUY in Zadar on arrival]** — you're flying in; **gas cannot go in checked or carry-on luggage.** Buy canisters locally day 1.
- Good tent pegs + guylines for exposed/windy alpine pitches **[BRING]**.

### Beach & water
- **Water/aqua shoes** **[BRING or BUY locally ~€10–15]** — for rocky Adriatic entries, **sea urchins** (present on rockier, less-touristy spots; the water's clear so you can usually spot and avoid them — but a spine in the foot ruins a day), and slippery river rocks at Kravica/Cetina/Tara.
- **Quick-dry towels, swimwear** **[BRING]**; **dry bag** for rafting/kayak/boat days — Mljet, Skadar, Tara **[BRING]**; **snorkel/mask** optional (clear water).

### Sun & heat
- **High-SPF sunscreen** **[BRING]** — coast sun *and* high-UV at altitude; reapply on hike days. **Sun hat + sunglasses** **[BRING]**, **lightweight long sleeves** for 30–35 °C middays (Mostar, Skadar).
- **Refillable water bottles** (1.5–2 L pp on hike days) **[BRING]**; electrolyte tabs for hot hikes **[optional/BUY]**.

### Hiking
- **Proper boots / trail shoes** **[BRING]** — Bobotov Kuk/Veliki Međed is 10–12 h over exposed scree; Biokovo and Paklenica also real terrain. **Daypack** + **trekking poles** (help on Durmitor scree) **[BRING]**.
- **Headlamp with red mode** **[BRING]** — for dawn-start hikes, camp, *and* preserving night vision when stargazing.
- **Packable rain shell** **[BRING]** — for Bosnia/Durmitor afternoon storms.

### Bugs & health
- **Strong repellent (DEET or picaridin)** **[BRING]** — **Skadar Lake at dusk is the worst on the route**; also useful around Bosnian rivers/lakes. **Antihistamine** cream + tablets for bites/reactions **[BRING]**.
- **Tweezers / urchin-spine kit** (fine needle, antiseptic) **[BRING]** and basic **first aid + blister plasters** **[BRING]**; personal meds **[BRING]**. Be **tick-aware** after Bosnian/Durmitor meadow hikes (check skin).

### Commonly-forgotten (the stuff trip reports always mention)
- Universal **sink plug** + camp wash kit; **spare headlamp batteries**; **duct/gaffer tape** for gear repair; **travel laundry soap + a few clothes pins** (13 days); a **reusable shopping bag** (HR & ME charge for bags); **cooler bag** for the car; a **paper backup** of all bookings + the rental/green-card docs in case phones die.

## 5. Date-specifics — moon, stars, events

### Moon phase, night by night (waxing all trip)
**New Moon Jun 15 → First Quarter Jun 21 → Full ("Strawberry") Moon Jun 29.** The whole trip the moon waxes from a sliver to nearly full:

| Night | ~Illum. | Where you sleep | Stargazing verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Jun 16–20 | ~2 → 30 % | Coast (Zadar→Dubrovnik→Mljet) | **Darkest skies of the trip** — thin crescent sets early, near-black nights. But you're on the (more light-polluted) coast. **If you want serious Milky Way, this is the window to be at altitude** — e.g. a Velebit/Zavižan or Biokovo night early on pairs new-moon dark sky with elevation. |
| Jun 21–22 | ~40–50 % | Mostar / Konjic | Half-moon up half the night; mediocre. |
| **Jun 23–24** | **~62–72 % waxing gibbous** | **Žabljak / Durmitor** (the marquee dark-sky spot) | **Bittersweet:** zero light pollution + altitude, but a **bright gibbous moon**. The moon sets ~01:30 (Jun 23) / ~02:15 (Jun 24), leaving only a **narrow dark window ~02:00–03:15** before astronomical dawn. Still worth it — the Milky Way core is up in the south and the sky is genuinely black once the moon's down — just set an alarm rather than expecting an all-night show. Red-mode headlamp; 20–30 min to dark-adapt. |
| Jun 25–27 | ~82 → 96 % | Skadar / Budva / Dubrovnik | Bright moon all night; not a stargazing window. |

**Bottom line on Durmitor stargazing:** the famous dark-sky reputation is real, but *our dates draw a gibbous moon there*, so the best Milky Way of the trip is actually the **early near-new-moon coast nights — cash them in only if you're somewhere high/dark** (Velebit, Biokovo). At Durmitor, plan a post-2 a.m. window on Jun 23/24.

### Events affecting roads/ferries — and where the data runs out
- **No major festival clashes found on our dates.** The big Montenegrin coast electronic festival, **Sea Dance**, **left Montenegro after 2022** (relocated to Croatia, and it ran mid-July/August anyway) — so **no Jaz Beach festival** on our nights. Budva's "Sea of Rhythms" festival is **May 21–25** (before us).
- **Split–Ancona** international ferry suspended **13 May–2 Jul 2026** — doesn't touch our itinerary (no Italy crossings).
- **Solstice / St John's (Ivanjske krijesove, ~Jun 23–24):** minor bonfire traditions on the Croatian coast — a local-color bonus, not a logistics factor.
- **Honest limit:** specific small-town festivals, road works and exact ferry timetables for late June are **not reliably published 12+ months out**. Treat the above as "nothing known." **Re-check ~2–4 weeks before departure:**
  - **Jadrolinija** timetables (confirm Prapratno↔Sobra & any Korčula sailings) — jadrolinija.hr
  - **HAK** for Croatian road works/closures; live **Debeli Brijeg / Karasovići border webcams** for queue reality on Jun 27
  - The **Kotor & Dubrovnik cruise calendars** for any last-minute ship changes
  - Local tourist-board "events" pages for Mostar, Kotor, Budva on your specific dates
