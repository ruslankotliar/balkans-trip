# Safety & Contingency Playbook — Balkans Road Trip (Jun 16–28, 2026)

Croatia → Bosnia & Herzegovina → Montenegro → Dubrovnik. Rental car (pick up Zadar, drop Dubrovnik), 3 border crossings, camping + adventure. **This is the "if X happens → do Y → call Z" sheet — print it / save offline.**

Researched from real traveler reports (Reddit, TripAdvisor/Rick Steves/Fodor's forums, park4night, trip blogs) + authoritative refs (auto clubs, mountain rescue, official emergency numbers). Sources at the bottom. **Flags:** anything marked ⚠ should be re-verified close to departure (prices, fuel, rules drift).

---

## 0. SAVE THIS FIRST — the one-screen essentials

- **112 works in all three countries** (HR, BA, ME) — free, any phone, even no-SIM/no-credit. When in doubt, dial **112** and ask for police / ambulance / fire / mountain rescue. You don't need to remember which country you're in.
- **Keep low-beam headlights ON at all times, the whole trip.** Mandatory daytime in BA and ME year-round (€30 on-the-spot fine in ME if off); harmless in HR. Just leave them on from Zadar to Dubrovnik.
- **Keep ONE sober driver, always — treat Montenegro as zero-tolerance** (limit reported 0.2–0.3‰; one beer can fail you). BA 0.3‰, HR 0.5‰.
- **Don't drive mountain roads at night** — Piva canyon tunnels, Durmitor ring, Sutjeska, Kotor serpentines. Daylight only.
- **Photograph/video the rental at pickup AND return** — all glass, four rims, fuel gauge, and the **front/rear undercarriage** (the documented fake-damage spot).
- **Passports + insurance + green card + rental papers: keep on-person, accessible at borders, never buried in the boot, never left in the car.**
- **Buy adventure-tier travel insurance and declare rafting/canyoning/via-ferrata at purchase.** BA & ME are non-EU — **EHIC does NOT cover you there.** Assume **cliff jumping is uninsured** on most policies.

## 1. IF X HAPPENS → DO Y → CALL Z

### 🚗 Car breaks down (flat, won't start, warning light)
**Reality:** Your Croatian rental's roadside assistance does **not reliably cross the border** — even the "good" companies just coordinate the call; you usually **pay the local tow/repair out of pocket and claim back later.** Carry cash (EUR + Bosnian marks/BAM).
1. Get the car safe, **put on the hi-vis vest BEFORE exiting** (keep it in the cabin, not boot), set the warning triangle.
2. **Call the rental emergency line first** (off the contract) — they may authorise a local fix and tell you what they reimburse.
3. In parallel, **call the local auto club** — they have the trucks + local garages:
   - **ME (Durmitor/Žabljak/coast):** AMSCG **19807** / +382 20 234 467
   - **BA (Sutjeska/Konjic/Mostar):** BIHAMK **1282** / +387 33 282 100
   - **HR:** HAK **1987**
4. Remote-mountain failure (Žabljak, Sutjeska, Piva): expect a **tow toward Nikšić / Podgorica (ME) or Foča / Mostar (BA)** — there's no real garage in the mountains. Be patient; cross-border tows are slow.

### 💥 Car accident / collision
1. **112** for injuries. Police report is mandatory for any insurance claim — **always get the police on scene and get the report.**
2. **Photograph everything**; exchange details; don't admit fault.
3. Call the **rental emergency line** + your **insurer's 24h line**.
4. **Do not move on / leave the country** before the paperwork is done — an accident in a country not authorised on your contract = you're uninsured (so make sure HR+BA+ME are written on the contract; see §2).

### 👮 Police stop / speeding fine (most likely in MONTENEGRO)
**ME enforcement is mobile radar, often at town-edge limit drops and on the coast road / Sozina tunnel.** Typical real fines €30–80, paid by card on their POS or at a bank/post office.
1. Pull over promptly, **headlights already on**, stay in the car, hands visible, be calm and polite.
2. Have ready: licence, passport, rental papers. **No officer should keep your passport** — red flag if they try.
3. If you genuinely sped, **accept the official fine.** Ask to pay by **card on the mobile POS** or insist on an **official ticket payable at the post office (Pošta)/bank — and get a receipt.**
4. **Don't offer cash bribes** — it's a crime and every "corrupt cop" horror story online started with the tourist offering money. If an officer steers you toward an undocumented cash "agreement" and it feels wrong, politely say you'll pay the official fine at the post office; the paperwork hassle usually ends it.
5. **Bosnia:** switch OFF any radar/speed-camera-detection feature in your nav app — **detection devices are illegal there.**
6. **Croatia:** pay an on-the-spot fine under €265 and you get a **50% discount**; camera fines get billed to the rental company (and forwarded to you) weeks later.

### 🔓 Car broken into / theft from car
**Low risk on this route** (window-smashing is near-nonexistent in ME, rare in HR — far below Barcelona/W. Europe). But:
1. Report to **police (112)** and **get a written report** — required for any insurance claim and lost-passport replacement.
2. Call rental + insurer.
3. **Prevention is the whole game:** pack the car to look **empty** (everything in the boot under a cover, **loaded before you reach the lot**), and **never leave passports/laptops/cash/keys in the car.** Rental theft insurance covers the car, **not your belongings.**

### 🛂 Lost / stolen passport
1. **Police report** (122/112) — you need it.
2. Go to your embassy/consulate: **Bosnia → Sarajevo; Montenegro → Podgorica; Croatia → Zagreb (consulates in Split/Dubrovnik).** EU citizens: **any EU embassy must help you** if yours isn't represented.
3. Since Dec 2025, EU states issue the **EU Emergency Travel Document (ETD)** for a single trip home. Bring any other ID + photo.
4. This is why an **offline photo of every passport** (see §8) matters — it speeds the replacement hugely.

### 🩺 Medical emergency
1. **112** (or 124 ambulance). 112 dispatchers in tourist areas generally speak English; in remote BA/ME mountains they may not — use the phrasebook (§7-bottom) / point at the offline translator.
2. **Know your evac reality:** Žabljak, Konjic, Sutjeska, Budva, Mljet are **clinic-only**. The real hospitals serving the adventure middle of the trip are **Nikšić (1h25 from Žabljak), Mostar, and Podgorica (KCCG).** See the per-zone table (§3).
3. **BA & ME are non-EU → EHIC does not cover you.** Be ready to **pay (cash/card) and reclaim** — a stitch job in Kotor was billed ~€370. Carry your insurer's 24h assistance number; with proper insurance some clinics bill the insurer directly.

### 🪂 Adventure-activity injury (rafting / canyoning / cliff jump / via ferrata / high hike)
1. On-water/in-canyon: the **operator runs first response** (licensed skippers, shore medic). Follow their lead; they call rescue.
2. Hiking injury with no signal: see "lost/injured in mountains" below.
3. **Insurance caveat (do this BEFORE the trip):** standard policies commonly **exclude** these. **Buy the adventure/hazardous-sports tier, declare canyoning + via ferrata + rafting explicitly at purchase** (can't be added after), **confirm Durmitor altitude (Bobotov Kuk ~2,523 m) is within the trekking cap, and keep operator receipts** (the "licensed operator" condition). **Cliff jumping is excluded on most policies — treat it as uninsured and behave accordingly.**

### ⛰️ Lost / injured in the mountains, no phone signal
**Signal dies in Sutjeska NP, on Durmitor interior trails (Black Lake → Veliki Međed ridge), and in the Piva canyon.** Download offline maps before you enter.
1. If you have any bars: **112**, then **GSS BiH +387 63 11 22 33** (Sutjeska) or **GSCG +382 40 256 084** (Durmitor).
2. **Give your location two ways:** **GPS lat/long** from your offline map app (Organic Maps / Mapy.cz show it without data) **and** your **what3words** address (easier to read over a bad line). Plus the nearest named feature (lake/peak/trailhead).
3. **Prevent it:** tell someone your route + turnaround time, agree a meeting point before splitting up, carry water/layers/headtorch. ME rescue is real but **slow and resource-limited** — self-sufficiency matters more here than in the Alps.

### 🐍 Snake bite (nose-horned viper / poskok — present in dry karst: Velebit, Dalmatia, BA/ME karst)
Bites are **rare and rarely fatal with hospital care.** Stay **calm**, **immobilise the limb**, **do NOT cut/suck/tourniquet**, get to a hospital (Zadar/Split/Mostar/Nikšić/Podgorica/Kotor all handle it). Prevent: closed boots on rocky trails, watch hands/feet on rocks and dry-stone walls.

### 🅿️ Parking ticket / car towed
**Where tourists actually get towed:** Dubrovnik (Old Town restricted zone — ⚠ new ZTL since Jun 2025, €150 fines for unaware rental drivers), Kotor (Pauk tow truck, ~€120 all-in), **Budva (most aggressive — "gone in 15 min", fines from €160)**, Mostar (Stari Most cobbles). **Always use signed paid lots** (see §5) and **park outside the old-town zones and walk in.** If towed: the paid lot / police (122) can tell you the pound; bring cash.

## 2. Car & rental — the at-pickup ritual, borders, gravel, fuel, tolls

### At pickup in Zadar (do ALL of this before driving off)
- **Timestamped walk-around video**, narrating: all four **wheel rims**, **windshield + all glass**, **fuel gauge**, every existing scratch/dent, and crucially the **front and rear undercarriage lips** — the "we looked under the car" claim is the documented Croatia scam (Sixt threads especially). Get every existing scratch **noted on the contract** too.
- **Confirm HR + BA + ME are all written on the contract** as authorised countries, plus the **Green Card** (international insurance cert) and **cross-border authorisation letter** in hand. Crossing into a non-listed country **voids your insurance** — that's the real risk, not the border guard.
- Get the **cross-border fee disclosed in writing** (reports range wildly, ⚠ €10–€75; non-EU at the high end). Watch for sneaked-in "prepaid fuel"/road-assistance fees.
- **Photograph the contract's emergency/breakdown number.** Confirm in writing whether roadside assistance / a replacement vehicle work abroad (assume largely not).
- Note the deposit hold (reports €1,200–€5,000; declining their CDW upsell triggers the bigger hold) — keep credit-card headroom.

### At drop-off in Dubrovnik
- Repeat the **timestamped video** before handing back. Have staff **sign off and process final payment immediately** so nothing can be added later. Don't drop the car after-hours into an empty lot if avoidable.

### Borders (HR→BA, BA→ME, ME→HR)
- Carry **passports** (EU ID cards not accepted for BA/ME), licence, **original vehicle registration**, rental agreement w/ cross-border auth, green card. Border guards mostly just check passports — but **make sure you get entry/exit stamps.**
- Neum corridor (short Bosnian coastal stretch on the old Dubrovnik road) needs **no cross-border fee** — relevant only if you skip the Pelješac bridge on Jun 16–18.

### Gravel / rough roads vs the contract
- **Read the "prohibited surfaces" clause.** Many rentals **ban unpaved/gravel roads** — driving them can void insurance AND hands them a fake-undercarriage claim.
- **Road-by-road for our route:**
  - **Šćepan Polje → Piva canyon → Plužine:** ✅ **paved, every car can do it.** But ~56–70 **unlit single-lane tunnels** over ~25 km — go slow, headlights on, yield early to oncoming trucks/buses. Daylight only. Allow 40–60 min for the canyon.
  - **Durmitor Ring (P14) / Sedlo pass:** ✅ **2WD-OK in summer (open ~Jun–Oct)**, but potholed, dusty unlit tunnels, narrow with steep unprotected drops. Slow and focused.
  - **Lukomir (BA highest village):** ⚠ **last ~16 km gravel; not recommended in a 2WD rental** (contract + damage risk). Do it as a **guided jeep tour**, or stop at **Umoljani** (as far as locals advise for a small car), or treat it as a drop-candidate.

### Fuel gaps
- **Fill up in BOSNIA whenever below half** — reliably the cheapest of the three (⚠ ~€1.20/L petrol in 2024; re-check 2026). HR is dearest.
- **Top up before the remote stretches:** in **Foča** before Sutjeska/Šćepan Polje; in **Plužine** before the Durmitor Ring (the ring road itself has no station). There ARE stations in Žabljak and Plužine, so you won't be stranded — just don't run low on a one-lane mountain pass.

### Tolls
- **Croatia:** toll booths (take ticket, pay on exit by distance), **cash EUR or card**. No vignette in 2026 (⚠ e-vignette repeatedly delayed).
- **Montenegro:** **Sozina tunnel €2.50** (only on the deep-south Bar route); Bar–Boljare A1 section €3.50; everything else (Piva, Durmitor, Kotor, Budva, Ostrog) toll-free. Carry small EUR notes.
- **Bosnia:** tolls only on A1 motorway sections (~€4–6/100 km, cash EUR/BAM or card). The Mostar–Konjic–Foča road is mostly non-motorway = little/no toll. No vignette.

## 4. Driving & police — rules that catch tourists

| Rule | HR | BA | ME |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daytime headlights | Winter only (off in June, but keep on) | **ON always** | **ON always** (€30 fine; DRLs not always accepted — use low beams) |
| Alcohol limit | 0.5‰ (0.0 under 25) | 0.3‰ | ⚠ 0.2–0.3‰ — **treat as zero** |
| Speed (urban / open) | 50 / 90–110 | 50 / 80–100 | 50 / 80 |
| Enforcement | Fixed + **average-speed** cameras on motorways | Mobile radar; **radar-detector apps ILLEGAL** | **Mobile radar** (no fixed cams) — the highest-stop country |
| On-the-spot fine for foreigners | Yes; **−50% if paid on the spot** under €265 | Yes | Yes — card POS or post office; **always get a receipt** |

- **Must be in the car (rental usually supplies — verify):** warning triangle, **hi-vis vest (keep in cabin!)**, first-aid kit, spare bulbs. Missing item = on-the-spot fine, and **the police fine YOU, not the rental company.**
- **Driving culture:** the real hazard is **aggressive overtaking/tailgating**, not police. Drive defensively, **let tailgaters pass**, don't feel pressured to overtake on blind curves. Watch for livestock and rockfall on mountain roads.
- **Night mountain driving: avoid.** Piva canyon (unlit tunnels), Durmitor ring, Sutjeska, and especially the **Kotor → Lovćen serpentine** (do it **uphill, daylight, early to beat tour buses**; cars sometimes must reverse to let buses pass).

### Where tourists get towed / fined — and where to actually park
- **Dubrovnik:** Old Town is car-free; ⚠ **new restricted (ZTL) zone since Jun 2025** caught many rental drivers (€150). Park at **Ilijina Glavica garage** (no permit needed) or cheaper **Gruž port (~€3/hr, €24–30/day)** and walk/bus in.
- **Kotor:** Old Town car-free; **Pauk tow is fast** (~€60 fine + €60 tow). Use **"Parking Old Town" / "Tabačina"** (€0.80–2.50/hr) or cheaper **Autoboka** (€0.60–0.90/hr).
- **Budva:** **most aggressive towing (15 min!), fines from €160.** Use lots **"Jat" / "Zeta Film" / "Exponat."**
- **Mostar:** Stari Most area car-free (cobbles wreck suspension). Park the perimeter (around Maršala Tita); **white lines = paid, blue = free but timed.**
- **Žabljak / Black Lake:** Durmitor NP entry ⚠ ~€5/person/day; **Black Lake parking ~€3/hr, staffed lot** (low theft risk).

## 5. Theft & valuables playbook

**This route is genuinely low-risk for car break-ins** (windows smashed for bags is near-nonexistent in ME, rare in HR — well below Western Europe). The **real** risks are petty **scams** and old-town **pickpockets**. Don't build the trip around theft paranoia — these habits cover ~95% of it:

**DO**
- **Pack the car to look empty** — everything in the boot under a cover, **loaded before you reach the lot** (don't reorganise the trunk at the trailhead with an audience).
- **Passports, phones, laptops, cash, car keys = on your person or locked in the tent** — **never in the car**, even the glovebox.
- **Use staffed/paid/gated lots and gated campsites** (Black Lake, Krka's official Lozovac lot, paid Kotor lots) — the safe default here.
- **Lock e-bikes / expensive kit to fixed metal** — the one campsite theft that actually recurs.
- **Vet specific wild/overnight spots on park4night/iOverlander review texts** before committing — that's where rare break-in flags surface.
- **Carry digital + printed copies** of all passports, the rental cross-border letter, and the green card, stored separately from originals; split documents across people.

**DON'T**
- Don't leave **anything visible** in the car, ever — the only theft variable you fully control.
- Don't park overnight on the street in **Sarajevo, Podgorica, or central Budva** — use a mall garage (~€10). Don't park on "twinkly pavement" (broken glass = known break-in spot).
- Don't **wild-camp in Croatian coastal/NP/beach zones** — actively fined (~€200–1,300). Montenegro is more relaxed if discreet and away from beaches/NPs/towns.
- Don't bushwhack off marked trails in rural BA/HR — **residual landmines exist in marked areas.** Stay on marked ground.

**Scams to brief the group on (the actual money risk):**
- **Taxis:** insist on the meter or use **Bolt/Uber**; unlicensed cabs at Split ferry / Mostar bus station / Budva quote insane fares (a €40 run billed €120–643 in real reports).
- **Nightclub / bar traps (Budva Topliška St, Split Bačvice, Mostar Kujundžiluk bazaar):** "free entry"/free-drink promoters → €300–2,000 bills, minimum-spend traps, female tourists' drinks added to shared bills, exit blocked by security. **Get pricing/minimum-spend in writing before entering, card-only, watch your card and drink, leave together.**
- **Jet-ski "damage" claim** (Budva/Jaz beaches): video the craft + signed condition report, pay by card.
- **ATMs:** use bank-branded machines, **decline "charge in home currency" (DCC) — always pick local currency** (EUR in HR/ME, BAM in BA).
- **Booking/apartment fraud:** book only in-app, never pre-pay off-platform (cloned listings + fake-WhatsApp payment requests reported in Split).

## 6. Health — insurance, water, ticks, sun, kit

- **Travel insurance is mandatory for BA & ME** (non-EU, no EHIC). **EHIC works only in Croatia** (EU). Buy the **adventure/hazardous-sports tier, declare rafting/canyoning/via-ferrata, confirm Durmitor altitude is covered, keep operator receipts.** **Cliff jumping ≈ uninsured.** (See §1 adventure-injury.)
- **Tap water:** safe in Croatian coast, Bosnian & Montenegrin cities, Podgorica, and Budva/Kotor (protected springs; stronger chlorine taste in peak summer). **Caution with untreated mountain spring / cistern water in Durmitor** (June snowmelt → bacteria risk) — carry a filter or bottled water for trekking and remote villas.
- **Ticks / TBE / Lyme:** TBE is concentrated in **continental N. Croatia** (low on your coastal/karst route), but **Durmitor/Sutjeska/Bosnian forests = treat as possible risk**, and **Lyme is endemic across all of Croatia incl. the littoral.** Long trousers tucked in socks, repellent, **full-body tick check every evening after hiking**, remove ticks with a proper tool (not oil/heat), watch for an expanding bull's-eye rash → doctor. **TBE vaccine not worth it for this trip** (needs months to complete; route is mostly low-risk) — repellent + daily checks + prompt Lyme treatment is the high-yield protection.
- **Sun/heat** is the most likely thing to actually go wrong: **Biokovo and Durmitor ridges are exposed with no shade** — start early, carry far more water than feels necessary (spring refills unreliable), hat + high SPF.
- **Sea urchins** on rocky Adriatic beaches → **buy water shoes for all 4** (also guard against sharp rocks). Spines: tweezers + pharmacy ichthammol cream draws them out. Jellyfish are mild here.
- **Mosquitoes at dusk** at Skadar Lake / Neretva delta wetlands in June — repellent + long sleeves for evenings (nuisance only, no malaria).
- **First-aid kit before remote legs (Durmitor, Sutjeska, Mljet, Konjic):** painkillers, antihistamine, anti-diarrheal, rehydration salts, blister care, **tick tool + tweezers**, antiseptic, sunscreen, after-bite, **water shoes**, personal prescriptions. Don't rely on a pharmacy being open in Žabljak or on Mljet.

## 7. Connectivity & offline copies

**Single most important comms fact: your EU roaming SIM is NOT free in Bosnia or Montenegro** (both non-EU → paid roaming; the EU–Balkans free-roaming deal targets 2028, ⚠ not active June 2026).
- **Best fix:** an **eSIM covering all three** — e.g. **Airalo "Eurolink"** (HR+BA+ME) or a Western-Balkans regional eSIM. ⚠ **Confirm both BA AND ME are explicitly listed** before buying — cheap "Europe" plans often drop them. One person can tether a hotspot.
- **Signal dies** in **Sutjeska NP**, on **Durmitor interior trails** (Black Lake → Veliki Međed), and in the **Piva canyon.** Download everything offline **before entering BA, and again before ME.**

**Download / carry offline (before you lose signal):**
- **Offline maps with live GPS + trails:** Organic Maps or **Mapy.cz** (hiker favourite for Durmitor) + Google Maps offline areas for driving. **AllTrails (Pro)** for Durmitor routes.
- **Google Translate offline packs:** Croatian + Bosnian (covers ME/Serb areas — languages mutually intelligible; Serbian offline pack is flaky). Camera mode reads Cyrillic signs in Republika Srpska.
- **what3words** (works offline for reading your own location) for rescue calls.
- **Documents — saved ON the device (not just cloud) AND a printed set in the glovebox:** all passports (photo page), rental contract + cross-border auth + **green card** + rental breakdown number, **insurance policy + 24h assistance number**, EHIC, this hospital list, embassy contacts.
