## 1. OPEN-VARIANT VERDICTS

### 1a. Plitvice — **NO** (unanimous)
`hr-north-notes.md` recommends skipping outright: it costs **+4:21 of pure driving** (`drive-times.md`: Zadar→Plitvice→Krka→Split 6:33 vs 2:12 coastal), starts **1.5 h the wrong way (north)** inside a hard 1.5-usable-day window, costs ~€40 pp, is regimented (timed boardwalks, **no swimming**), and is the wrong vibe for an adventure group. **Krka** delivers the turquoise-waterfall payoff on the southbound line with **legal swimming at Roški slap/Stinice/Pisak** (swimming at Skradinski buk banned since 2021) — and even Krka is rated "defensible to skip both NPs" in favour of a Velebit/Zrmanja active day. If the group overrules: `trip-ops.md` says sleep nearby, book the timed-entry slot online, be inside before 08:00 (crowds peak ~13:00). **No session argued for Plitvice.** It stays a candidate pin only.

### 1b. Mountains vs coast, Zadar→Split — **mountains-lite: Paklenica day + inland line, but only if you do the stops**
`hr-north-notes.md`: the inland route via Knin costs **+1:28** (3:40 vs 2:12, `drive-times.md`) and the road alone is *not* worth it — the payoff is the chain it unlocks: **Zrmanja kayaking and/or Tulove grede → Pariževačka glavica viewpoint → (Cerovac caves) → Knin fortress** ("a real shame to miss"). Decision rule: doing Zrmanja/Tulove grede as the active day → go inland; skipping them → take the coast via Krka. Paklenica is cheap either way (0:54 from Zadar) and anchors "Plan M": Jun 16 PM camp Starigrad, Jun 17 gorge + Anića kuk (or Zrmanja kayak) → inland stops → night Split/Omiš. Islands (Dugi Otok/Kornati) **do not fit** — the car ferry forces a ~10 h commitment (`hr-north-notes.md`). `experiences-notes.md` adds a tiebreaker: **new moon Jun 15 → Jun 16–18 is the trip's best Milky Way window, and Velebit/Zavižan is the darkest accessible sky in Croatia** — a mountain night early on cashes that in; coast nights waste it.

> **⛔ Krka NP: REJECTED for this trip.** `day2-decision.md` (2026-06-11) closes the debate: **swimming at Skradinski Buk has been banned since 2021** — what remains is a crowded boardwalk walk at **€40pp (€160 for 4)** with no swimming at the main falls. Legal swimming is only at Roški Slap, requiring a separate 4h boat excursion (+€20pp). Recent TripAdvisor reviews: "not worth the €40 entrance fee." **Day 2 = Omiš: Cetina extreme canyoning (~€70pp, 12m jumps + 55m rappel + natural pools) or zipline (~€80pp, 8 lines, 150m above the canyon). Both are a perfect group fit, and you arrive at the overnight anyway.** The combination day (Krka + Omiš) does not work in practice — either timing slips or you spend €160 on a walk and rush to Omiš. Cut Krka; do Omiš properly. Kravica Falls in Bosnia (Day 6) is "what Krka used to be" at ~€10pp with full swimming. Source: `day2-decision.md`.

### 1c. Korčula vs Mljet vs both — **Mljet overnight if one; 2-day Pelješac swing if two**
`hr-south-notes.md`: **Mljet is the fit for this group** (lakes kayak/bike, Montokuc, Odysseus Cave cliff jumping) but it must be an **overnight, not a day trip** — ferry lands at Sobra ~30 min from the NP and entry is steep. Korčula is the town+beach+wine pick with the cheapest, most frequent ferry (~20+/day); skip Vela Luka (far west, +1 h, "no better water"). **Both** is genuinely feasible because Orebić↔Prapratno is only **1:24** (`drive-times.md`): Ston walls + Mali Ston oysters + Dingač winery → Korčula day → Mljet overnight → Sobra→Prapratno → on to Bosnia. `mljet-logistics.md` (2026-06-11) confirms Mljet sailings 5/day each way in June (07:00/10:15/13:00/17:00/20:30 out; 06:00/09:00/12:00/16:00/19:30 back), **car+4 ≈ €50 each way**, and — critically — **no car reservation exists on any of these lines** (first-come; queue 60+ min early; see §6).

### 1f. Šćepan Polje border crossing — **GO: fine in a normal rental, just slow; daylight only**
Three sessions agree (`ba-notes.md`, `me-notes.md`, `logistics-notes.md`): the crossing itself is one of the quietest in the Balkans (~20–45 min; BA exit fast, ME entry wants vehicle docs + Green Card). The scary reputation belongs to the **roads**: Foča→Hum is narrow/partly single-lane (BA-side reconstruction stalled since 2022 — don't expect improvement by June), and the Piva canyon side is "tunnel after tunnel" — dozens, many **unlit and one-lane**. Fully paved, every car can do it (`contingency.md`). **Budget 2.5–3 h Šćepan Polje→Žabljak**, drive daylight only, lights on, honk before blind tunnels, avoid in heavy rain (landslide history). This explains the OSRM routing oddity in `drive-times.md`. The Tara rafting hub sits right at the border — pair the crossing with the rafting day.

### 1g. Tara rafting timing — **Day 8 (Šćepan Polje crossing day), NOT Day 10** (v2 correction)
**`critical-review.md` Finding 3 (2026-06-11) identifies a structural routing error in v1.** The v1 skeleton placed Tara rafting on Day 10 (Jun 25), requiring a ~2h backtrack from Žabljak to the rafting put-in at Brštanovica — the same area the group literally drives through on Day 8 when crossing the border. The logical fix: **raft on Day 8 morning as part of the border-crossing sequence.**

How it flows: Sarajevo (Day 7 overnight) → leave 07:30 → Foča 09:00 → start rafting Brštanovica 10:00–10:30 → finish at Šćepan Polje ~14:00–15:00 → cross BiH/ME border (operators are based right at the border) → Piva canyon → Žabljak by 18:00–19:00. The operators (Rafting Centar Drina-Tara, TaraSport) are based in Foča and the Brštanovica→Šćepan Polje section (18 km, 21 major rapids) is the standard one-day route.

**What this buys:** Day 10 becomes Đurđevića bridge → Ostrog → Skadar Lake — a more relaxed transit day. Durmitor gets its two nights intact for hiking. The v1 Day 10 (rafting from Žabljak backtrack + Ostrog + Skadar in one day) was a 12–14h day only doable with a 07:00 departure.

**Timing constraint:** Day 8 with rafting is a full day — be out of Sarajevo by 07:30, finish the Piva canyon drive in daylight (sunset ~20:40). The Piva tunnels are drive-daylight-only (see §1f). It's tight but fully doable in June. Source: `critical-review.md`, `me-deep2.md` §2.

> **Action: Book Tara rafting for Jun 23 (Day 8), not Jun 25.** Email office@raftingtara.com or info@tarasportrafting.com now — inside the booking window (see §6).

Bonus: Ston walls + Mali Ston oyster stands (~€1/oyster) sit right on the bridge approach (`hr-south-notes.md`, `food-notes.md`).

## 5. WEATHER/TIMING RULES (top 5 from `trip-ops.md`)

1. **Mountain hikes start at dawn.** Durmitor/Biokovo thunderstorms build 12:00–15:00 with zero shelter on the ridges; light by ~05:10 makes it easy. Out of camp 06:00–07:00, off high ground before noon. (Also a heat rule for Biokovo/Kotor Ladder/Sveti Ilija — midday on bare rock is genuinely dangerous.)
2. **Sightsee at the edges, swim in the middle.** Coast + Herzegovina middays run 30–35 °C; honeypots (Dubrovnik walls at 08:00 via Ploče gate, Mostar bridge before 09:00 or after 17:00 — softly lit 21:00 for overnighters) are before-9-or-after-17 plays. Midday = Kravica/Buna/beach. (**Krka removed** — see §1b.)
3. **Cross Debeli Brijeg (ME→HR) before 08:00 or after 21:00** — summer queues 1–4 h (up to 5), worst 10:00–14:00, EES biometrics add more. **For the Jun 27 return, use Vitaljina (Kobila/Konfin) back door via Molunat**, not Debeli Brijeg — passenger cars only, 15–45 min wait vs 1–4 h. `me-deep2.md` §7 verifies Vitaljina as open and running normally in 2026. Protects the Jun 28 flight.
4. **Žabljak tent nights are 6–10 °C (dips to ~5 °C) at 1,450 m.** 3-season bags (comfort 0/+5 °C) + insulated pads + beanies, or the camping pick in §3 becomes a bad night. Beach bags = cold.
5. **Keep Kotor on Jun 27** — the published cruise calendar shows **1 ship/170 pax** that day vs 7,500+ on Jun 21 & 25. The quietest marquee-site day of the fortnight. **Enter Kotor old town by 07:30** — fortress ticket booth opens ~07:00, climb before 09:00 when the first ship's tenders start unloading passengers. At ~€15pp (2026 confirmed, `me-deep2.md` §6), the free "Ladder of Kotor" alternative (bypasses ticket gate from outside the walls, same summit) saves €60 for 4. (Re-check the cruise calendar ~2 weeks out.)
6. **(v2 added) Vitaljina for Jun 27 crossing.** Confirmed: use Vitaljina, not Debeli Brijeg, for the final ME→HR crossing. See §5.3 above and `me-deep2.md` §7. Book to leave Kotor/Montenegro before 17:00 to avoid crossing in pitch dark on the Molunat road.

*Bonus moon rule:* new moon Jun 15 → **Jun 16–18 are the darkest skies of the trip — spend one of them at altitude (Zavižan/Biokovo)**; Durmitor on Jun 23–24 has a bright gibbous moon with only a ~02:00–03:15 dark window. The famous dark-sky stop is, on our dates, the wrong place for stars.
