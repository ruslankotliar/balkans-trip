## 1. OPEN-VARIANT VERDICTS

### 1a. Plitvice — **NO** (unanimous)
`hr-north-notes.md` recommends skipping outright: it costs **+4:21 of pure driving** (`drive-times.md`: Zadar→Plitvice→Krka→Split 6:33 vs 2:12 coastal), starts **1.5 h the wrong way (north)** inside a hard 1.5-usable-day window, costs ~€40 pp, is regimented (timed boardwalks, **no swimming**), and is the wrong vibe for an adventure group. **Krka** delivers the turquoise-waterfall payoff on the southbound line with **legal swimming at Roški slap/Stinice/Pisak** (swimming at Skradinski buk banned since 2021) — and even Krka is rated "defensible to skip both NPs" in favour of a Velebit/Zrmanja active day. If the group overrules: `trip-ops.md` says sleep nearby, book the timed-entry slot online, be inside before 08:00 (crowds peak ~13:00). **No session argued for Plitvice.** It stays a candidate pin only.

### 1b. Mountains vs coast, Zadar→Split — **mountains-lite: Paklenica day + inland line, but only if you do the stops**
`hr-north-notes.md`: the inland route via Knin costs **+1:28** (3:40 vs 2:12, `drive-times.md`) and the road alone is *not* worth it — the payoff is the chain it unlocks: **Zrmanja kayaking and/or Tulove grede → Pariževačka glavica viewpoint → (Cerovac caves) → Knin fortress** ("a real shame to miss"). Decision rule: doing Zrmanja/Tulove grede as the active day → go inland; skipping them → take the coast via Krka. Paklenica is cheap either way (0:54 from Zadar) and anchors "Plan M": Jun 16 PM camp Starigrad, Jun 17 gorge + Anića kuk (or Zrmanja kayak) → inland stops → night Split/Omiš. Islands (Dugi Otok/Kornati) **do not fit** — the car ferry forces a ~10 h commitment (`hr-north-notes.md`). `experiences-notes.md` adds a tiebreaker: **new moon Jun 15 → Jun 16–18 is the trip's best Milky Way window, and Velebit/Zavižan is the darkest accessible sky in Croatia** — a mountain night early on cashes that in; coast nights waste it.

### 1c. Korčula vs Mljet vs both — **Mljet overnight if one; 2-day Pelješac swing if two**
`hr-south-notes.md`: **Mljet is the fit for this group** (lakes kayak/bike, Montokuc, Odysseus Cave cliff jumping) but it must be an **overnight, not a day trip** — ferry lands at Sobra ~30 min from the NP and entry is steep (**€25 pp in June, −25 % after 16:00**). Korčula is the town+beach+wine pick with the cheapest, most frequent ferry (~20+/day); skip Vela Luka (far west, +1 h, "no better water"). **Both** is genuinely feasible because Orebić↔Prapratno is only **1:24** (`drive-times.md`): Ston walls + Mali Ston oysters + Dingač winery → Korčula day → Mljet overnight → Sobra→Prapratno → on to Bosnia. `logistics-notes.md` confirms Mljet sailings 5/day each way in June (07:00/10:15/13:00/17:00/20:30 out; 06:00/09:00/12:00/16:00/19:30 back), **car+4 ≈ €50 each way**, and — critically — **no car reservation exists on any of these lines** (first-come; queue 60+ min early; see §6).

> ⚠️ **CONTRADICTION — Korčula ferry price.** `hr-south-notes.md` says car €14.71–16.20 + adult €3.99 → **~€31** for car+4; `logistics-notes.md` says 2026 prices car €4.40 + adult €2.50 → **~€14–15**. Trust the logistics figures as newer (2026), but verify on jadrolinija.hr at booking — either way it's cheap and doesn't change the verdict.
> ⚠️ **CONTRADICTION — Ploče–Trpanj price.** `hr-south-notes.md`: car €25.20 + adult €6.70 (~€52); `logistics-notes.md`: 2026 June *low-season* car €18.00 + adult €4.50 (~€36) — hr-south appears to have used 2025/high-season rates. Departure times agree (10:30/14:15/19:30); both sessions agree the verdict: **don't use this ferry for Jun 18** (§2).

### 1d. Sarajevo overnight — **YES, 1 night** (unanimous, and effectively free)
`ba-notes.md` verdict: do it. `drive-times.md`: via-Sarajevo costs **only +13 min driving** vs the "direct" Konjic→Tjentište road — and `ba-notes.md` road research shows the shortcut is remote high-plateau terrain ("rough with rocks, mud… not suitable for ordinary cars") while the M18/M20 through Sarajevo is the paved route everyone actually drives. So Sarajevo is the *real* road anyway. What it buys: Baščaršija + ćevapi (Željo/Petica), **Trebević cable car + the abandoned 1984 bobsled track** (the trip's signature weird highlight per `experiences-notes.md`), Tunnel of Hope, and "the best genuine night out of the Balkans leg" with €2.30 beers. €50-for-4 apartments are realistic (`ba-notes.md`, `airbnb-wishlist-notes.md`: The Pearl Downtown €48 ★4.95). Bonus: **Garden of Dreams festival Jun 21–23** is the only in-window, on-route festival of the whole trip (`experiences-notes.md`) — note `logistics-notes.md`'s events sweep missed it and only flagged Baščaršija Nights (opens Jun 30, after us).

### 1e. Deep south Bar/Ulcinj/Ada Bojana — **marginal: half-day Stari Bar dip yes, full day only for kitesurfing**
`me-notes.md`: the loop costs **+1.5–2 h driving** (`drive-times.md`: 3:10 vs 1:38). The one genuinely special thing is **Stari Bar** (abandoned fortress town, rating 5) + the 2,240-year-old olive tree — capturable in a ~1.5 h near-en-route dip on the Skadar→coast day. The unique full-day draw is **kitesurfing at Velika Plaža/Ada Bojana (Kiteloop school)**, but June thermals are lighter than mid-summer and the school has low-wind complaints — no guaranteed session. Ulcinj is "a lesser Kotor"; Ada Bojana's beach is nudist + crowded in June. Forum consensus: "if you have extra time," not a must. Sozina tunnel toll applies (~€2.50 per `contingency.md` vs ~€3.50–4 per `logistics-notes.md` — minor discrepancy, carry small notes).

### 1f. Šćepan Polje border crossing — **GO: fine in a normal rental, just slow; daylight only**
Three sessions agree (`ba-notes.md`, `me-notes.md`, `logistics-notes.md`): the crossing itself is one of the quietest in the Balkans (~20–45 min; BA exit fast, ME entry wants vehicle docs + Green Card). The scary reputation belongs to the **roads**: Foča→Hum is narrow/partly single-lane (BA-side reconstruction stalled since 2022 — don't expect improvement by June), and the Piva canyon side is "tunnel after tunnel" — dozens, many **unlit and one-lane**. Fully paved, every car can do it (`contingency.md`). **Budget 2.5–3 h Šćepan Polje→Žabljak**, drive daylight only, lights on, honk before blind tunnels, avoid in heavy rain (landslide history). This explains the OSRM routing oddity in `drive-times.md`. The Tara rafting hub sits right at the border — pair the crossing with the rafting day.

### Other cross-session flags (smaller, but know them)
- **Lukomir road**: `ba-notes.md` says a normal rental **will** make the ~11–14 km gravel in dry weather (slow); `contingency.md` says **not recommended in a 2WD rental** (gravel may void insurance + fake-damage risk) — do a guided jeep tour or stop at Umoljani. Resolve against the rental contract's prohibited-surfaces clause at pickup.
- **Lovćen mausoleum fee**: `me-places` pin says ~€3 pp; `hikes-notes.md` flags 2025 sources at **~€8 pp**.
- **Velika Paklenica fee**: pin says ~€10; `hikes-notes.md` reports 2026 adult €8 — treat as €8–10.
- **Trebinje→Mostar time**: `ba-notes.md` ~1h45–2h vs `drive-times.md` OSRM 2:17 — plan ~2 h.

## 2. JUN 18 AIRPORT MATH (the one hard deadline)

From `logistics-notes.md` §1 (seconded by `hr-south-notes.md`):

- **Route: Makarska → D8/A1 → Pelješac Bridge → Ston → Dubrovnik → airport (Čilipi, 22 km SE of the city).** Toll-free, zero borders, zero ferries; open normally in June (renovation starts Oct 2026).
- **Pure driving: ~3 h 30** (Makarska→Dubrovnik 2:52/187 km + airport 0:36/22 km). With June Riviera traffic + one stop, **budget ~4 h door-to-door**.
- **To be at the airport by ~19:00, leave Makarska by 14:30–15:00.** That leaves the morning free for a Punta Rata swim and/or a Biokovo/Sveti Jure run.
- **Rejected alternatives:** Ploče–Trpanj ferry — only 3 sailings/day in June (10:30/14:15/19:30), 1 h crossing, then you still drive Pelješac: "slower, schedule-locked, saves nothing." Neum corridor — ~12 min shorter on paper but a Schengen↔BA crossing with summer waits up to ~2 h: "unacceptable gamble before a flight pickup" (`hr-south-notes.md`).
- **That night:** sleep towards **Trebinje** (58 min from the airport via the quick Ivanica–Gornji Brgat crossing, `drive-times.md`/`ba-notes.md`) — the cheapest good zone on the whole trip (§3). Bonus: Ston walls + Mali Ston oyster stands (~€1/oyster) sit right on the bridge approach (`hr-south-notes.md`, `food-notes.md`).

## 5. WEATHER/TIMING RULES (top 5 from `trip-ops.md`)

1. **Mountain hikes start at dawn.** Durmitor/Biokovo thunderstorms build 12:00–15:00 with zero shelter on the ridges; light by ~05:10 makes it easy. Out of camp 06:00–07:00, off high ground before noon. (Also a heat rule for Biokovo/Kotor Ladder/Sveti Ilija — midday on bare rock is genuinely dangerous.)
2. **Sightsee at the edges, swim in the middle.** Coast + Herzegovina middays run 30–35 °C; honeypots (Dubrovnik walls at 08:00 via Ploče gate, Mostar bridge before 09:00 or after 17:00 — softly lit 21:00 for overnighters, Krka at opening) are before-9-or-after-17 plays. Midday = Kravica/Buna/beach.
3. **Cross Debeli Brijeg (ME→HR) before 08:00 or after 21:00** — summer queues 1–4 h (up to 5), worst 10:00–14:00, EES biometrics add more — or take the **Vitaljina/Kobila (Konfin) back door via Molunat** (cars only, ~1 h max). Protects the Jun 28 flight.
4. **Žabljak tent nights are 6–10 °C (dips to ~5 °C) at 1,450 m.** 3-season bags (comfort 0/+5 °C) + insulated pads + beanies, or the camping pick in §3 becomes a bad night. Beach bags = cold.
5. **Keep Kotor on Jun 27** — the published cruise calendar shows **1 ship/170 pax** that day vs 7,500+ on Jun 21 & 25. The quietest marquee-site day of the fortnight; climb the fortress early anyway for heat. (Re-check the calendar ~2 weeks out.)

*Bonus moon rule:* new moon Jun 15 → **Jun 16–18 are the darkest skies of the trip — spend one of them at altitude (Zavižan/Biokovo)**; Durmitor on Jun 23–24 has a bright gibbous moon with only a ~02:00–03:15 dark window. The famous dark-sky stop is, on our dates, the wrong place for stars.

## 6. BOOKING ACTIONS (time-sensitive, consolidated)

**Key ferry fact first (`logistics-notes.md`):** the Mljet (Prapratno–Sobra), Korčula (Orebić–Dominče) and Ploče–Trpanj car ferries take **NO car reservations — first-come queue only**. Buy date tickets online to skip the kiosk, then **queue 60+ min early** (Mljet 13:00/17:00 sailings on sunny weekends are the risk; Korčula runs so often a miss costs ~30 min). Plan Mljet as early-boat-over, mid-morning-back.

### Book / arrange NOW
- **Rental paperwork:** confirm Green Card explicitly lists **BA and ME**, cross-border permission for all three countries written on the contract, cross-border + one-way fees in writing (`logistics-notes.md`, `contingency.md`).
- **Adventure-tier travel insurance** with rafting/canyoning/via-ferrata declared at purchase (EHIC is useless in BA/ME; cliff jumping ≈ uninsured regardless) (`contingency.md`).
- **Žabljak beds** — high-season mountain town, the €65 cheap end goes first; book the 2 Durmitor nights before anything else (`airbnb-wishlist-notes.md`).
- **Thin-market Airbnb gems if wanted** (free-cancellation rates; none currently show June availability — act or drop): **Kostela Stone House** (★4.94/157), **Skadar village houses Rvaši/Ivan's**, **Mountain Camp Burrows**, **Mountain Lodge Šavnik**, **Zen House Sarajevo** (only if Sarajevo locked) (`airbnb-wishlist-notes.md`). Mostar/Sarajevo/Trebinje/Budva/Kotor are thick commodity markets — fine to wait.
- **ARK D-0 bunker (Konjic):** pre-book a slot — names/passport numbers required, fixed tour times (`ba-notes.md`).
- **Biokovo toll road:** timed online slot, 20 cars/hour cap, €15 pp (`hr-south-notes.md`).
- **Perućica guided entry** (16 people/day quota) if the group wants the primeval forest; **Tvrdoš winery tasting** needs booking weeks ahead (`ba-notes.md`).
