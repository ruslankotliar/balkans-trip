## 1. OPEN-VARIANT VERDICTS

### 1a. Plitvice вАФ **NO** (unanimous)
`hr-north-notes.md` recommends skipping outright: it costs **+4:21 of pure driving** (`drive-times.md`: ZadarвЖТPlitviceвЖТKrkaвЖТSplit 6:33 vs 2:12 coastal), starts **1.5 h the wrong way (north)** inside a hard 1.5-usable-day window, costs ~вВђ40 pp, is regimented (timed boardwalks, **no swimming**), and is the wrong vibe for an adventure group. **Krka** delivers the turquoise-waterfall payoff on the southbound line with **legal swimming at Ro≈°ki slap/Stinice/Pisak** (swimming at Skradinski buk banned since 2021) вАФ and even Krka is rated "defensible to skip both NPs" in favour of a Velebit/Zrmanja active day. If the group overrules: `trip-ops.md` says sleep nearby, book the timed-entry slot online, be inside before 08:00 (crowds peak ~13:00). **No session argued for Plitvice.** It stays a candidate pin only.

### 1b. Mountains vs coast, ZadarвЖТSplit вАФ **mountains-lite: Paklenica day + inland line, but only if you do the stops**
`hr-north-notes.md`: the inland route via Knin costs **+1:28** (3:40 vs 2:12, `drive-times.md`) and the road alone is *not* worth it вАФ the payoff is the chain it unlocks: **Zrmanja kayaking and/or Tulove grede вЖТ Pari≈ЊevaƒНka glavica viewpoint вЖТ (Cerovac caves) вЖТ Knin fortress** ("a real shame to miss"). Decision rule: doing Zrmanja/Tulove grede as the active day вЖТ go inland; skipping them вЖТ take the coast via Krka. Paklenica is cheap either way (0:54 from Zadar) and anchors "Plan M": Jun 16 PM camp Starigrad, Jun 17 gorge + AniƒЗa kuk (or Zrmanja kayak) вЖТ inland stops вЖТ night Split/Omi≈°. Islands (Dugi Otok/Kornati) **do not fit** вАФ the car ferry forces a ~10 h commitment (`hr-north-notes.md`). `experiences-notes.md` adds a tiebreaker: **new moon Jun 15 вЖТ Jun 16вАУ18 is the trip's best Milky Way window, and Velebit/Zavi≈Њan is the darkest accessible sky in Croatia** вАФ a mountain night early on cashes that in; coast nights waste it.

> **вЫФ Krka NP: REJECTED for this trip.** `day2-decision.md` (2026-06-11) closes the debate: **swimming at Skradinski Buk has been banned since 2021** вАФ what remains is a crowded boardwalk walk at **вВђ40pp (вВђ160 for 4)** with no swimming at the main falls. Legal swimming is only at Ro≈°ki Slap, requiring a separate 4h boat excursion (+вВђ20pp). Recent TripAdvisor reviews: "not worth the вВђ40 entrance fee." **Day 2 = Omi≈°: Cetina extreme canyoning (~вВђ70pp, 12m jumps + 55m rappel + natural pools) or zipline (~вВђ80pp, 8 lines, 150m above the canyon). Both are a perfect group fit, and you arrive at the overnight anyway.** The combination day (Krka + Omi≈°) does not work in practice вАФ either timing slips or you spend вВђ160 on a walk and rush to Omi≈°. Cut Krka; do Omi≈° properly. Kravica Falls in Bosnia (Day 6) is "what Krka used to be" at ~вВђ10pp with full swimming. Source: `day2-decision.md`.

### 1c. KorƒНula vs Mljet vs both вАФ **Mljet overnight if one; 2-day Pelje≈°ac swing if two**

### 1f. ≈†ƒЗepan Polje border crossing вАФ **GO: fine in a normal rental, just slow; daylight only**
Three sessions agree (`ba-notes.md`, `me-notes.md`, `logistics-notes.md`): the crossing itself is one of the quietest in the Balkans (~20вАУ45 min; BA exit fast, ME entry wants vehicle docs + Green Card). The scary reputation belongs to the **roads**: FoƒНaвЖТHum is narrow/partly single-lane (BA-side reconstruction stalled since 2022 вАФ don't expect improvement by June), and the Piva canyon side is "tunnel after tunnel" вАФ dozens, many **unlit and one-lane**. Fully paved, every car can do it (`contingency.md`). **Budget 2.5вАУ3 h ≈†ƒЗepan PoljeвЖТ≈љabljak**, drive daylight only, lights on, honk before blind tunnels, avoid in heavy rain (landslide history). This explains the OSRM routing oddity in `drive-times.md`. The Tara rafting hub sits right at the border вАФ pair the crossing with the rafting day.

### 1g. Tara rafting timing вАФ **Day 8 (≈†ƒЗepan Polje crossing day), NOT Day 10** (v2 correction)
**`critical-review.md` Finding 3 (2026-06-11) identifies a structural routing error in v1.** The v1 skeleton placed Tara rafting on Day 10 (Jun 25), requiring a ~2h backtrack from ≈љabljak to the rafting put-in at Br≈°tanovica вАФ the same area the group literally drives through on Day 8 when crossing the border. **What this buys:** Day 10 becomes ƒРurƒСeviƒЗa bridge вЖТ Ostrog вЖТ Skadar Lake вАФ a more relaxed transit day. Durmitor gets its two nights intact for hiking. The v1 Day 10 (rafting from ≈љabljak backtrack + Ostrog + Skadar in one day) was a 12вАУ14h day only doable with a 07:00 departure.

**Timing constraint:** Day 8 with rafting is a full day вАФ be out of Sarajevo by 07:30, finish the Piva canyon drive in daylight (sunset ~20:40). The Piva tunnels are drive-daylight-only (see ¬І1f). It's tight but fully doable in June. Source: `critical-review.md`, `me-deep2.md` ¬І2.

> **Action: Book Tara rafting for Jun 23 (Day 8), not Jun 25.** Email office@raftingtara.com or info@tarasportrafting.com now вАФ inside the booking window (see ¬І6).

## 5. WEATHER/TIMING RULES (top 5 from `trip-ops.md`)

1. **Mountain hikes start at dawn.** Durmitor/Biokovo thunderstorms build 12:00вАУ15:00 with zero shelter on the ridges; light by ~05:10 makes it easy. 2. **Sightsee at the edges, swim in the middle.** Coast + Herzegovina middays run 30вАУ35 ¬∞C; honeypots (Dubrovnik walls at 08:00 via PloƒНe gate, Mostar bridge before 09:00 or after 17:00 вАФ softly lit 21:00 for overnighters) are before-9-or-after-17 plays. Protects the Jun 28 flight.
4. **≈љabljak tent nights are 6вАУ10 ¬∞C (dips to ~5 ¬∞C) at 1,450 m.** 3-season bags (comfort 0/+5 ¬∞C) + insulated pads + beanies, or the camping pick in ¬І3 becomes a bad night. Beach bags = cold.
5. **Keep Kotor on Jun 27** вАФ the published cruise calendar shows **1 ship/170 pax** that day vs 7,500+ on Jun 21 & 25. 6. **(v2 added) Vitaljina for Jun 27 crossing.** Confirmed: use Vitaljina, not Debeli Brijeg, for the final MEвЖТHR crossing. See ¬І5.3 above and `me-deep2.md` ¬І7. Book to leave Kotor/Montenegro before 17:00 to avoid crossing in pitch dark on the Molunat road.
