# Logistics notes (research session — fetched 2026-06-10)

Covers ferries, borders & rental-car paperwork, camping legality, money/tolls/fuel, events, and connectivity for the Jun 16–28 2026 trip. Numbers are **2026 where published, 2025 where 2026 isn't out yet (flagged)**. Sources at the bottom of each section.

**Three headline takeaways:**
1. **Skip the Ploče–Trpanj ferry for the Jun 18 Dubrovnik run.** The toll-free Pelješac Bridge (open in June 2026; renovation only starts Oct 2026) bypasses Bosnia/Neum with zero borders and is faster and more reliable than the 3×/day ferry. Use the ferry only if Trpanj/Pelješac itself is the goal.
2. **The real time-sink is the ME→HR border at Debeli Brijeg/Karasovići on Jun 27/28** — 1–4 h queues, worst 10:00–14:00. Cross before 08:00 / after 21:00, or use the **Vitaljina/Kobila (Konfin)** back-door crossing (cars only, ~1 h max). Build this into the last day so we don't miss the flight.
3. **EES is live (since 10 Apr 2026).** Croatia's land borders with BA and ME are full Schengen external borders with biometric registration — add buffer at every HR↔BA and HR↔ME crossing this June.

## 1. Car ferries

All car ferries below are **Jadrolinija** (state operator) unless noted. Croatia's local car-ferry lines (Prapratno–Sobra, Ploče–Trpanj, Orebić–Dominče) are **first-come-first-served for vehicles — you cannot reserve a car space.** You can buy a ticket online in advance, but it's date-only and does **not** guarantee a deck slot; boarding is by physical queue order. Buy online anyway to skip the kiosk line.

### (a) PRIORITY — Prapratno (Pelješac) ↔ Sobra (Mljet) — line 832, ~45 min
Our main island candidate. June sits in Jadrolinija's "shoulder" season block **29 May – 2 Jul**, which runs **5 sailings/day each way** (peak Jul–Aug bumps to 6):

| Direction | June 2026 departures |
|---|---|
| Prapratno → Sobra | 07:00, 10:15, 13:00, 17:00, 20:30 |
| Sobra → Prapratno | 06:00, 09:00, 12:00, 16:00, 19:30 |

**2026 prices (high-season rate, applies 29.05–27.09):** adult €6.10 · child 3–12 €2.00 · car ≤5 m/≤2 m €25.50.
→ **4 adults + 1 car = €49.90 each way (~€100 return).**

**Booking / chaos:** No car reservation possible (first-come). In peak summer car spaces "sell out by queue order" — travelers report needing to be **in the vehicle queue 60–120 min before departure**, more on weekends/changeover days. Rough weather can delay the Sobra side ~60 min. June is shoulder season so less brutal than Aug, but the 13:00 and 17:00 sailings on a sunny weekend can still fill — arrive ~60 min early to be safe.
**Tip:** if camping/overnighting on Mljet, take an early car ferry over and a mid-morning one back to dodge the queue.

### (b) PRIORITY — Ploče ↔ Trpanj (Pelješac) — line 633, ~60 min — *Jun 18 decision*
June is the off-peak block (valid to 2 Jul): **only 3 sailings/day each way** (peak Jul–Aug = 7):

| Direction | June 2026 departures |
|---|---|
| Ploče → Trpanj | 10:30, 14:15, 19:30 |
| Trpanj → Ploče | 06:45, 12:00, 17:15 |

**2026 low-season prices (June):** adult €4.50 · child €2.30 · car ≤5 m €18.00. → **4 + car ≈ €36 each way.** (Some listings still show the 2025 high-season car fare ~€22.94.)

**VERDICT for Jun 18: don't use it to reach Dubrovnik — drive the Pelješac Bridge.** Since the bridge opened (Jul 2022) this ferry's traffic dropped ~95% (so no boarding chaos anymore), but with only **3 sailings/day** in June the schedule is rigid, the crossing is a full hour, and you still have to drive Pelješac afterward. The bridge is toll-free, crosses **no border**, and is simply faster and more predictable. Keep the ferry as a backup only if we decide to base on Pelješac.

### (c) Orebić (Pelješac) ↔ Dominče (Korčula) — line 634, ~15–20 min
Relevant if Korčula is in. Runs **~05:30 → 22:30**, **dozens of departures/day** in high season (a few even after midnight) — frequent enough that car queues are rarely a real problem, though it's still first-come.
**2026 high-season prices:** adult €2.50 · car €4.40 → **4 + car ≈ €14–15 each way** (cheap; it's a short shuttle). Buy the car ticket online in advance for peak days to avoid being bumped when full.
**Don't confuse** with the foot-passenger Orebić↔Korčula-town boat (line 614, run by *G&V Line Iadera*) — that one takes no cars.

### (d) Backups
- **Zadar ↔ Brbinj (Dugi Otok)** — car ferry line 434, ~1 h 35–1 h 50, high season 5–6/day. (The faster Zadar–Božava–Brbinj catamaran, line 9404, is **foot-passengers only — no cars.**) Foot fares ~€3.85–6.37. Only relevant if we do a Dugi Otok/Telašćica day on days 1–2.
- **Split ↔ Supetar (Brač)** — ~50 min, one of the busiest/most frequent lines; **Split ↔ Stari Grad (Hvar)** — line 635, ~2 h, high season 6/day, foot €5.71–8.10, car extra. These only matter if a northern-island day gets added on the way south (CLAUDE.md flags Hvar/Brač as probably not fitting days 1–3).
- **Drvenik ↔ Sućuraj (east tip of Hvar)** — short hop (~35 min) from the Makarska Riviera coast; frequent shuttle, useful only if approaching Hvar from the south. *(2026 schedule not pulled — verify if it becomes relevant.)*

### Jun 18 timing math — Makarska → Dubrovnik airport by ~18:00–20:00
Airport is **Čilipi, ~22 km SE of Dubrovnik** (the wrong side of the city — past the old town).

- **Recommended (bridge, no border, no ferry):** Makarska → D8/A1 → **Pelješac Bridge** → Ston → Dubrovnik → airport.
  - Makarska → Dubrovnik ≈ **2 h 52 / 187 km** (OSRM, already via bridge) + Dubrovnik → airport **+0 h 36 / 22 km** = **~3 h 30 pure driving, zero borders, zero ferry.**
  - June coastal traffic on the Riviera + one stop → budget **~4 h door-to-door**.
  - To land at the airport by **~19:00, leave Makarska by ~14:30–15:00.** That still leaves the morning for a Punta Rata swim and/or a Biokovo/Sveti Jure run before heading off.
- **Ploče–Trpanj ferry alternative:** would force catching the **10:30 or 14:15** sailing, +1 h crossing, then Trpanj → Dubrovnik (~1 h 30 via the bridge anyway). Net: **slower, schedule-locked, and saves nothing.** Don't.

## 2. Borders & rental car

### Schengen + EES — the 2026 change everyone misses
- **Croatia has been in Schengen since Jan 2023**, so HR↔BA and HR↔ME land borders are **full external Schengen borders** — systematic passport checks every time.
- **EES (Entry/Exit System) went live 10 April 2026.** Non-EU/EEA travelers get **fingerprints + facial scan registered on first entry**; this is adding time at HR external crossings through 2026. **EU/EEA citizens** just show passport/ID and use the faster lane. Whatever the group's passports are, expect HR↔BA / HR↔ME crossings to be slower this June than in prior years.
- BA and ME are **not** in Schengen/EU — their own posts, own (simpler) checks, own stamps.

### HR ↔ BA crossings on our route
- **Dubrovnik → Kravica/Mostar:** main crossing is **Doljani (HR) / Metković** — well-staffed and efficient, typically **15–45 min**; the one predictable jam is **Fri 15:00–18:00**. Thanks to the bridge, this route now crosses the border **only once** (the old Neum double-crossing is gone).
- **Kamensko / Bijača:** usually **<15 min**.
- Major crossings (Doljani, Kamensko, Karasovići) are open **24 h**.

### BA ↔ ME via Šćepan Polje (Hum / Šćepan Polje) — the group's wanted route
- **Border itself:** remote and quick. Real reports: Bosnia exit = fast passport check, no stamp, no vehicle docs asked; Montenegro entry = they want **vehicle documents** + give an entry stamp, done in minutes. Little/no congestion. The Tara-canyon bridge has a **12-tonne limit** (irrelevant for a car; rules out big motorhomes).
- **Road state — important:** the **Foča → Hum** approach is **narrow, in places single-lane** (you stop and wait for oncoming traffic), with derelict war-era buildings. On the Montenegrin side the **Piva canyon road (Šćepan Polje → Plužine → Mratinje dam) is "tunnel after tunnel"** — dozens of them, many **unlit and one-lane** carved into the cliff. Spectacular but **slow**: budget **~2.5–3 h Šćepan Polje → Žabljak** and drive carefully (lights on, honk before blind tunnels). This explains why OSRM refused the short route in `drive-times.md` — the road is real and driveable in a normal rental car, just slow. BA-side reconstruction has crept along since 2022 (only ~5 km done; the main 13 km + new bridge still unfunded as of 2025), so don't expect it improved by June 2026.
- **Net:** totally doable in a standard car, scenically worth it, but pad the schedule and don't plan to do it after dark for the first time.

### ME ↔ HR — Debeli Brijeg / Karasovići — *the big one, Jun 27/28*
- **Summer queues 1–4 h, up to 4–5 h on peak days (Fri/Sun worst).** Worst window **10:00–14:00** (Dubrovnik day-trippers pile in). Open 24 h.
- **Cross before 08:00 or after 21:00.**
- **Back-door alternative: Vitaljina / Kobila (a.k.a. Konfin)** — leave the Adriatic highway via **Molunat** on local roads. **Cars and light vehicles only**, far quieter — **max ~1 h even on a peak weekend.** Strongly recommended for the Kotor→Dubrovnik return so we don't risk the Jun 28 flight.
- **Check live before driving:** Montenegro MUP cameras `kamere.mup.gov.me` (refresh ~40 s); wait-time trackers `nakordoni.eu` and `borderalarm.com`.

### Rental-car cross-border paperwork (what people actually get burned by)
Our Zadar→Dubrovnik one-way cross-border rental is arranged — but **verify these before leaving Zadar:**
- **Green Card** (international motor-insurance proof) is **essential** for BA and ME (non-EU). Some agencies include it, some issue on request, some charge. **Confirm the Green Card explicitly lists BA *and* ME.**
- The rental paperwork must state **cross-border permission for HR, BA and ME** — tell them every country you'll enter. Crossing without permission noted = denied at the border / insurance void.
- **Cross-border fee:** ~€20 for EU-to-EU, **~€50 for crossing into BA/ME** (varies €10–50 by supplier) — may already be in our one-way deal; confirm.
- **Carry at every border:** passports, driving licence, rental agreement, Green Card.
- **Neum corridor:** with the bridge we never touch it. (For the record: it's a ~9 km strip of Bosnian coast on the old D8; quick stamps, but obsolete now.)

## 3. Camping rules

### Wild camping legality (real reports)
- **Croatia 🚫:** Officially **banned everywhere** — tents and bivouacs, private *and* state land, no "free-standing" exception. Fines reported **~€200–1,300** (commonly up to ~€400) and **consistently enforced on the coast and in national parks.** Inland/away from tourist zones it's often tolerated for one night or you're simply asked to move on without a fine. → **Don't wild camp on the Dalmatian coast or in NPs; use campsites there.**
- **Bosnia ⚠️ tolerated:** Not officially allowed but **most authorities/residents tolerate it** outside towns and tourist areas. **Landmine risk** in remote areas (BiH is among Europe's most-mined countries) — only camp on obviously used ground, never wander into unmarked rural fields/woods.
- **Montenegro — Durmitor exception 🏕️:** Wild camping not officially allowed generally (fines up to **€1,500** in tourist zones/NPs if caught), **but wild camping *is* permitted inside Durmitor NP** — a **ranger comes around ~07:00 and charges ~€3/vehicle** on top of the **~€5 NP entry**. Elsewhere tolerated if discreet and away from beaches/centers. (Same landmine caveat applies to a few remote ME areas.)

### June availability & turning up unannounced
- **Coastal/island campsites in Dalmatia book up in peak**, and June is shoulder-into-peak; **direct-sea-access sites especially** sell out, sometimes months ahead. On the HR coast/islands (incl. Mljet) **book popular sites in advance**, especially for weekends — don't count on walking in.
- **Inland HR, Bosnia and Montenegro sites are far more relaxed** — usually fine to just turn up.
- **park4night etiquette:** great for finding tolerated/legal pitches, but **many listed HR spots are technically illegal** — arrive late, leave early, one night, no chairs/awnings/BBQ sprawl, leave no trace. Don't treat a park4night pin on the HR coast as permission.

### Camp prices for 4 + tent(s) + car (see also §4)
HR coastal/island ~**€40–60**/night (Croatia averages ~€40, peak); **Bosnia ~€19–20**; Montenegro mid (incl. the Durmitor ranger+entry ~€8 if wild-camping there).

## 4. Money

### Tolls & vignettes
- **Croatia: NO vignette.** A1 uses the **ticket system** — take a ticket entering, pay leaving; **cash (€) and cards accepted** at all plazas. (A free-flow digital system is planned by late 2026 but **not** in effect this June.)
- **A1 currently ends at Čarapine (near Ploče).** **Zadar (istok) → Čarapine ≈ €15.60** for a Category I car (Zagreb→Zadar €18.20, Zagreb→Čarapine €33.80 → difference). An ENC transponder gives ~21.7% off but isn't worth it for a single trip.
- **Pelješac Bridge: completely TOLL-FREE** for all vehicles, and crosses no border. Open normally in June 2026 — the renovation that brings single-lane works only **starts Oct 2026** (deliberately after peak season).
- **Bosnia:** no vignette; no toll on our route (the only BiH motorway tolls are around Sarajevo's A1, e.g. Sarajevo–Zenica, a couple of €).
- **Montenegro:** no vignette. The one notable toll is the **Sožina tunnel** on the Podgorica–Sutomore/Bar road (~€3.50–4 for a car) — relevant only if we do the **deep-south leg** via that tunnel. *(Verify current price.)*

### Currencies & cash
- **Croatia: EUR** (since Jan 2023). **Montenegro: EUR** (uses it unilaterally, not in the EU). **Bosnia: Convertible Mark (KM / BAM)**, fixed **1 EUR = 1.95583 BAM**.
- **Cards** widely accepted in cities and tourist spots (Sarajevo, Mostar, the coast). **Carry cash** for: rural Bosnia, markets, small konobas, **campsites, NP entry, ferry kiosks, the Durmitor ranger, parking machines.** In BA get **KM from an ATM** — EUR is sometimes taken at tourist spots but at poor rates.

### Typical costs (recent traveler reports)
- **Campsite (4 + tent + car):** HR coast ~€40–60 · BA ~€19–20 · ME mid.
- **Beer (draft):** BA €1.50–2.50 (Sarajevsko/Nektar) · HR €2–4.50 (pint ~€4) · ME mid.
- **Food:** Bosnia is cheap (a full day's food/drink ~€18 is doable; ćevapi is the budget hero). **Croatia is now mid-range, not the bargain it once was;** Bosnia is clearly the cheapest of the three, Montenegro in between.

## 6. Connectivity (SIM / eSIM / roaming)

- **Croatia: EU roaming applies** — an EU/EEA SIM or plan works at home rates. No action needed for the HR legs.
- **Bosnia & Montenegro: NOT EU/EEA → no EU roaming.** EU SIMs hit **expensive international roaming** there. (BA & ME have signed Western-Balkans "roam like at home" among themselves and an EU roaming-reduction deal is *phasing in*, but as of 2025/26 **EU↔BA/ME roaming is not free** — confirm with your own carrier.)
- **Recommended: eSIM.** Practical combo:
  - **Croatia** → covered by your normal EU plan / a Europe eSIM.
  - **Bosnia + Montenegro** → a **Holafly "Balkans" regional eSIM** covers both (Albania, Kosovo, BA, N. Macedonia, Serbia, Montenegro) — but **does *not* include Croatia** (Croatia is in their Europe plan). **Airalo** also sells cheap per-country BA/ME eSIMs (BA from ~$4.50; Holafly unlimited BA ~$9.90+).
  - Cleanest: keep your EU SIM for Croatia, add **one Balkans eSIM for the BA+ME stretch.**
- **Physical SIM** alternative: buy local in Mostar/Sarajevo (BH Telecom / m:tel) and in Montenegro (Telenor / Crnogorski Telekom / m:tel) — cheap, but for a short visit the eSIM avoids the hassle.
- Practical: download **offline maps** (the Piva canyon, Durmitor and inland Bosnia have patchy coverage) and screenshot ferry/border info before you lose signal.
