## Top 5 findings strong enough to CHANGE the route shape

### 1. The Eastern Montenegro swing (day 10) — the strongest case in this file
**Replace** Žabljak → Šavnik → Ostrog → Podgorica (3:16) **with** Žabljak → Đurđevića Tara bridge (0:32, already planned for rafting/zipline) → **Tara canyon road to Mojkovac** (dangerousroads.org: "this drive is well worth the time", ~50-60 min of gorge-and-waterfall driving along the deepest canyon in Europe) → **Biogradska Gora** lake loop (1h, €4-5) → Kolašin → **Morača/Platije gorge** (the E80 itself is a spectacle) → Podgorica. OSRM total ≈ 5:48, realistic ≈ 4:30-5:00. Net cost **+2.5-3.5h** because Ostrog becomes a +2:08 round-trip spur from Podgorica — or slots into day-11 morning before Skadar (Ostrog↔Podgorica 1:04 each way), or gets dropped (group call: it's a *shortlist* wish, so keep it as the morning spur).
Why it wins: the skeleton's day 10 is a pure transfer; the swing converts it into arguably the best driving day of the trip, threads the **Mrtvica trailhead** (case 3 below) at zero extra driving, and Tara rafting at the bridge stays exactly where it was. Skip-friendly too: every stop on it is individually droppable.

### 2. Trnovačko jezero — half-day insert into day 8 (Sutjeska)
The heart-shaped lake everyone photographs in this region is **not** on the skeleton — and it's only a half day off it: Tjentište → Prijevor ~18km rough gravel (~1h each way, "drivable by a regular car... slowly"; 4x4 transfers available at Tjentište), then a well-marked ~2h hike each way; ranger collects €1-3 at the lake. Requires leaving Mostar/Konjic early on day 8 so the Šćepan Polje crossing + Piva canyon still fit before dark — or sleeping at Tjentište (`ba-tjentiste-mladost` exists) and shifting Durmitor by half a day. Group fit is perfect (young, hike-hungry, "meditation on a mountain"). Prijevor itself adds the Perućica-rainforest viewpoint and Maglić walls for free. Risk to manage: the gravel road in a rental — drive it slowly or buy the 4x4 transfer.

### 3. Mrtvica canyon — the conditional hike (rides on case 1)
136 AllTrails reviews, "worth the effort" consensus, ice-blue water, Kapija želja rock gate. Trailhead at Međuriječje sits ON the eastern-swing road — zero added driving. But: flood-destroyed bridges still unrebuilt as of June 2025, ropes on the last 200m, waymarks fade — GPX mandatory, dry weather only. Decision logic: if the swing happens AND legs are fresh after Durmitor, do the short version (to the Gate and back, ~3-4h); otherwise admire the Morača from the car. It competes directly with Durmitor fatigue, hence "conditional".

### 4. Prokletije / Grebaje valley — the big honest trade
The community is consistent: Grebaje under the Karanfili spires out-dramatizes Durmitor ("raw intensity found nowhere else in the Balkans"; local press: on par with the best Julian Alps valleys; Volušnica→Talijanka = "the most famous 360° panorama in the Balkans"). The price is equally consistent: Podgorica→Grebaje 2:05 one-way, an overnight in Gusinje, and a full hike day = **+1.5-2 days** that must come out of the deep-south coast (Bar/Ulcinj/Ada Bojana, itself an open +2h variant) AND a Budva/Kotor night. Argument FOR: this group ranks adventure over beach bars, and Durmitor (2 days) + Prokletije (1.5 days) makes the trip mountains-defining. Argument AGAINST: day 12 border-queue timing to Dubrovnik gets tight, weather risk in big mountains can torch the investment, and the 4th traveler joined for the coast too. Verdict: present to the group as an either/or against the deep south — don't decide unilaterally.

### 5. Day-6 Herzegovina re-route via Trebinje–Stolac (M6)
If the post-pickup night lands near Trebinje (already an open skeleton question — airport is 20km that way), the natural Mostar approach is the M6 through Ljubinje and Stolac, which threads **Radimlja stećci (UNESCO, +0:00, literally on the roadside)** → optional **Hutovo Blato boat safari (+~35 min)** → Počitelj → Kravica → Mostar. Totals: Dubrovnik→Radimlja 2:16 + Radimlja→Mostar 1:01 = 3:17 vs 3:11 for the standard Kravica run — **time-neutral**, no road driven twice, and it converts border-village backroads into three extra sights. Even without the Trebinje night this is the tiebreaker that should decide WHERE day 4's sleep goes. Smaller sibling: **Imotski lakes as the day-3 inland variant** (Makarska→Imotski→Vrgorac→Dubrovnik, +1:00 on the deadline day) — strong if the Blue Lake has water (swim season historically starts ~late June; have the rim-view as fallback), and it dodges the Neum-corridor border stop.

## Research-method notes
- Coordinates: every pin geocoded against OSM/Nominatim (Radimlja corrected from the village to the actual necropolis; Žabljak Crnojevića cross-checked with Wikipedia 42.317/19.158; "Niagara" pin = restaurant beside the falls).
- Local-language searches paid off: hr sources on Modro jezero swim-season timing ("sezona kupanja... krajem lipnja" — late June, our exact risk window); Montenegrin sharemontenegro.me piece naming Grebaje/Komovi/Hajla as the country's most unfairly overlooked places; Bosnian furaj.ba swim-spot lists corroborating Pliva/Kravica/Koćuša hierarchy; Montenegrin press on the Đalovića gondola status (under construction — kills it for 2026).
- June caveats recur in three entries (Modro jezero water level, Cijevna flow, Hutovo Blato bird season) — flagged with tag `june-borderline` where relevant.
- Crowding: only Biogradska boardwalk ("bus-busy in high season") and the Biševo cave (midday queues) drew crowd warnings; everything else here is structurally low-crowd — that's the point of off-route.
