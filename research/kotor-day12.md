Research date: 2026-06-11. All prices/hours cross-checked from multiple 2025-2026 sources.

## 1. Kotor Fortress — Entry, Parking, Timing

### Opening hours
**Official: 07:00–20:00** in summer season. Multiple 2026 guides confirm 7 AM open.
One June 2025 TripAdvisor review reported paying €18 (up from the listed €15), suggesting a possible mid-season price bump. Budget €15pp as baseline, €18pp as worst case.

### The 07:30 plan — is it realistic?
Yes, with caveats:
- Gates physically open at 07:00; booths staffed from 07:00 (not 08:00 as some older sources say).
- 07:30 entry is prime: before the first cruise ships dock (~09:00), before the walls become unbearable heat-traps (~10:00).
- The walls face east — you get direct morning sun on the ascent, which is better than the scorching midday sun but still warm in late June. Carry water.
- Arriving at 07:30 means you finish at the fortress top around 08:15–08:30 and are back down by 09:15–09:30 — before the worst of the crowds.

### Ticket price
- **€15 per person** (official 2026 rate; one June 2025 report of €18 — unclear if seasonal surcharge or rounding)
- Children under 12: free
- Total for 4 adults: **€60** (or up to €72 if the €18 rate applies)
- Cash and card accepted

### Entry gate & what to look for
Two official gates — both lead to the same pay booth mid-climb:
1. **North/River Gate entrance** (recommended for drivers): Exit the old town through the River Gate (Porta Fluviale, north side, on the Škurda River). Walk straight past the Bastion restaurant, look for a triumphal archway on your left with a "Main Entrance to St. John's Fortress" sign. This is the cleaner approach and avoids the labyrinth of the old town at dawn.
2. **Cathedral entrance**: Behind St. Tryphon Cathedral in the old town street maze — harder to navigate, same price booth.

### Parking near the fortress gate
Park at the **Tabačina lot** — directly across from the North/River Gate entrance. This is the closest lot to the fortress trailhead, a 2-minute walk to the gate.
- Rate: ~€1.50–€2.50/hr in peak season
- Arrive by 07:15–07:20 to secure a spot; the lot fills quickly once cruise passengers arrive
- Alternatively: the large "Parking Old Town" lot at the Sea Gate, ~500m walk to the North Gate — marginally cheaper, same price bracket

**Do not attempt to park on the main road past 09:00 on a Saturday in June — cruise ship coaches will have blocked everything.**

### Climb duration
- **Ascent to San Giovanni summit (fortress top):** 45–60 min at a moderate pace. ~1,350 steps, 254m elevation gain.
- **With stops for photos:** 60–75 min up.
- **Descent:** 30–45 min (faster, knees take the load).
- **Full round-trip including time at the top:** 2–2.5 hours.
- **"As far as you want" version:** The walls offer rewarding views long before the top. The first major rampart with a panoramic view is ~20–25 min in. Some travelers turn back there. If someone is tired, they can wait at the halfway point while others summit.

## 2. Ladder of Kotor — Free Alternative

### What it is
The Ladder of Kotor (Kotorske Ljestve) is a separate, ancient mountain road — 70 hairpin switchbacks climbing from Kotor up to the Krstac Pass (elevation ~1,000m) and beyond toward Lovćen and Cetinje. It is **not the same trail as the fortress climb**. It runs north of the Škurda River, outside the city walls entirely.

### Trailhead
- Exit via the **North/River Gate**, cross the Škurda bridge, turn right on Tabačina Road.
- Pass the Bastion restaurant and Pekara AS bakery.
- The path starts almost immediately — no ticket booth, no gate, no fee.
- **Entirely free, open 24/7** (it is a public mountain road, not a managed attraction).

### Does it reach the fortress?
The Ladder of Kotor and the City Walls/Fortress trail are **parallel routes that used to connect** via a hole/window in the upper wall. As of late 2023/2024, that passage has been permanently closed (the official guide says to "preserve ancient masonry"). The two trails now **do not loop**.

Practical implications:
- If you hike the Ladder up, you cannot descend via the fortress trail without paying at the gate. You must descend the same way (or exit to Krstac, which puts you well above Kotor with no easy return).
- The Ladder is a **much more serious hike** — 6.4 km one-way, 940m elevation, 2–3 hrs up — vs the fortress trail (2.5 km, 254m, 45–60 min). It rewards people wanting a full mountain day, not a quick morning warm-up before driving to Perast.

### Verdict for Day 12
**Skip the Ladder of Kotor on Day 12.** The fortress paid trail is the right choice: it's shorter, has the castle at the top (the actual payoff), and fits the time budget. The Ladder is for a dedicated hiking day and does not practically bypass the fee anymore. €15pp is fair and keeps you on schedule.

## 3. Old Town — 30 Minutes After the Walls

After descending ~09:15–09:30, you'll already be inside the old town near the St. Mary's Collegiate Church end. 30 minutes gives you:

**Must-do in 30 min (self-guided, no booking):**
1. **Piazza delle Armi (Arms Square)** — the main square, 2 min walk from the fortress base. Clock Tower with the "Pyramid of Shame" at its base. Grab a coffee at an outdoor table (cafes open from 08:00). This is the heart of Kotor; just sitting here for 10 min is worth it.
2. **Cathedral of Saint Tryphon** — opens 09:00 (€3 entry, optional). The Romanesque facade is visible from outside for free. If someone wants to step in, the silver bas-relief altarscreen is the highlight; 10–15 min inside is enough.
3. **Wander one or two lanes** — Kotor's old town is genuinely charming but small; you can cover most of it in 30 min. Don't try to "do" it all. The backstreets near the Flour Square (Trg od brašna) and the Grgurina lane are photogenic.

**Is it self-explanatory?** Largely yes. The old town is small and compact — ~500m across. Signs are in multiple languages. No need for a guide or audio tour. The flow from the fortress descent → Arms Square → Cathedral → Sea Gate is intuitive and can be done without a map.

**Crowd note:** Saturday at 09:30 in late June. By the time you're wandering, the town is waking up but cruise crowds haven't flooded it yet (ships dock 09:00, passengers filter in on foot by ~10:00). You have a narrow window of relative calm.

## 4. Perast + Our Lady of the Rocks

### Drive Kotor → Perast
- **~14 km, 20 minutes** by car along the Bay of Kotor (E65 coastal road north).
- Scenic drive, no issues.

### Parking in Perast
Perast is tiny (population ~400, less than 2 km end-to-end). There is a small car park at the entrance to the village. In high season Saturday mid-morning, it fills — arrive early or park on the approach road and walk 5 min. No parking fees reported inside the village.

### How the boat works
**This is the key logistics question.** There is NO fixed ferry schedule. It works like this:
- Private local boatmen operate from the Perast waterfront (main promenade, near the stone pier/dock). They approach you, or you approach them.
- They take you to the island (~5 min crossing), wait while you visit (30–40 min), and bring you back (~5 min).
- **Price: ~€5 per person return** (some sources say €5–€10pp; most recent reports say €5pp is standard, larger groups sometimes negotiate).
- **Church admission: €2 per person** (covers church + small museum).
- **Total cost for 4:** ~€28 (boat €20 + church €8) at the €5/pp rate; up to €48 at the €10/pp rate.

**Earliest boats:** Boats start operating when boatmen show up, typically from ~09:00 onward. The church itself opens at **09:00**. First boats are usually available by 09:00–09:30. On a summer Saturday, boatmen are at the dock early.

**Round trip time from shore:**
- Walk from car to dock: 5 min
- Wait for/negotiate with boatman: 5 min
- Boat to island: 5–10 min
- Visit church + museum: 25–35 min
- Boat back: 5–10 min
- Walk back to car: 5 min
- **Total: ~60 minutes** (tight scheduling) to **75–90 minutes** (relaxed).

### Is it worth it vs skipping?
**Worth it, but it's a personal call.** The community consensus:
- The island is genuinely unusual — a man-made islet built over centuries by Perast sailors who threw stones into the bay. The church interior has hundreds of votive silver plaques covering the walls, an obsessive folk-baroque aesthetic unlike anything else on the trip. 30–35 min is enough.
- Perast itself (without the boat) is pleasant for a 20-minute waterfront walk past baroque palaces, but it's thin content on its own.
- If you're tight on time or energy, skip it and just do a 15-min Perast waterfront stop. But if you have 75 min to spare, it's worth the €7pp.

**Verdict for Day 12:** Include it. The boat departs on demand (no schedule to catch), it fits between Kotor old town and the border drive, and it's a highlight of the Bay of Kotor that you won't get another chance at on this route.

## 5. Drive: Budva → Kotor

- **~23 km, 25–35 minutes** (Google Maps: ~25 min off-peak; allow 35 min on a summer Saturday morning with coastal road traffic).
- The E65 coastal road is straightforward — no mountain passes.
- **Saturday morning caveat:** Leave Budva early. By 08:00 on a summer Saturday, the road is already busy with day-trippers from Budva heading toward Kotor. The 06:30–07:00 window is clean.

### Parking summary
- **Best for fortress:** Tabačina lot (North Gate), €1.50–€2.50/hr. Arrive by 07:15–07:20.
- Pay by meter (coin/card). Carry €5–€10 in coins as backup.

## 6. Drive: Kotor → Vitaljina Border → Trebinje → DBV

### Kotor to Vitaljina border
- **~52 km, ~55–65 minutes** via the E65/coastal road south then the Vitaljina turnoff.
- Route: Kotor → south on E65 → Herceg Novi → continue south (not toward Debeli Brijeg/main crossing) → turn off toward Njivice/Molunat → follow country road through Vitaljina to the border checkpoint.
- The approach roads to Vitaljina are **paved but narrow and winding** through rural countryside. Passable with a normal car; just slow.

### Vitaljina border crossing itself
- Small, single-lane crossing. Passenger cars only (no buses/coaches — this is why it's faster than Debeli Brijeg).
- **Off-peak wait:** 10–30 minutes.
- **Peak Saturday wait:** 30–90 minutes (reports vary; one traveler reported "one car ahead of me" at noon). The sweet spot is arriving **before 13:00** on a Saturday.
- **Risk:** Occasionally closed without notice (rare but real). If closed, you must detour to Debeli Brijeg and lose 1–2 hours. Check fanfani.me real-time camera before setting off.
- Compare with Debeli Brijeg/Karasovići on a peak Saturday: 2–5 hour waits are common. Vitaljina is almost always better.

### Vitaljina (Croatia side) → Trebinje
- After crossing, you are in Croatia's Konavle region.
- From Vitaljina to **Trebinje (BiH):** you re-enter Bosnia-Herzegovina via the Klek/Num/Čapljina road. The Trebinje border is a separate crossing from Vitaljina — short wait (local traffic).
- **Vitaljina → Trebinje total driving time: ~45–60 minutes** including the short BiH border crossing.
- Alternatively, some travelers go directly from Vitaljina toward **Dubrovnik Airport (DBV):** ~20–25 minutes (14 miles from Vitaljina to DBV — confirmed by Rome2Rio).

### From Trebinje to Dubrovnik Airport (DBV)
- **~43 minutes, ~42 km** — confirmed direct route.

## 8. What Can Be Cut If Things Run Late?

Priority order of cuts (cut in this sequence if time slips):

1. **Our Lady of the Rocks boat** — skip the boat but still do Perast waterfront walk (15 min vs 75 min, saves 60 min). The island is special but not unmissable.
2. **Perast entirely** — drop it and drive straight to Vitaljina after old town (saves 90+ min). Still have the fortress and old town experience.
3. **Fortress summit** — if someone can't face the climb (heat, energy), stop at the halfway rampart (~25 min up) for the views and descend. Saves 30–40 min vs full summit.
4. **Old town** — compress to 15 min (just the Piazza delle Armi and a quick walk) if fortress ran long.

**Do NOT cut:** The 07:30 start and the Vitaljina crossing. These are the structural supports of the day. Sleep in until 08:00 and you'll spend Day 12 trapped in cruise-ship hell and a 3-hour border queue.
