## 2. Trebinje — Jun 18 evening arrival

The group arrives Trebinje the evening of Jun 18 after picking up the 4th person at Dubrovnik airport. This is the first night in Bosnia. Here's what's open, what's worth doing, and what the town is actually like.

### Dinner: what to book

**Studenac** — THE riverside place, on the banks of the Trebišnjica. This is the most-recommended restaurant in Trebinje by locals and travelers consistently. Ask anyone in town.
- Riverside summer garden (~120 seats) + fireside dining hall (~70 seats)
- Menu: grilled trout (fresh from their own pond), tuna steak, Jeger steak, mixed grill, fish soup
- Price: affordable/moderate ("lots of local food for little money," "great price")
- **Cash only — no card payments** (confirmed recent 2025 reviews)
- Reservations essential on weekends — phone ahead if arriving late
- **Note:** it is slightly outside the old town and not easily walkable — "reachable only by car" or a difficult walk. One recent negative review mentioned being turned away without a reservation (though others had good service). Go with a booking.
- **Tripadvisor rating:** 4.4/5 with 321 reviews — consistently top-ranked in Trebinje

**Alternatives if Studenac is full:**
- **Kolo** — fancy option in the heart of the Old Town, classic regional dishes with contemporary flair, good for a walk-up dinner
- **Restoran Ognjište** — rustic bar-and-grill, Balkan dishes, friendly service, open morning to late night (walk-up friendly)
- **Vinarija Vukoje** — not just a winery; the restaurant is on floors 6-7 of the winery building with panoramic views of Trebinje and vineyards, up to 300 seats, open-air terrace. One of the best views in town. For a late arrival this may work if they have terrace seats.

### Morning (Jun 19): what to do before driving north

The Old Town of Trebinje is described as "the least touristy Old Town in the entire Balkans — no queues, no overpriced entrance fees." Compact and walkable; narrow Ottoman alleys, a couple of mosques, clock tower, all free.

**Morning walk order (2–3 hours, all free/cheap):**
1. **Stari Grad (Old Town)** — start with a Bosnian coffee at Platani or Botanik in the main square (Slovenska trg / the plane-tree square). Coffee ritual: order "domaća kafa," let the džezva sit 2-3 min, pour slowly, sip with sugar cube and lokum.
   - **Botanik Coffee & More** (Svetosavska 13) — locals' favorite coffee spot
   - **Platani** — excellent morning coffee while watching the main square activity
2. **Arslanagić Bridge (1574)** — 15-minute walk downriver. Originally built 10 km upstream, moved stone-by-stone in the 1960s when a dam flooded its original location. Great photo spot early morning.
3. **Ivo Andrić Bridge** — best photo of Trebinje's Mediterranean rooflines reflected in the river. "The most beautiful view of Trebinje."
4. **Herzegovačka Gračanica church** — on a hill ~3 km from center (drive or 45-min walk). Replica of the Kosovo medieval church, built 2000. Stunning panoramic views of the whole city. Worth it for views alone.
   - **Contact:** +387 59 261 362 for opening hours
5. **Morning market** in the main square — local cheeses, honey, ajvar; the figs and produce are famous here

**Grk ice cream parlor** — described as "an institution in Trebinje" with 30–40 sundae varieties. If the group has a sweet tooth, this is the town's most-loved detour.

### Trebinje wine country: the two serious options

**Vukoje Winery** (Mirna 28 — at the entrance to Trebinje):
- One of the best Herzegovina wineries, frequently praised
- Modern facility, 6th/7th floor restaurant with terraced vineyard views
- Grapes: Žilavka (crisp local white) and Vranac (full-bodied red)
- Wine tasting: ~€5/person (ask about per-glass option to avoid getting a full bottle each round)
- Call ahead for tastings: best in late morning. Not just a winery — the restaurant is genuinely good.
- Note: "the road is newly built" to the Vukoje facility; easy access with the Sportage

**Tvrdoš Monastery** (~4 km south of Trebinje):
- The oldest wine cellar in Herzegovina (15th-century barrel cellar, century-old oak barrels)
- Monks produce Decanter-gold-rated Vranac; you can buy bottles at the shop
- **Open: 07:00–19:00** (monastery)
- Wine tastings need pre-booking (weeks ahead for organized tastings; walk-up may get you into the shop)
- Dress respectably — it's an active monastery
- Free to visit the grounds

### Is the old town walkable from apartments?
Yes, very. The Old Town is small and flat. Most apartments in Trebinje are within 5–15 min walk. The winery and Studenac restaurant require a car/taxi.

### VERDICT for Trebinje:
Excellent first-night stop. The dinner choice is Studenac (book ahead, cash only) or Vukoje restaurant for views. The morning is a 2-3 hour free walk. Then drive north. Trebinje is genuinely underrated — less touristy than anywhere else on the route, relaxed Mediterranean pace, genuinely cheap.

## 3. Sarajevo beyond the tourist trail

Earlier research covered Baščaršija, ćevapi at Željo/Petica, the cable car, bobsled track. Here's what's deeper:

### Bosnian coffee ceremony — where to actually do it right

**Caffe Bar ANDAR** — a family-run cafe in a former shoe repair shop in the Old Town. Staff walk you through the ceremony step by step: how to let it sit, how to pour, the sugar cube ritual. "Smoke-free environment, great value, unique experience." One of the most-cited authentic kafa spots. Exact address: in Baščaršija area (verify on Maps).

**Rahatlook** — described as "the most authentic experience in Baščaršija." Locals' recommendation.

**Caffe Divan** — inside the historic Morica Han complex (an old Ottoman caravanserai), Baščaršija. Oriental decor, low lighting, popular with locals and students especially in Ramadan. For atmosphere more than purity of ceremony.

**Ministry of Ćejf** — praised for both coffee and baklava together; "genuine hospitality, family-style service."

**What to say:** "domaća kafa" (domestic/Bosnian coffee). Wait 2-3 min, pour slowly into small cup, drink slowly with sugar cube and rahat lokum. This is not a fast espresso — it's a 20-30 min social ritual.

### Vijećnica (National Library / City Hall)
- **Open: daily 09:00–17:00**
- **Entry: 10 KM (~€5) adults, 5 KM students**
- Stunning neo-Moorish architecture (1894); burnt and devastated in 1992, rebuilt 2014. One of the most beautiful buildings in the Balkans. Visit takes 30-60 min.
- Best photos: morning before 11:00, sunlight on the facade from the Miljacka River side
- **Address:** Brodac 1, Sarajevo

### Neighborhoods beyond Baščaršija

**Bistrik:**
- Starts at Latin Bridge, climbs the hill directly across from the Old Town
- "Best area for quiet, local feel with easy access to center"
- Residential, Ottoman-Austro-Hungarian architectural mix, locals-only vibe
- The Trebević cable car lower station is a 10-min walk from here (in Bistrik)
- Sarajevo Brewery is here — the brewery that sustained the city during the Siege

**Kovači:**
- Directly above Baščaršija, steep hill climb toward Yellow Fortress
- One of Sarajevo's oldest residential mahala (neighborhood) — small houses, narrow alleys, mosques
- Yellow Fortress (Žuta Tabija) at the top: free, panoramic city view, especially beautiful at sunset
- Authentic contrast to the tourist-heavy bazaar below

**Grbavica:**
- Cross-river, slightly south; the neighborhood where a famous siege-era sniper controlled the river crossing
- Less tourist-relevant but worth knowing the city extends here

**Skenderija (underground):**
- Zvono Cultural Center in converted Cold War bomb shelters under the former Olympic skating rink
- Thursday evening jam sessions blending traditional and jazz music
- Very local, experimental theatre and poetry readings

### Bars and nightlife — the specific list

**Zlatna Ribica** ("Goldfish") — arguably Sarajevo's most famous bar. Every inch of interior covered in antiques, mirrors, vintage trinkets, goldfish tanks. Dreamlike maximalist decor. Cheap beer, good music played at conversation volume. Mix of locals and knowing tourists. Genuinely special.

**Kino Bosna** (Marijin Dvor) — former cinema turned atmospheric bar with stunning architecture. Live Bosnian folk music Monday and Thursday. Entry fee. One of the most authentically local venues.

**Pivnica Sarajevo / Pivnica HS** (Stari Grad area) — traditional brewery-restaurant, craft beer, Bosnian dishes, live music. Local crowd.

**Trezor** — basement club, techno/hip-hop/RnB nights, entry ~5 KM, open until 05:00. The late-night local option.

**Silver & Smoke** — electronic music, international DJs, associated with Garden of Dreams events. This is the high-quality electronic venue.

**Pink Houdini** (Bistrik area) — jazz and blues, live music, local crowd. Cozy.

**Birtija** — small local bar, after-work salon for artists and journalists. Cheap rakija (~4 KM). Very local, anti-touristy. Regulars include Sarajevo creatives.

**Wine NOT Sarajevo** (Bistrik) — curated wine bar, both Bosnian and international wines. Intimate, hidden-gem feel.

**Baoli Wine Bar** (Baščaršija) — upscale, beautifully restored historic building, charcuterie.

### Rooftop bars — verified options

**Sky Lounge 35 / Avaz Twist Tower** (Tešanjska 24A) — top of the tallest building in Bosnia. 4.6/5 with 979 reviews. Entry 5–10 KM. Best panoramic view in the city. Not especially local-feeling but genuinely spectacular view.

**S One Sky Lounge** (Skenderija 1) — 4.6/5, 762 reviews. River-adjacent location.

**Igman Rooftop Bistro** (Džemala Bijedića 169A) — more local/residential area, moderate prices.

**Garden of Dreams Jun 14 event** was on a building rooftop (Krov zgrade Svjetlosti) — this venue exists as a rooftop event space, but access is normally ticketed events only.

### Coffee shop detour: the Vijećnica area
Walk from the library along the Miljacka river, cross the Latin Bridge (where Franz Ferdinand was assassinated in 1914 — the brass markers are in the pavement), into Baščaršija for coffee. This 30-min walk is the compression of Sarajevo's history.

## 4. Boračko jezero — proper research

**Location:** 33 km from Konjic by road, up a hairpin mountain road. The lake sits in a steep glacial valley at ~960 m elevation.

### Eko Selo Boračko Jezero — the main campsite

- **Site:** https://ekoselo-bih.com/en/
- **Season:** May 1 – September 30 (restaurant open Jun 15 – Sep 15)
- **Capacity:** 250 pitches; 106 beds in cabins/bungalows; 50 tent pitches
- **Pricing:**
  - Day visit (08:00–20:00): adults €5, children 5-15 €2.50, under 5 free
  - Overnight camping: from ~€25 for a camper + 2 adults (park4night data); some reports of 100 KM (~€50) for camper + 4 + electricity — for 4 adults with tent expect **~€25–35/night** (confirm by phone)
  - Accommodation units: from €34/night (pitchup.com listing)
  - **Cash preferred; bring BAM**
- **Facilities:** sandy beaches, pontoon deck, volleyball/football, restaurant (local cuisine, 100 indoor + 150 terrace seats), beach bar, showers (solar-heated — variable hot water), toilets, WiFi in restaurant, security
- **Honest caveat from park4night (3.54/5, 26 reviews):**
  - Beautiful lake, crystal-clear water, friendly staff, good food
  - BUT: aging facilities, cold/inconsistent hot water (solar), sanitary conditions deteriorate on peak weekends ("just 2 outdoor showers"), WiFi rarely works
  - Best experience: weekdays; worse on summer weekends when it fills up
  - One review: "Urgent renovation needed! No showers, no wifi." Another: "Beautiful location, perfect for swimming."
  - **Verdict: worth it for the lake and setting, not for facilities. Manage expectations on showers.**
- **Booking:** advance booking recommended — "guests who book have priority." Call: +387 66 830 309

### Rafting at Boračko Jezero (Eko Selo's own program)
- **Canyon:** Glavatičevo → Konjic, ~23 km, the same Neretva canyon as the Konjic operators
- Eko Selo's base is 5 km from the rafting start; organized transfer (~10 min)
- Raft Bosnia by Eco Vision operates from here too (22 years of operation)
- From **~€45 pp all-in** (package with equipment, transfer, riverside BBQ)
- See ba-notes.md §5 for the full rafting operator comparison

### Boračko vs. Sarajevo: which to prioritize?

If the group is doing Neretva rafting from Konjic anyway, Boračko Lake is a natural pair — same road, ~16 km up the mountain after rafting. The lake is swimmable, the setting is dramatic (steep forested slopes), and it's genuinely quieter than Sarajevo.

**BUT:** Boračko is a nature/camping vibe. If the group wants city, food, history, nightlife, and the cable car/bobsled track — that's Sarajevo. These are different experiences, not alternatives to the same thing. The route skeleton has both on consecutive nights; that works perfectly.

**Verdict: Boračko Lake is worth a swim stop even if not overnight.** If overnight, go on a weeknight (not Fri/Sat) and don't count on hot showers.

## 5. Sutjeska NP logistics — 2-3 hour transit stop, fees, Perućica

### Drive time from Sarajevo:
~100 km, **~2h** on M18/M20 (Sarajevo → Trnovo → Foča → M20 → Tjentište). Slow and winding; not highway speed.

### Entry fees (KM = Bosnian Mark; 1 KM ≈ €0.51):
| Access type | Price |
|---|---|
| Main park areas | **10 KM/person** (~€5) |
| Memorial complex at Tjentište + sports zone | **5 KM/person** |
| Annual pass | 30 KM |
| Family pass (up to 5 people) | **25 KM single** |
| Students | 2 KM |
| Under 14 / over 65 | 5 KM |
| Group vehicle | 30 KM/motor vehicle |

**Can you drive through without paying?** The M20 road runs through the park. The fee structure targets visitors to park areas and the memorial complex — if you're just driving the highway, you may pass through without encountering a fee booth. However, if you want to stop at the monument or any park area, **10 KM/person applies**. 4 people = 40 KM (~€20) or 25 KM family ticket (~€13). Cheap enough to not worry about.

**Source:** https://foca24.info/vijest/780/nove-cijene-u-np-sutjeska-u-dolinu-heroja-za-pet-do-vodopada-skakavac-za-1000-km

### Perućica Primeval Forest — GUIDED ONLY, advance booking required

Perućica is one of Europe's last true primary (old-growth) forests. Access is **strictly guided-only** — "any visit is strictly prohibited except when announced, approved and monitored by authorized staff."

- **Tour cost:** **€38/person** (local transport + licensed guide included)
- **Park entry:** 10 KM additional (not included)
- **Duration:** 4–5 hours total, ~3h hiking; moderate, suitable ages 7–60
- **Group minimum:** 4+ people (you have exactly 4 — perfect)
- **Season:** April–November
- **Logistics:** Meet at Hotel Mladost or Restaurant Komlen, then 40–50 min by off-road vehicle to Dragosh Sedlo (1,260 m) starting point
- **Book via:**
  - Official NP Sutjeska: office@sutjeskanp.com / +387 58 233-130
  - Third-party operator Rainforest.ba: info@rainforest.ba / +387 65 381 255 (site: https://rainforest.ba/)
  - **Quota: ~16 people/day (10 groups max, 10 people each)** — book ahead, especially in June

**Alternative tour operators confirmed:**
- Inlandia.com: scheduled departures including Jun 25, Aug operators (book 2-3 weeks ahead)
- Outdoor Tara / Tara Sport Rafting: also offer Perućica hikes

**What a 2–3 hour transit stop looks like (without Perućica):**

If you don't book Perućica in advance, the best transit stop is:

1. **Battle of Sutjeska Monument / Tjentište** (5 KM/person): the famous brutalist "wings" — one of Yugoslavia's great architectural achievements, 200+ steps, otherworldly. Takes ~45 min including steps.

2. **Dragoš Sedlo viewpoint** (~2 km north of Tjentište, signposted): free, ~100 m walk from the road. Look DOWN into Perućica forest + see Skakavac waterfall (75 m) from above. This is the Perućica experience without the guided hike.

**Total for a 2–3h transit stop:** Monument + Dragoš Sedlo = ~1.5–2.5h and easily combines. No Perućica forest inside — just the aerial view.

**If you want Trnovačko Lake or Maglić:** those are full-day affairs requiring an overnight at Tjentište (see ba-notes.md §4). Don't plan these on a transit day.

## 6. Šćepan Polje border crossing — practical 2025/2026

### Basic facts:
- **Open:** 24/7 for passenger and goods traffic (official agreement between BiH and ME governments)
- **Type:** full stamp-and-check crossing (not wave-through)
- **Wait times:** typically under 30 min; "one of the quieter Balkans borders" — the Bosnian AA club reports "waiting not longer than 30 minutes" at GP Hum. With a normal car in June expect 10–20 min outside peak hours.
- **Source:** https://awaywiththesteiners.com/bosnia-montenegro-border-crossing/ and TripAdvisor forum

### Document checks:
**Bosnia side (exit):**
- Passports checked; no exit stamp required
- Vehicle documents NOT checked on the Bosnian exit side

**Montenegro side (entry):**
- Passports checked
- **Vehicle documents ARE required** — Montenegro is "scrupulous with documentation for the vehicle"
- Insurance certificate / coverage document
- **Headlights must be on** (this is a Montenegrin road rule; check is sometimes done at border)

### Green Card:
- **Formally no longer required** since August 2021 (per TripAdvisor destination expert)
- What matters is proper vehicle insurance that covers Montenegro
- **For the Sicily By Car rental:** this is the key action item (already flagged in rental-car.md). You need:
  1. Written confirmation from Sicily By Car that Montenegro is an allowed country
  2. The vehicle's insurance certificate showing Montenegro coverage OR a cross-border permit document
  3. Cross-border fee: Croatian rental companies often charge **€50 per country** for BiH and Montenegro — confirm at pickup
- **Practical: bring all the car's documents** (rental agreement, insurance certificate, vehicle registration) and have them ready. The Montenegro side will ask for them.

### Road approach caution:
The road from Foča to Šćepan Polje (the last ~25 km on the Bosnian side) is the notorious rough stretch:
- "Narrow and sometimes dirt road," "last ~10 km a one-lane asphalt squeeze"
- A 5 km section has been under reconstruction since 2022 — now "mostly paved but still narrow"
- Speed cameras and police patrols on the approach
- **Drive in daylight only, slowly**
- The wooden/steel bridge at the border has a **12-tonne weight limit** (the Sportage is fine)
- Spend remaining BAM at the last service station in Brod before the border

## 7. Sarajevo food — specific verified picks

### Ćevapi

**Željo** (already known) — the classic, two branches. Gets crowded; go at non-peak hours (before 12:00 or after 14:00).

**Petica (aka Asdž Petica)** — equally praised by locals, "just as delicious" spice blend. Called "Petica" informally; TripAdvisor lists it under its full name. Walk away from Željo and locals will point you to this.

**Mrkva** — the third name that comes up in local recommendations. Less prominent online but locals mention it.

**Ferhadija Specijal (Cevabdzinica Specijal)** — another contender.

**Key local rule:** a good ćevapi restaurant does NOT serve alcohol. If a restaurant serves ćevapi AND beer, they are NOT making their own fresh cevapi. Find the specialist places.

**Format:** always ordered by the 5 (pet) or 10 (deset) — small cigar-shaped pork/veal sausages in fresh somun bread with raw onion and kajmak (cream). 5 pieces + somun = ~5 KM.

### Baklava & sweets

**Baklava Dućan** — THE place. Located in the Old Town (Curciluk Veliki 56, or ask anyone in Baščaršija). Described as "hidden gem," 100% handmade. Bosnian baklava style = more layers, no pistachio, less sweet and syrupy than Turkish, crunchier. Their specialty: "Džandar baklava" — takes 3 days to prepare. Official site: https://baklava.ba/en/

**Baklava Sarajbosna** (Gajev Trg Bb) — the main local rival, many rate it equal or higher.

**Slastičarna Egipat** (Ferhadija street) — wider selection of traditional desserts: tufahija, kadaif, boem, baklava, famous ice cream.

**Ministry of Ćejf** — combines coffee ceremony with baklava; the "flaky baklava" is praised. Good for doing both in one stop.

### What to avoid:
Restaurant-bar hybrids near Stari Most in Mostar that serve ćevapi with beer — tourist traps with no fresh-made product.

### Lunch/dinner beyond ćevapi:

**Cinemas Sloga** (Stari Grad) — traditional tavern, authentic atmosphere, workers and locals, cheap. The anti-touristy lunch spot.

**Kino Bosna** (Marijin Dvor) — former cinema, architecture alone worth it, live sevdah (Bosnian blues) on Mondays and Thursdays, cheap food and drinks.

## 8. Trebević cable car — facts confirmed

- **Ride:** 7–9 min, 2100 m cable, 33 modern gondola cabins, 1,200 passengers/hr capacity
- **Price:** ~20 KM (~€10) return; cash or card
- **Lower station:** in Bistrik neighborhood (~10 min walk uphill from Baščaršija)
- **At the top:** Vidikovac viewpoint, the abandoned 1984 Olympic bobsled track (1.3 km of graffiti + bullet holes, free to walk), hiking trails
- **Time needed:** half-day for cable car + bobsled walk + coffee at top
- This is the must-do activity for the group — covered in ba-notes.md but confirmed here
