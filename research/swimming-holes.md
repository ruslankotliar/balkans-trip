### 1. Zrmanja River — Berberov Buk & Muškovci
**Status:** `[IN DATA]` — `hr-zrmanja-kayaking` covers the canyon kayak + swims. This note adds swimming-only access detail.

**What research found:**
Zrmanja has four named swim/jump spots accessible without a guided kayak:

**Berberov Buk (self-drive access — best for a quick stop)**
- Location: ~1h from Zadar, ~44 km; search "Berberov Buk Zrmanja" in Google Maps — gets you to a small parking area with restaurants (open daily in main season).
- The falls are tufa ridgelines across the river; you jump off a road bridge into the pool below.
- Facilities: 3 restaurants/bars, toilets, 1km walking trail along the falls. Free entry. Gets busy weekends but manageable weekday mornings.
- June conditions: water clear and cold (Zrmanja is always colder than the sea — budget 18-22°C). Jumping is excellent; shallow-end wading too.
- Verdict: **worth the 1h Zadar detour on day 1** if you have the time before heading south. An hour stop, no commitment, free.

**Muškovci (end of kayak route, swimmable independently too)**
- Approximate coordinates: 44.27°N, 15.77°E — tiny hamlet at the confluence where the canyon opens.
- This is where guided kayak tours finish; there's a tufa waterfall + natural jacuzzi cascade + "Ogar" waterfall upstream.
- Self-guided access: drive from Obrovac via small roads — use satellite view, road is paved to the hamlet.
- Note: the upper canyon (Kaštel Žegarski to Muškovci) is technically inside Velebit Nature Park; solo kayaking is restricted but swimming/walking is fine.
- June conditions: water is at its best — clear, emerald, 18-20°C.

**Verdict for the group:** Berberov Buk = 1h roadside splash on day 1 (Zadar → Split day). Muškovci = only worth it if doing the guided kayak (already in data), not standalone.

**Sources:**
- https://two-brits.com/2020/09/03/visiting-the-zrmanja-canyon-in-croatia/
- https://www.croatian-guide.com/attractions/berberov-buk
- https://explore-croatia.net/muskovci-beautiful-excursion-on-the-zrmanja-river/

### 2. Cetina River — Cliff Jumping Above Omiš
**Status:** `[IN DATA]` — `hr-cetina-cliff-jump-luka` has the key info. Additional swim detail below.

**What research found:**
The Cetina canyon above Omiš has a sequence of swim/jump spots along a 10km stretch:

- **Izletište Luka** (already in data): ~6km up the canyon from Omiš. Free, drive-up. 3m and 6m jumps into deep green water. June: water crystal clear, cold (~18°C), powerful enough to be refreshing. Tour groups stop here 10:00–15:00 — go early morning or late afternoon for space.

- **Radmanove Mlinice restaurant** (~6km from Omiš): historic mill + restaurant. Most guided trips end here. There's calm swimmable water beside the mill. More scenic than adrenaline.

- **Upper canyon / Gubavica vicinity** (~25km from Omiš toward Zadvarje): the canyon narrows and deepens. Canyoning tours go here; not easily accessible for a quick dip. The Gubavica waterfall (53m) has a pool at the bottom but it requires a guided canyoning tour to reach safely.

**June reality check:** Water is cold, 15-18°C. Completely safe, clean — Cetina supplies drinking water for much of Dalmatia. Guided rafting (38-45€/person) includes cliff jumps; free if you drive up to Luka.

**No new pin needed** — existing data covers this well.

**Sources:**

### 3. Cetina Upper Canyon — Gubavica Waterfall Pool
**Status:** `[NEW PIN CANDIDATE]`

**Coordinates:** 43.4324°N, 16.8836°E (parking/viewpoint; waterfall pool is 5min walk down a rocky path)

**What it is:** 53-meter waterfall plunging into a turquoise pool inside a 180m-deep canyon near the village of Zadvarje. Free viewpoint from the rim (dramatic). Reaching the pool itself requires a rocky scramble or a guided canyoning tour.

**Access:** From Omiš: drive ~25km inland toward Zadvarje (D39 then local roads). Parking at the GPS above with an info board. 5-minute walk to viewpoint. Reaching the swimming pool at the base requires either: (a) hiring a canyoning guide from Omiš (~80-100€/person), or (b) scrambling down if you have the right shoes and confidence.

**June conditions:** Water extremely cold (canyon barely sees sun), probably 12-15°C. Dramatic and photogenic. Not a leisurely swim — more of a "jump in, scream, get out" experience.

**Verdict:** Worth the 30min detour from the Makarska corridor if the group is going through Zadvarje anyway. Incredible photo. Swimming at the base is a bonus — the viewpoint alone is worth it. **Slot it on day 2 (Split → Makarska leg)** as a 1h inland detour.

**Sources:**
- https://croatiahiddengems.com/lakes-and-waterfalls/waterfall-gubavica
- https://www.tripadvisor.com/Attraction_Review-g14007837-d15046216-Reviews-Waterfall_Gubavica-Zadvarje_Split_Dalmatia_County_Dalmatia.html

### 7. Vruja / Brela — Sea but Worth Noting
**Status:** `[NEW PIN CANDIDATE]`

**Coordinates:** 43.3733°N, 16.9397°E (approximate — the northernmost cove of Brela, ~2km north of Punta Rata along the coastal path)

**What it is:** Vruja is not a river swimming hole but a unique phenomenon — a massive submarine freshwater spring (60m diameter cave at 28m depth) erupts directly onto a pebble sea beach. The result: you swim in clear sea water that is noticeably cooler and fresher near the shore. The underwater spring is Croatia's only protected underwater spring. The beach itself is secluded (surrounded by cliffs, accessible by coastal trail or kayak from Brela).

**Access from Brela:** Walk north on the coastal path from Punta Rata (~2km), or kayak. No road access — cars park in Brela. No facilities at the cove itself; bring everything.

**June:** Uncrowded (far fewer people than Punta Rata). Water 21-23°C sea surface, noticeably colder near the spring upwelling. Snorkeling over the spring vent is surreal.

**Verdict:** A genuine hidden gem that adds real character to a Makarska Riviera stop. The group already has Punta Rata on shortlist — **add Vruja as a 2h afternoon extension** if staying near Brela/Makarska. Don't make it a standalone drive — it's a walk from Brela.

**Sources:**
- https://brela.hr/en/nature/vruja
- https://dalmatia-outdoor.hr/en/attraction/vruja-bay/

### 8. Kravica Waterfalls — June Swimming Detail
**Status:** `[IN DATA]` — `ba-kravica`. This fills in the June swim specifics the data entry lacked.

**June reality, sourced:**
- Water temperature: 16-20°C in summer; at opening in June probably closer to 16-17°C — cold but swimmable for energetic people.
- Crowds: tour buses (from Dubrovnik, Split, Mostar) arrive 11:00–13:00. **Arrive before 09:30 for near-empty experience.** Kravica opens at 08:00.
- Swimming quality: excellent — the horseshoe of falls creates a large natural pool with multiple swim areas, depth varies 1-3m.
- **07:00 arrival:** not possible — opens at 08:00. But 08:00 opening gives you 2-3 hours before peak crowds.
- Early June is the golden window: enough water flow for dramatic falls + pre-peak crowds.
- Parking: paid (a few euros), easily handled.

**Verdict: Go early (08:00 open), swim freely, leave by 10:30.** This is the best single swimming hole on the Bosnia leg — don't skip.

- https://exploremostar.com/kravica-waterfall/

### 9. Diva Grabovica (Neretva Canyon Beach)
**Status:** `[IN DATA]` — `ba-diva-grabovica` in gap-finds.json. The existing entry focuses on hiking (Žlijeb route to Čvrsnica). This note adds the **beach/swim angle** that wasn't in the data.

**Swimming detail:** The valley floor at Diva Grabovica has pebble beaches where the Diva Grabovica stream meets the Neretva. Two streams form a small delta — the beach between them is the swim spot. Water is cold (Neretva is always glacier-fed cold, ~12-15°C even in summer), clear, emerald-green. Wild camping allowed/tolerated here.

**Access by car:** M-17 Mostar→Jablanica. Cross the Neretva at the HE Grabovica dam, then ~5km macadam to the valley floor (passable in a normal car slowly). Coordinates for the beach: **43.6036°N, 17.6763°E** (existing data pin is accurate).

**Alternatively by kayak:** 3h self-guided kayak from Jablanica — more dramatic approach through the canyon walls.

**June:** Water very cold. The beach itself is uncrowded. This is the "reward" spot after driving the M-17 canyon corridor — you can stop for 1-2h on the way to/from Jablanica. The surrounding Čvrsnica walls are staggering.

**No new pin needed** — update the existing description to mention the swim beach angle.

### 11. Neretva Canyon — Čelebići Swimming Spot
**Status:** `[NEW PIN CANDIDATE]`

**Coordinates:** 43.6700°N, 18.0333°E (Čelebići village; the Idbar tributary junction with the Neretva, ~25km north of Mostar on M-17)

**What it is:** Where the Idbar River meets the Neretva at Čelebići, there's a small waterfall near a cave, adjacent to a namesake restaurant. Local swimming spot. Also: the M-17 Jablanica corridor has several pull-off swim spots where locals swim from the gravel banks of Jablaničko Lake (the reservoir).

**Access:** Drive the M-17 between Mostar and Konjic — multiple roadside pull-offs where you can see turquoise water. Čelebići specifically: the restaurant there means parking and toilets. Walk to the riverbank.

**June water quality:** Cold (Neretva is spring-fed, stays cold all summer — plan for 12-15°C). Gorgeous emerald color. The Neretva is considered one of Europe's cleanest rivers.

**Verdict:** Not a destination in itself, but a perfect 30min stop en route Mostar→Konjic. Don't drive to Čelebići specially — hit it in passing. More of a "pull over and jump in" than a planned spot.

### 12. Piva Lake (Plužine) — Turquoise Canyon Reservoir
**Status:** `[NEW PIN CANDIDATE]`

**Coordinates:** 43.1609°N, 18.8353°E (Plužine town beach — "City Beach")

**What it is:** The Piva Canyon was flooded in 1975 to create this reservoir — but the result is one of Montenegro's most jaw-dropping swimming spots: an intensely turquoise artificial lake at 675m altitude, 45km long, surrounded by sheer limestone canyon walls. The water is exceptionally clear (no industry) and 20-22°C by peak June.

**The group drives through here anyway** on the confirmed route: enter Montenegro at Šćepan Polje → Piva canyon road → Plužine → Žabljak. This is literally ON the route — zero detour cost.

**Swimming spots:**
- **Plužine City Beach:** small beach with easy water access right at the town. Parking, café nearby. Best for a swim stop.
- **Canyon pull-offs:** the Piva canyon road (some sections single-lane through tunnels) has occasional viewpoints where you can scramble down to the water. Use satellite view to find pull-offs before tunnels.

**June conditions:** Water temperature 18-22°C in June (high-altitude reservoir warms slowly). Color is surreal — intense turquoise that photographs strangely blue. Crowd-free on weekdays.

**Verdict:** **Don't miss this.** It's on-route, free, and unlike anything else on the trip. The canyon drive alone is worth it; the swim is the bonus. Budget 45min in Plužine for a swim stop.

**Sources:**
- https://viaferratapiva.me/blog/lake-piva-montenegro-complete-guide/

### 13. Tara River — Secret Beach Below Đurđevića Bridge
**Status:** `[NEW PIN CANDIDATE]`

**Coordinates (approximate):** 43.1514°N, 19.2669°E — the gravel path turning off before the P4 road's tunnel, south of Đurđevića Bridge. No exact pin exists publicly; navigate using the directions below.

**What it is:** The Tara River runs ~1,200m below Đurđevića Bridge — and there are two access points to swim in it:

**The "Secret Beach" (best option):**
- From the bridge, drive south on Route P4.
- Before the tunnel (look for the tunnel entrance), turn LEFT onto a gravel path. Drive to the end, park.
- Descend the steep marked trail (5-10 min, use hands — it's rocky). Beach is to the left at the bottom; a small waterfall is 5min to the right.
- **Best time:** After 15:00 — rafting groups have gone, beach becomes private. Before 15:00 you'll share with 20-30min interruptions from tour groups.

**Rafting start point:**
- Further along P4. Also accessible after 15:30.
- Less scenic but easier to reach.

**Water temperature:** 7-12°C year-round (glacial, never warm). This is not a "swim for 30 minutes" spot — it's a "gasp, go in, scream, come out" cold plunge that you will never forget. Neoprene booties if you have them; otherwise just waterproof sandals.

**June context:** Rafting season is active so expect tour groups 09:00-15:00. After 15:00 the canyon is yours. The water color (deep teal) under the limestone walls is extraordinary.

**Verdict:** **Worth it as a side quest from Žabljak.** The Đurđevića Bridge visit is already on the itinerary (viewpoint of the deepest canyon in Europe) — combine with the 20min scramble to the river. Cold-water swimmers will love this; everyone else will love the photo and the 5-second plunge.

**Sources:**
- https://theboldpassport.com/places-to-swim-tara-river-secret-spots/
- https://www.zabljak.com/tara-river-canyon-durdevica-tara-bridge

### 14. Mrtvica Canyon — Swimming Pools on the Trail
**Status:** `[IN DATA]` — `me-mrtvica-canyon` in off-route-gems. Good existing entry. This adds swim-specific detail.

**Swimming confirmed:** The trail has a signed "Plaza" (beach) swimming spot early in the canyon (~1km from trailhead), and the "Gate of Wishes" turquoise pool (~4.25km in, ~1.5h walk) is the main swim destination. Water is described as "bone-chilling" — expect 10-13°C even in June (canyon is mostly shaded).

**Current access issue (important):** Bridges destroyed by 2020 flooding were still unbuilt as of June 2025. The circular loop is broken — it's now an out-and-back. This means 3-4h total for the swim payoff at the Gate of Wishes. Confirm bridge status before visiting (check AllTrails recent reviews).

**Route placement:** Mrtvica is on the E65/E80 between Kolašin and Podgorica — directly on route if going Žabljak→Skadar. It's a hike-then-swim combo, not a quick dip. Works if the group wants an active half-day stop between Durmitor and the coast.

**No new pin needed** — existing data captures this. Just add to the swim-holes mental map.

### 15. Skadar Lake — Swimming Spots
**Status:** `[IN DATA]` — multiple Skadar entries. This consolidates swim-specific intelligence.

**Can you swim in Skadar Lake?** Yes. Water quality meets European bathing standards. Summer temperature ~26°C (same as Adriatic) — this is the warmest swim on the entire trip. Warm, calm, clear where you're away from reeds.

**Leeches?** The search results from locals and boat operators don't mention leeches as a concern at the designated swim spots. However: **avoid swimming near thick reed beds or shallow marshy edges** — these are snake/leech habitats. Swim from open beaches or pontoons only.

**Best spots:**
1. **Murići Beach** (southern shore): accessible by road from Bar or Virpazar. Sandy, extended, warm, wild. Multiple reviews call it the best beach on the lake. Coordinates: 42.1308°N, 19.1792°E.
2. **Pješačac Beach** (near Godinje village, ~10min from Virpazar): foot-access only (adds seclusion), rare sandy shoreline, crystal clear water. Best early morning or late afternoon.
3. **Boat tour swim stops**: organized boat tours from Virpazar include mid-lake swimming breaks — guides pick spots away from vegetation. If doing a winery tour, this is built in.

**Verdict:** Skadar swimming is one of the unexpected pleasures of the trip — warm lake water after days of cold rivers. Plan a swim at Murići or from a boat tour while doing the winery/kayak day.

**Sources:**
- https://skadarlakeboatcruise.com/blog/swimming-spots-skadar-lake-montenegro/
- https://www.montenegropulse.com/lake-skadar.html

### 16. Rijeka Crnojevića — River Swim + Kayak
**Status:** `[NEW PIN CANDIDATE]`

**Coordinates:** 42.3333°N, 19.0167°E (the village where the river meets Skadar Lake)

**What it is:** A short (12.3km) crystal-clear river flowing from the old bridge at Rijeka Crnojevića down into Skadar Lake. You can rent kayaks in the village and paddle out toward the lake, stopping to swim en route. The current is calm; the water is clear and warm (lake-adjacent, so warmer than Tara/Neretva).

**The bridge (Danilo's Bridge):** 19th-century arch bridge — iconic photo spot. The river below it is swimmable.

**Access:** On route if doing Skadar → Cetinje → Budva. Rijeka Crnojevića is ~25km from Virpazar, ~25km from Cetinje. Small village. Park by the bridge.

**June conditions:** Warm (lake water), calm, clear. A pleasant contrast to the cold canyon rivers. The horseshoe bend viewpoint above the village (15min walk) is one of Montenegro's most iconic landscape photos.

**Verdict:** Great add-on if spending a day around Skadar Lake. Not worth a separate detour but perfect as part of the Skadar day (winery + Rijeka Crnojevića loop). Budget 1.5h for kayak + swim + viewpoint.

**Sources:**
