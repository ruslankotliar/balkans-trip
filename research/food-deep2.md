# Food Deep Research: Specific Restaurants by Zone

*Compiled Jun 2026. Sourced from TripAdvisor, Reddit, travel forums, restaurant sites. Prioritizes places with recent (2024-2026) traveler reviews.*

## 1. ZADAR (Jun 16 evening)

### First-Night Dinner: Locals' Picks

**Konoba Rafaelo**
- Address: Obala Kneza Trpimira 50, Zadar 23000
- Phone: +385 23 335 349
- Hours: Daily 12:00–00:00
- Located along the marina, slightly outside the packed old-town tourist zone. Family feel, big portions, generous grilled fish and meats. Google 4.7, Restaurant Guru 4.6 (5800+ reviews). Feb 2026 review: "nice local-like place with cozy atmosphere and lovely staff." A safe first-night choice without the tourist-strip premium.
- What to order: grilled fish of the day, seafood risotto, lamb
- Price: €15–25 mains

**Konoba Stomorica**
- Address: Stomorica ul. 5, Zadar 23000
- Tucked in the Varoš neighborhood (old town's less-touristed pocket). Locals recommend it; reviewers note "went here by recommendation by locals." Very atmospheric stone alley setting. Known for grilled seafood and traditional Dalmatian dishes.
- Caveat: some reviewers find portions a bit small for price — order the daily specials.
- Price: €15–25 mains

**Konoba Skoblar**
- Address: Srecko Skoblar, Zadar
- Phone: +385 23 213 236
- TripAdvisor #183 of 310 overall, but recent reviews (Jun 2025) call it "excellent fresh fish" at "fair prices." Praised specifically for: cuttlefish ink risotto, grilled tuna, semifreddo with figs. A hidden-gem tier, less hyped than Rafaelo.
- Price: €15–22 mains ($$ range)

### Fresh Fish Restaurant

**Konoba Rafaelo** (see above) is the strongest all-rounder for fresh fish. For the most focused fish experience, also consider:

**Foša Restaurant**
- Address: Kralja Dmitra Zvonimira 2 (by the old city gate on the sea wall)
- More upscale but known specifically for extraordinary fresh fish and dramatic views of the ancient walls. If budget allows one splurge on the Croatian coast, this is the format.

### Zadar Fish Market (Ribarnica)

- Address: Tržnica Zadar, ulica Pod Bedemom 1/A
- Hours: Mon–Sat 7:00–15:00, Sun 7:00–12:00
- Right in the old town, near the waterfront. Fresh daily catches from local fishermen. Also sells herbs, olive oil, cheese. Go before 10:00 for best selection.
- Note: The market is morning-only. For a Jun 16 evening arrival you'll miss it — but Day 2 morning is a great chance to browse before heading south.

## 2. SPLIT (Jun 17 transit)

### Ćevapi

**Kantun Paulina**
- Address: Matošića ulica 1, Split 21000 (just off the old town pedestrian zone)
- Hours: Mon–Sun 08:00–22:00
- Cash only. Operating since 1967. This is Split-style ćevap: fluffier lepinja bread, ajvar (red pepper relish) instead of kajmak, with raw onion (kapula). Small benches outside, mostly takeaway. TripAdvisor top 10% worldwide 2024. Phil Rosenthal (Somebody Feed Phil) filmed here. Expect a short queue at lunch.
- What to order: ćevapi with ajvar and kapula; add kajmak if you want the creamy version
- Price: €3–5 a portion — genuinely cheap

**Lapinja**
- TripAdvisor top 10% 2024 as well. Smaller, charcoal-grilled to order. Praised for "freshest cevapi" in Split — more of a local spot than Kantun Paulina.

### Quick Lunch Near Diocletian's Palace (Not a Tourist Trap)

**Villa Spiza**
- Address: Ul. Petra Kružića 3, Split 21000 (a few steps off Bosanska Street, inside the palace walls)
- Hours: Mon–Sat 13:00–22:00, closed Sunday
- The single best locals' recommendation for a quick, authentic lunch inside the palace. Tiny — counter for 8 people, 2 tables outside. Menu chalked on a board and changes daily based on the market. Run by Ivana Gamulin (daughter of a legendary Croatian chef). Beloved by locals and food journalists alike. Expect a 10–45 min wait at peak.
- What to order: whatever the daily fish is, seafood risotto, pasta
- Price: €12–18 mains, exceptional value inside the palace

**Mala Kate**
- 10-min walk from the palace. Mostly lunching office workers. Huge portions of Dalmatian classics. Best before noon or after 14:00 to avoid the rush.

### Wine Bar

**MoNiKa's Wine Bar**
- In the old town. Owner is knowledgeable, will guide you to the right Dalmatian bottle. Outstanding selection, described as "probably the best wine offer in the area." Quick drop-in for a glass of Plavac Mali or Pošip.

**Zinfandel Food & Wine Bar**
- Old town, narrow street. ~30 wines by the glass, focused on Croatian varietals including Plavac, Dingač, Pošip. Also does good food (homemade tonnarelli with Prošek wine sauce). Good for a longer stop if you have time.

## 3. OMIŠ / MAKARSKA RIVIERA (Jun 17 evening)

### Near Omiš / Cetina Canyon

**Konoba Gusti**
- Omiš, up an alley from the main street (typically fully booked — reserve ahead)
- Organic ingredients, local produce, "hidden gem." Food described as outstanding freshness and quality. Friendly staff. One of those small places where the chef goes to the market that morning.
- Reserve: almost certainly needed in June

**Konoba Restoran Ćaća**
- Omiš, between the town and the Cetina River. Summer terrace with river breezes. Praised for pasta with shrimp buzara, excellent swordfish steak, seafood grill.
- Price: €15–25 mains, generous portions

**Gusarska Konoba**
- Near the Cetina Canyon area. 127+ positive reviews on evendo. Traditional Croatian with a modern twist, homemade-focus. Name means "pirate konoba" — thematic but the food is the real draw.

### Makarska Riviera Fish / Local Vibe

**Konoba Kalalarga**
- Makarska old town. "Fish arrives freshly caught from the sea, not from bags." Beautiful location in the stone old town at affordable prices. Unpretentious, local-feeling.

**Konoba Decima**
- Address: Šetalište Dr. Franje Tuđmana 13A, Makarska (new location as of May 2024)
- Praised for perfectly cooked bream, charred skin. Described as a "culinary gem" in multiple 2024 reviews.

**Jež**
- Local people's restaurant, just up from the main waterfront promenade. Dalmatian dishes with domestic fresh ingredients. Deliberately avoids the tourist-strip setting.

**Order-ahead tip:** If you have a fixed evening planned, call any of these konobas 24h ahead and order lamb, veal, or octopus peka (cooked under the bell, peka). It's the most authentic Dalmatian cooking and not available spontaneously.

## 4. MALI STON (Jun 18, on route to Dubrovnik)

### Oyster Restaurants — Specific Picks

**Bota Šare Restaurant & Oyster Bar** — THE choice
- Address: Mali Ston b.b, Mali Ston (on the waterfront)
- Phone: +385 20 754-482
- Email: maliston@bota-sare.hr
- TripAdvisor #2 of 26 restaurants in Ston; rated 4.4/5 (2300+ reviews), Restaurant Guru 4.6 (3200+ reviews)
- Family-owned 40+ years; family that actually farms the oysters in Mali Ston Bay. The oysters come directly from their beds: Ostrea edulis, the European flat oyster, considered one of the finest in the Adriatic. Restaurant sits inside a medieval salt storage room — atmospheric.
- Also has a sushi menu (creative fusion on Dalmatian fish, unexpectedly good)
- Price: oysters typically €2.50–3.50/piece for this grade; a dozen is ~€30–40. Full fish meal €25–35/person. Not the cheapest in the bay but best quality + provenance guarantee.
- What to order: a dozen house oysters, mussels in white wine, grilled dentex or sea bass
- Reserve: in June, call ahead or arrive at opening (12:00)

**Other options in Mali Ston:** Kapetanova Kuća is the other well-known name, also with oyster farming heritage. Slightly larger and more tourist-oriented than Bota Šare; similar quality, similar price.

**Practical note:** Mali Ston is 50 min before Dubrovnik on the Pelješac route. An oyster stop here on Jun 18 morning/early afternoon works perfectly before the airport pickup. Don't over-eat — just a dozen oysters and mussels is the move; save appetite for Trebinje dinner.

## 5. TREBINJE (Jun 18 evening)

### Best Dinner on the River

**Konoba / Spa Hotel Studenac**
- Address: Milentija Perovića, Gorica — 3km from Trebinje center, on the Trebišnjica riverbank
- Phone: +387 59 482 627
- TripAdvisor #2 of 36 restaurants in Trebinje, rated 4.4/5
- Surrounded by greenery, spring-fed fishponds, riverside summer garden. Dictionary-definition picturesque. Serves Herzegovinian cuisine: fresh trout from their own fish farm, grilled meats, traditional stews. The setting alone is worth the 3km drive out of town.
- What to order: river trout, lamb, Herzegovinian meat platter
- Price: €12–20 mains, very good value

**Kukurića Vrtovi (Gardens)**
- On the river, large terrace with a pond of live fish. Summer ambiance is exceptional. Slightly more casual than Studenac. Good for a big group dinner on the terrace.

### Wine Bar: Žilavka and Vranac

**Vinska Galerija Vukoje / Restoran Vukoje**
- Vukoje is Trebinje's most famous winery (since 1982), specializing in exactly the two grapes you want: Žilavka (white) and Vranac (red). The wine gallery/bar is in the city center; the full restaurant is on floors 6–7 of the winery building with panoramic terrace views.
- A short tasting at the wine bar is the move: try Žilavka (crisp, minerally, pairs with the Adriatic fish you had at lunch) and Vranac (the signature Herzegovinian red). If you want a guided cellar tour + tasting, book ahead.
- Price: wine glass €3–5, tasting flight €8–15
- Note: the Winery restaurant is for a splurge dinner — the combo dinner + wine at the winery top floor is reportedly excellent but pricier (~€25–35/person with wine).

**Practical evening plan for Trebinje:** arrive Jun 18 evening after airport pickup. Drive 45 min from Dubrovnik. Quick wine stop at Vukoje gallery in town, then river dinner at Studenac. Very manageable.

## 6. MOSTAR (Jun 21)

### Ćevapi: The Debate

**Ćevabdžinica Tima-Irma** — current top pick
- Address: Onešćukova bb, 88000 Mostar (heart of the Old Town, UNESCO zone)
- Phone: +387 66 905 070
- Website: cevabdzinica-tima.com
- Cash only, no reservations, may need to queue for dinner
- Rating: 4.8/5 on Restaurant Guru (5600+ reviews). Family business 30+ years, named after two siblings. Described repeatedly as "best ćevapi in Mostar, best I've ever had." Big portions. The ćevapi here are slightly more beef-forward than Sarajevo style.
- What to order: portion of ćevapi with kajmak, fresh somun bread, raw onion. Also try the mixed grill plate if hungry.
- Price: €4–6/portion, extremely cheap

**Šadrvan**
- Address: Jusovina 11, Stari Grad Mostar
- Rating: 4.6/5 Restaurant Guru (8200+ reviews). Right in the old bazaar near the Crooked Bridge. Serves dolma, sarma (stuffed cabbage/grape leaves), ćevapi, traditional stews. More of a full sit-down restaurant vs Tima-Irma's fast-food format. Staff in traditional dress. Some note it leans touristic but the food is genuinely good and portions are generous.
- Good for: if you want a full traditional Bosnian meal rather than just ćevapi

**Verdict:** Go to Tima-Irma for ćevapi specifically. Go to Šadrvan if the group wants a broader traditional sit-down meal.

### Beyond Ćevapi

**Buregdžinica Ago**
- The best burek stop in Mostar. Try the buredžici: meat pita smothered in garlicky sour cream and yogurt sauce. Eat it for breakfast or a cheap lunch. Under €3.

**Hindin Han**
- Old Town location. Generous portions, low prices, extensive menu of traditional Bosnian dishes. Good for soups, bosanski lonac (highland stew), japrak (stuffed chard leaves). Popular with locals too.

### Good Dinner NOT on the Tourist Strip

**Konoba Taurus**
- Beautiful old stone building on a small street away from the main bridge crush. Ottoman feel. Traditional dishes plus seafood. One of the most atmospheric spots in Mostar without the Stari Most tourist premium.

**Food House Mostar**
- Quiet alley near the Old Bridge but off the main tourist path. Traditional Herzegovinian food at affordable prices. Described as a local favorite, clean, unpretentious.

## 7. SARAJEVO (Jun 22)

### Ćevapi

**Ćevabdžinica Željo (Branch 1: Kundurdziluk 19, Baščaršija)**
- The classic. In Baščaršija district, 200m from the main square. Sarajevo ćevapi are smaller and more numerous per portion than Mostar (10 pieces, looser grind, more lamb). Served in somun with raw onion and kajmak. National Geographic recommended. Tourists and locals both go here; it's an institution, not just hype.
- Second branch: Kundurdziluk 3, also in Baščaršija
- Price: €3–5/portion

**Ćevabdžinica Mrkva (Baščaršija)**
- The "local alternative" to Željo. Hotel staff and longtime residents often prefer it. Quality is comparable; some say the ćevapi at Mrkva are cooked slightly less done (more pink inside). Personal preference — if you're here a full day, try both.
- Verdict: locals are split; Željo is the safe choice, Mrkva is for the curious. Both are on the same street system in Baščaršija.

### Bosnian Coffee Experience

**Ministry of Ćejf**
- Bosnia's first third-wave specialty coffee café. Roasts own beans, serves traditional Bosnian coffee (džezva, fildžan, rahat lokum on the side) alongside flat whites and pourover. Best of both worlds — the ritual of Bosnian coffee with specialty-grade beans.
- Location: Baščaršija area

**Caffe Divan (inside Morića Han)**
- Address: Sarači 77, Baščaršija
- Inside a 500-year-old caravanserai courtyard (1551). The most atmospheric coffee experience in Sarajevo — you're sitting in a medieval Ottoman inn. Coffee is traditional Bosnian style. The courtyard with its fountain is genuinely beautiful.
- Note: the attached restaurant has mixed reviews (some say cevapi are average here) — go for coffee and atmosphere, not a full meal.

### Best Cheap Traditional Restaurant for Group of 4

**Nanina Kuhinja (Grandma's Kitchen)**
- Address: Kundurdziluk 35, Baščaršija, Sarajevo
- Website: naninakuhinja.ba
- Rating: 4.4/5 Restaurant Guru (2500+ reviews)
- The best full Bosnian meal in Sarajevo at fair prices. Menu: bosanski lonac (meat and vegetable stew), klepe (Bosnian dumplings in yogurt), zeljanica (spinach pita), sarma, tufahija (poached apple with walnuts), baklava. Homemade feel, generous portions, gluten-free options noted, large tables suit a group of 4. "Homy vibe, fresh ingredients, cozy ambiance."
- Price: €8–15/person for a full meal. Outstanding value.
- What to order: bosanski lonac, klepe, spinach pie, tufahija for dessert

**Morića Han Restaurant**
- Same address as the cafe above: Sarači 77. More mixed reviews on food quality but unbeatable atmosphere (Ottoman caravanserai courtyard). Use it for coffee + dessert rather than a main meal.

### Bar / Nightlife for a Group Night Out

**Baščaršija streets (Muvekita, Zelenih Beretki)**
- The main evening strip. Atmospheric, cobblestone, mix of locals and travelers. Best for bar-hopping on foot. Most places close ~01:00 but it's lively until then.

**Black Dog Pub**
- Popular with travelers and locals for craft beer and river views. English-speaking, social atmosphere, good for meeting people.

**Ort Caffe (Zelenih Beretki St)**
- Morning coffee spot beloved by Sarajevo students. Doubles as a mellow evening bar. Low tourist density, genuine local crowd, easy to strike up conversation.

**Anonymous Night Club**
- For late-night: traditional and turbo-folk music, each night with different themes/drink promotions. Women sometimes get free cocktails Saturdays. This is the authentic young-Sarajevo experience — not a clubbing export, but real local nightlife.

## 8. ŽABLJAK / DURMITOR (Jun 23–24)

### Near Black Lake

**Nacionalni Restoran Crno Jezero (Black Lake Restaurant)**
- Location: inside Durmitor National Park, right at Black Lake (Crno Jezero), 1400m elevation
- Opened 2018. Sells coffee, snacks, basic local food. The setting — literally at the lake edge looking up at the massif — makes any food taste better. Use it for a post-hike coffee and burek rather than a full dinner (limited menu).

### Best Dinner in Žabljak Village

**Krčma Nostalgija**
- Address: Vuka Karazdika, Žabljak 84220
- Rating: 4.8/5 Restaurant Guru (1200+ reviews) — highest-rated restaurant in town
- Cozy, rustic decor, "typical establishment almost frozen in time." Traditional Montenegrin cuisine: juicy grilled meats, lamb, cicvara (creamy polenta dish), hearty stews. Praised for excellent value and generous portions. The steak Durmitor (local cut, robustly grilled) and mixed grill plate are standout.
- Price: €8–15 mains, very affordable
- Note: some outdoor seating is near the road; ask for indoor or inner terrace seats

**Restaurant Or'O**
- Žabljak center. Described as a "hip place" with good food and drinks. More modern/cafe style than Nostalgija. Good for lunch or a drink.

**What to eat in Durmitor area:**
- Cicvara: rich polenta with kajmak and cheese, the mountain staple
- Kačamak: buckwheat or corn porridge with cream
- Lamb or kid goat: often roasted on open fire
- Prljuša: smoked pork/lamb specialties from the mountains
- Plum brandy (šljivovica): the local spirit, offered everywhere

## 9. BUDVA (Jun 26)

### Best Fish Restaurant

**Rivijera Restaurant**
- Address: Njegoševa 16, Budva Old Town (in the tranquil "Palm Square" alley)
- Hours: Daily 11:00–00:00
- Price: ~€25–30/person
- Hidden in a charming alley inside the old town walls, fisherman-themed decor under vines. Menu: grilled octopus, local goat cheese, beef stew with handmade pasta, seafood risotto. Consistently well-reviewed for quality and atmosphere. One of the Old Town's most respected spots.
- What to order: grilled octopus with olive oil and garlic, grilled fish of the day, risotto

**Fish Grill** (cash-only storefront)
- Central Budva (small storefront, not old town). Tiny, local-operated. You pick your fish from the refrigerated display, they grill it with Dalmatian marinade. Cash only. Described as a "locals' favorite" and "one of the best in Budva" by multiple recent reviewers. Very informal — no ambiance, just perfect fish.
- Price: €15–20 for a full fish meal, very honest value

**Konoba Stari Grad**
- Inside the old town center. Family-run, traditional Montenegrin dishes, local seafood, homemade desserts. Has its own beer. Occasional live guitar. Authentic without being a tourist performance.

### Beach Bar Worth Mentioning

**Casper Bar**
- Budva Old Town, opposite the Archaeological Museum. Jazz bar with a shaded terrace. Craft beer, cocktails. Atmospheric day-to-night spot — coffee in the afternoon, cocktails at sunset. Known for live music in the evenings (small groups).

**Note on Budva:** the tourist strip in July/August is notoriously loud and expensive. These three picks are the antidote — focus on the old town walls and the local side streets.

## 10. KOTOR (Jun 27)

### Morning Coffee Before the Fortress Climb

**Restobar Taraca**
- Address: Tabačina 566, Kotor (just outside the old town walls, along the river Škurda)
- Hours: Mon–Sun 08:00–23:00
- Google 4.6 (729 reviews), TripAdvisor #4 of 114 restaurants in Kotor
- Opens at 8:00 — perfectly timed for a pre-climb coffee. On the river with views of the Kotor walls. Serves cold brew, oat milk lattes, proper espresso alongside a full breakfast menu. Also good post-hike brunch. Light, modern, European-Asian menu; notable vegan/vegetarian options. The most consistently praised café-restaurant in Kotor.
- What to order: morning cold brew or flat white, eggs, fresh juice before the climb

**Senso Café**
- Also in old town. Known specifically for "crispy flaky croissants" — perfect pre-hike fuel. Quick in-and-out option.

### Good Lunch in Old Town

**Konoba Scala Santa**
- Inside the old town in an "adorable little square." Fresh grilled squid, traditional Dalmatian stew, gnocchi with truffles and shrimp, saffron and prawn linguine. Described as best value for money in Kotor without compromising quality. Violinist in summer evenings. Lovely outdoor setting.
- Price: €15–25 mains, fair for Kotor

**Bastion 1**
- Near the old town walls. "Huge plates of seafood without tourist prices" — keeps locals coming back. Best pure value-to-quality ratio for a group lunch.

**Hotel Marija 4-course lunch**
- A 4-course set lunch for €10/person. If the group is hungry after the climb and wants maximum value, this is absurdly cheap for the quality. Seated in a cobbled courtyard.

**Restoran Atrium**
- Hidden gem: small alley, greenhouse-like skylight, velvet couches, candlelight and greenery. For a special final lunch before the drive to Dubrovnik. Standout dishes: mussels in blue cheese sauce, octopus with ink sauce. More of a dinner restaurant but does lunch.

## Budget Anchor Points

- Bosnia and Montenegro are ~40% cheaper than Croatia for equivalent food quality
- Ćevapi portion: €3–5 anywhere in Bosnia/Montenegro
- Full traditional Bosnian meal: €8–15/person
- Fresh grilled fish in Croatia: €18–30/person (depends on fish weight)
- Oysters at Mali Ston: ~€2.50–3.50/piece; a dozen for 4 people to share = €30–40
- A good konoba dinner in Dalmatia: €20–30/person with wine
