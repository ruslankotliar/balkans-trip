## Part 1: Skadar Lake accommodation — Day 10 (Thu Jun 25), Virpazar area

### Clarification on naming: "Guesthouse Skadar Lake" = "Raicevic"

These are the **same property.** The full Booking.com listing name is **"Guesthouse Skadar Lake - Raicevic."** It is located in **Godinje village**, a stone-walled winery village on the west shore of the lake, ~3-4 km from Virpazar proper. The family is Danica and Pavle Raicevic. The name on Facebook is "Guesthouse Skadar Lake, Godinje village, Montenegro" (@godinjebnb).

### Option A — Guesthouse Skadar Lake (Raicevic), Godinje village

**Booking link:** https://www.booking.com/hotel/me/godinje-apartment.html  
**Hostelworld:** https://www.hostelworld.com/bed-and-breakfasts/p/90413/guesthouse-skadar-lake/  
**Trip.com:** https://www.trip.com/hotels/virpazar-hotel-detail-9492153/guesthouse-skadar-lake/  
**Facebook / direct contact:** https://www.facebook.com/godinjebnb  

**Rating:** 9.3 on Booking.com (134 reviews)  
**Price range:** approximately €40-50/night for 2 rooms (private rooms with double or twin beds, shared bathroom)  
**IMPORTANT — CASH ONLY.** No card reader on-site.

**What you get:** Two private rooms with shared living room and shared bathroom. Danica and Pavle don't speak much English but are genuinely warm — reviewers describe it as "grandma's house." Dinners: fish soup, marinated lake carp, vegetables, glass of home wine — €10/person. Breakfast €5/person. Grandfather's homemade wine and rakia on arrival.

**Location:** Godinje is 3-4km uphill from Virpazar, on a winding stone road through an old vineyard village. The views over the lake from the terrace are the genuine article — this is NOT a generic town apartment.

**Is this right for 4 young adults?** Yes, conditionally. This is a Tier 1/2 hybrid: incredibly characterful (stone village, real Montenegrin family, lake view, home-cooked food), budget-friendly, and the location is perfect for a sunrise morning. Limitation: only 2 private rooms with a shared bathroom — might feel cramped for 4 adults. Confirm at booking whether a third room or the whole property is available. **Best scenario:** book both rooms + ask if a third is available. If they can't accommodate 4 comfortably, Camp Radoman (below) is the cleaner alternative.

**Availability caveat:** Not verified as open Jun 25, 2026. Check Booking.com directly — the listing shows on the platform. Book well ahead (June = high season starting).

### Option B — Camp Radoman, Virpazar

**Booking link:** https://www.booking.com/hotel/me/auto-camp-radoman.html  
**Planet of Hotels (reviews):** https://en.planetofhotels.com/montenegro/virpazar/auto-camp-radoman-1241  

**Rating:** 9.2 on Booking.com  
**Price:** ~€25-30/night for 4 people + tent + car (estimated; confirm on Booking.com)  
**Showers:** YES — "showers and kitchen facilities were clean and regularly maintained"  
**Car-accessible:** YES — it is a standard auto camp (car + tent format)

**What you get:** Small family-run camp overlooking mountains and Skadar Lake. Hosts greet you with a bottle of homemade wine. Pitches are well-spaced. Sun terrace, garden, free WiFi, outdoor fireplace, shared kitchen. "Wonderful little camp run by a local family."

**Location:** On the lake-approach road near Virpazar — closer to Virpazar's dock than Godinje is, convenient for morning kayak.

**Verdict:** The most practical camp option. Great family vibe, documented showers, lake views, easy car access, walking distance to the Virpazar kayak dock. Camp Radoman > Camp Podkraj for atmosphere.

### Option C — Camp Podkraj, Virpazar

**Booking link:** https://www.booking.com/hotel/me/camp-podkraj-virpazar.html  
**Planet of Hotels:** https://en.planetofhotels.com/montenegro/virpazar/camp-podkraj  

**Rating:** 9.7 on Booking.com (the highest-rated property in Virpazar)  
**Price:** ~€12-15/night per pitch (tent + car); ~€48-60 for 4 people total when you factor food/extras  
**Showers:** YES — private bathroom in the tents; described as very clean  
**Car-accessible:** YES, 771m from Virpazar centre  
**Extras:** Outdoor pool, children's playground, table tennis, coffee shop and bar, free WiFi, free parking. Dinner €9 (with wine or beer). Breakfast €7.

**What you get:** More resort-like than Radoman. The 9.7 reflects genuinely exceptional hospitality: "very hospitable and welcoming owners, delicious food." The pool is a bonus on a hot June afternoon.

**Verdict:** Highest-rated option, nicest facilities, but feels slightly more polished/less raw than Radoman. Pick Podkraj if you want a pool and maximum comfort; pick Radoman if you want the "local family camp" atmosphere at a slightly lower price. Both are good. Avoid choosing based on the star rating alone — both are excellent.

### Wild camping at Skadar Lake — honest verdict

**Officially not permitted.** Skadar Lake is a protected national park (wetland). Rangers patrol, especially near Virpazar. Several sources confirm: "Camping is only permitted at designated campsites or on private land with permission."

**Practical reality:** The lake's south and east shore (Murići area) is remote enough that some travelers ignore this, but the risk of a fine is real and the Virpazar area specifically is patrolled. Wild camping works well a day's drive away (Durmitor, Bosnia) where rules are laxer — at Skadar, just use a campsite. With Camp Radoman and Podkraj both under €15/pitch/night, there's no meaningful saving in going wild here.

**If you want a remote lake view:** Murići beach (south shore, ~40min from Virpazar) has some flat spots but is patrolled occasionally. Not recommended here. The campsite options above are genuinely beautiful.

### Day 10 accommodation recommendation

**Tier 2 (budget comfortable, best for 4 adults):** Camp Radoman (9.2) or Camp Podkraj (9.7)

### Day 10: Žabljak → Đurđevića bridge → Tara beach → Ostrog → Skadar Lake

| Leg | OSRM time | km | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Žabljak → Đurđevića Tara bridge | **0:31** | 23 | Route P5→P4, paved, curves — allow 0:40 |
| At bridge (view + zipline optional) | **0:30** | — | View only: 20-30min. Zipline €20-25, adds ~45min |
| Bridge → Tara secret beach | **~0:10** | ~3 | Drive down P4, tunnel junction, gravel path. Park + descend steep trail ~10min |
| At Tara beach (cold plunge) | **1:00** | — | Water ~10-12°C in late June. Very steep descent — shoes required |
| Tara beach → Ostrog Monastery | **~1:45** | ~95 | Reverse back toward Žabljak / Mojkovac direction is WRONG. Correct route: south via Šavnik → Nikšić → Ostrog (~95km). Allow 1:45-2:00 with mountain speeds. NOTE: `drive-times.md` shows Žabljak→Ostrog = 2:11 / 95km — bridge+beach add ~40km but subtract the Žabljak town time. Net: bridge area → Ostrog ≈ 1:45-2:00. |
| At Ostrog Monastery | **1:30-2:00** | — | Free. Drive to upper car park (avoid lower car park + long stair walk). Cover knees + shoulders. Crowds build after 10:00. |
| Ostrog → Virpazar (Skadar Lake) | **~1:30** | ~72 | Ostrog→Podgorica ~1:03/43km, Podgorica→Virpazar ~0:33/29km. Total ~1:36. Call it 1:30-1:45. |
| **Day 10 drive total** | **~4:30-5:00** | ~195 | Excluding time at stops |
| **Day 10 with stops** | **~9:30-10:00h** | — | From Žabljak 07:30 → Virpazar arrival ~17:30-18:00 |

**Feasibility verdict: TIGHT but doable — one thing must go.**

Working backward from a ~18:00 arrival at Skadar Lake:
- Depart Žabljak **07:30**
- Bridge: 08:10–08:40 (30min)
- Tara beach: 08:50–09:50 (1h cold plunge; access ~20min total walk/descent)
- Drive to Ostrog: 09:50–11:35 (~1:45 drive)
- Ostrog: 11:35–13:15 (1:40 at monastery)
- Drive Ostrog → Virpazar: 13:15–14:55 (~1:40)
- Arrival Skadar Lake: ~15:00 — enough time to check in, swim, and watch sunset over the lake. **This works.**

**What to cut if needed:** The Tara beach cold plunge is the natural skip. It's a steep scramble down to cold water — beautiful if everyone is keen, but a 30min stop + 20min hiking in the heat of the morning is easily dropped. The bridge itself (view + zipline) is the main draw and takes only 30min.

**Second option:** Skip the Tara bridge entirely and use that 1.5h for more time at Ostrog (less rushed) or earlier arrival at Skadar for a first swim.

**Note on Tara beach access:** The Bold Passport confirms: "as of summer 2025, the road eventually becomes blocked off due to construction, making it only a pleasant detour for now" — verify this hasn't changed. The access point is the tunnel junction off P4. Rafting tour crowds peak before 15:00 — if arriving mid-morning you'll share the beach with groups.

### Day 11: Skadar Lake → Stari Bar → Sveti Stefan → Budva

| Leg | OSRM time | km | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|

| Stari Bar → Sveti Stefan | **0:40-0:50** | ~35 | Route via Sozina tunnel or Bar coastal road. Use drive-times: Virpazar→Sveti Stefan = 0:47/35km. From Bar (already 24km south of Virpazar) = ~0:25-30. |
| At Sveti Stefan beach | **1:00** | — | Public beach on the causeway side (Sveti Stefan islet = Aman hotel, private). Swim + photo stop. Very crowded by noon — arrive before 12:00 if possible. |
| Sveti Stefan → Budva | **0:15-0:20** | 10 | `drive-times.md`: 0:18 / 10km. |
| **Day 11 drive total** | **~1:30** | ~70 | Very low driving day |
| **Day 11 with stops** | **~7:30-8:00h** | — | From Virpazar 08:00 → Budva arrival ~16:00-17:00 |

**Feasibility verdict: RELAXED — this is a good-pace day.**

Suggested flow:
- 08:00 kayak departs from Virpazar (~€20-25 for 4 in kayaks, self-guided 1h) or 2h guided tour (~€22pp)
- 10:00/11:00 done, breakfast/coffee in Virpazar
- 11:00-11:30 drive to Stari Bar
- 11:30-13:00 Stari Bar + olive tree
- 13:00-13:30 drive to Sveti Stefan
- 13:30-14:30 beach swim at Sveti Stefan
- 14:45 arrive Budva — afternoon/evening free for beach, town, dinner

**What to cut if Day 11 is over-packed:** Sveti Stefan beach can be shortened to a 30min photo stop + quick swim if you need to check into accommodation early. Alternatively, skip the Stari Bar olive tree (it's quick and cheap but not critical) and just do the ruins. Nothing on Day 11 is genuinely tight — it's the easier of the two days.

## Summary verdict

| | Feasible? | Tight? | Cut if needed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day 10 | Yes | Tight (~9:30h from start to arrival) | Tara beach cold plunge (saves ~1:20) |
| Day 11 | Yes | Relaxed (~7:30h) | Nothing critical; shorten Sveti Stefan if needed |

**Biggest single risk on Day 10:** Ostrog Monastery timing. Arrive before 12:00 to beat the tour buses. If you leave Žabljak at 07:30 as shown, you arrive at Ostrog around 11:35 — good. If you sleep in or spend 45+ min at the bridge, Ostrog becomes crowded and rushed.

**Day 11 winery note:** `me-notes.md` recommends Vinarija Kopitović (Gornji Brčeli, ~10min from Virpazar) as the best Skadar Lake tasting — reviewers often stay 3+ hours. Don't attempt this AND a 2h kayak AND Stari Bar in the same morning. If the group wants wine, replace the kayak with a shorter boat tour and do Kopitović instead.
